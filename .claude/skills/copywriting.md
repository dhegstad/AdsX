# Copywriting Skill

## Purpose
Create compelling, conversion-focused copy for AdsX - an agency specializing in advertising within AI search results (Agentic Search).

## Brand Voice

### Tone
- **Confident**: We're pioneers in a new advertising frontier
- **Direct**: No fluff, get to the point
- **Innovative**: Forward-thinking, cutting-edge
- **Professional**: B2B focused, executive-level audience
- **Approachable**: Expert but not condescending

### Language Style
- Active voice, strong verbs
- Short sentences, punchy paragraphs
- Avoid jargon unless industry-specific and necessary
- Use "you" to speak directly to the reader
- Present tense for immediacy

## Messaging Framework

### Core Value Proposition
"The agency for running ads in AI search results"

### Key Messages
1. **First-mover advantage**: AI search is the new frontier - be there before competitors
2. **Expertise**: We specialize exclusively in agentic search advertising
3. **Results-driven**: Focus on measurable outcomes and ROI
4. **Future-proof**: Position your brand where search is heading

### Target Audience
- CMOs and Marketing Directors at growth-stage companies
- E-commerce and SaaS businesses
- Brands looking to diversify beyond Google/Meta
- Forward-thinking companies embracing AI

### Pain Points to Address
- "Traditional search ads are getting more expensive and less effective"
- "We're missing the AI search audience entirely"
- "We don't know how to advertise in ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Claude"
- "Our competitors might get there first"

### Differentiators
- First agency focused exclusively on AI search ads
- Deep expertise in agentic search platforms
- Proprietary strategies and methodologies
- Proven results in an emerging channel

## Page-Specific Guidelines

### Homepage
- Hero: Bold claim + immediate value prop
- Social proof early (logos, stats)
- Clear path to services and contact
- Create urgency around first-mover advantage

### Services Page
- Lead with outcomes, not features
- Use clear service tiers or categories
- Include what's involved in each service
- End with strong CTA

### Case Studies
- Results-first headlines (numbers, percentages)
- Challenge → Solution → Results format
- Specific metrics and timeframes
- Client quotes when available

### About Page
- Mission-driven narrative
- Team expertise and background
- Why AI search advertising matters
- Company values (brief)

### Pricing Page
- Transparent pricing builds trust
- Emphasize value at each tier
- Address common objections
- Clear "what's included" lists

### Contact Page
- Multiple contact options
- Set expectations for response time
- Include a qualifier question if needed
- Reduce friction in forms

### Blog
- Educational content about AI search landscape
- Industry news and analysis
- Tactical how-to content
- Thought leadership pieces

## Copy Formulas

### Headlines
- **Problem-Solution**: "Stop Guessing. Start Showing Up in AI Search."
- **Benefit-Focused**: "Reach Customers Where They're Actually Searching"
- **Curiosity**: "What Happens When 40% of Searches Skip Google?"
- **Command**: "Claim Your Spot in AI Search Results"

### CTAs
- Primary: "Get Started", "Book a Strategy Call", "See Our Work"
- Secondary: "Learn More", "View Case Studies", "See Pricing"
- Avoid generic: "Submit", "Click Here", "Contact Us"

### Proof Points
- Specific numbers > vague claims
- "47% increase in qualified leads" > "significant improvement"
- Client logos and testimonials
- Industry recognition

## Writing Checklist
- [ ] Does it speak to the target audience's pain points?
- [ ] Is the value proposition clear within 5 seconds?
- [ ] Are there specific proof points?
- [ ] Is every sentence earning its place?
- [ ] Does it end with a clear next step?
- [ ] Would an executive read this? (respect their time)
