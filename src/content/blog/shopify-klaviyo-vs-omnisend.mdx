If you run a Shopify store and you're choosing an email marketing platform, the decision almost always comes down to Klaviyo or Omnisend. Both are built for e-commerce, both integrate natively with [Shopify](https://shopify.pxf.io/c/6318547/3797171/13624), and both can power the core revenue-driving automations your store needs: abandoned cart, welcome series, post-purchase flow, and win-back.

The difference is in depth, complexity, and price. This guide breaks down exactly where each platform excels and who should use which.

## Pricing: The Biggest Differentiator

Pricing is where Klaviyo and Omnisend diverge most dramatically.

**Klaviyo pricing (email + SMS):**
- Free: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month
- 1,001-1,500 contacts: $45/month
- 5,001-6,000 contacts: $100/month
- 10,001-11,000 contacts: $175/month
- 25,001-27,000 contacts: $400/month

**Omnisend pricing:**
- Free: Up to 250 contacts, 500 emails/month
- Standard (500 contacts): $16/month
- Standard (5,000 contacts): $65/month
- Standard (10,000 contacts): $115/month
- Standard (25,000 contacts): $210/month
- Pro (adds more SMS credits): $59/month base

At 10,000 contacts, Klaviyo costs $175/month vs. Omnisend's $115/month — a $720/year difference. At 25,000 contacts, Klaviyo charges $400/month vs. Omnisend's $210/month — a $2,280/year difference.

That cost gap needs to be justified by either superior revenue generation or capabilities you actually use.

## Automation Flows: How They Compare

Both platforms offer pre-built automation templates for the core e-commerce flows. The execution quality is comparable for standard flows.

**Klaviyo automations:**
- Abandoned Cart: Industry-leading customization, multi-branch logic based on cart value, product categories, or customer history
- Browse Abandonment: Trigger on specific product views with product-personalized content
- Welcome Series: Multi-email with conditional splits based on signup source or first purchase behavior
- Post-Purchase: Dynamic flows that differ for first-time vs. repeat buyers
- Win-Back: Predictive sending based on expected purchase date

Klaviyo's edge is in conditional logic. A Klaviyo flow can branch 15 different ways based on customer data, sending genuinely different messages to different customer segments within the same automation. This requires more setup time but delivers more relevant messaging.

**Omnisend automations:**
- Abandoned Cart: Three-email sequences with dynamic product blocks, simple and effective
- Product Abandonment: Similar to Klaviyo's browse abandonment
- Welcome Series: Standard multi-touch sequence with easy-to-customize templates
- Post-Purchase: Clean flow with SMS and push notification integration
- Win-Back: Pre-built 3-email sequence

Omnisend's flows are easier to configure and cover the core use cases well. Where it falls short is in the branching complexity and data-driven personalization that Klaviyo enables. For most merchants, the Omnisend flow quality is sufficient to capture the majority of automation revenue.

**Revenue benchmark comparison (industry data):**
- Average Klaviyo-powered stores generate 25-35% of revenue from email
- Average Omnisend-powered stores generate 20-30% of revenue from email

The gap exists but is narrower than the price difference suggests — largely because the core flows perform similarly on both platforms.

## Segmentation: Where Klaviyo Leads

Shopify segmentation is where Klaviyo has a genuine technical advantage. The platform offers:

**Predictive analytics segments:**
- Predicted customer lifetime value (segment high-LTV customers for VIP treatment)
- Predicted next purchase date (send win-back emails before customers actually churn)
- Churn risk score (identify at-risk customers before they disappear)

**Advanced behavioral segments:**
- Customers who viewed a specific product category in the last 30 days
- Customers who have purchased 3+ times from a specific collection
- Customers whose average order value has declined over their purchase history

**RFM segmentation:**
- Recency, Frequency, Monetary analysis built into the platform
- Champions, Loyal Customers, At-Risk segments created automatically

Omnisend's segmentation covers standard use cases — purchase behavior, engagement, campaign interactions — but lacks Klaviyo's predictive analytics and the depth of custom behavioral segments. For a data-driven marketer who runs targeted campaigns to 20 different segments, this matters. For a merchant running 3-4 standard segments, Omnisend is sufficient.

## SMS: Different Approaches

**Klaviyo SMS:**
- Tightly integrated with email flows — you can branch between email and SMS within the same automation
- Unified subscriber profiles show SMS and email engagement together
- SMS credits purchased separately from email plan pricing
- Strong compliance tools (opt-in management, quiet hours, unsubscribe handling)

**Omnisend SMS:**
- SMS included in all paid plans with set monthly credit allowances
- Simple setup with pre-built SMS automation templates
- Multi-channel automation builder where email, SMS, and push are siblings in the same flow
- More affordable entry point for SMS

For a merchant just adding SMS to their marketing, Omnisend's included credits and simpler setup make it easier to get started. For a merchant running sophisticated cross-channel orchestration — where a customer who opens an email gets different follow-up than one who does not — Klaviyo's unified profile and conditional logic provide more control.

## Analytics and Reporting

**Klaviyo analytics:**
- Revenue attribution per flow and campaign (last-touch or multi-touch)
- Email deliverability dashboard with domain-level metrics
- A/B test analysis with statistical significance indicators
- Cohort analysis for subscriber acquisition
- Benchmark data comparing your metrics to industry averages

**Omnisend analytics:**
- Sales dashboard showing revenue attributed to campaigns and automations
- Email performance metrics (open rate, click rate, unsubscribe rate)
- Revenue per email and revenue per subscriber metrics
- Basic A/B testing with open and click rate comparison

Klaviyo's analytics are materially more sophisticated. If you use data to make marketing decisions — adjusting flows based on revenue attribution, benchmarking your deliverability against industry standards, identifying cohorts of high-value subscribers — Klaviyo's reporting delivers more actionable insight.

## Who Should Choose Klaviyo

- Stores doing $500K+ in annual revenue where email ROI justifies the platform cost
- Marketers who will actively build and iterate complex flows with conditional logic
- Businesses with large lists (10,000+ subscribers) where segmentation precision generates meaningful revenue differences
- Teams with a dedicated email specialist who can leverage the platform depth
- Merchants who want predictive analytics to inform retention strategy

## Who Should Choose Omnisend

- New and growing stores where email marketing is being built from scratch
- Merchants with lists under 10,000 who want solid core automations without complexity
- Stores where email is important but not the primary revenue driver
- Teams without a dedicated email specialist who need a simpler tool
- Merchants who want SMS and web push included without add-on pricing

## The Migration Question

If you are on Omnisend and wondering whether to switch to Klaviyo, the answer depends on whether you are leaving revenue on the table.

Run this test: Look at your current email revenue attribution. If automations are generating less than 20% of your total email revenue (with campaigns generating 80%+), you may not be fully utilizing even Omnisend's automation capabilities. Before upgrading platforms, optimize the flows you have.

If you are at a point where you need predictive segmentation, advanced conditional flows, or deeper analytics — and your list is large enough that the Klaviyo cost is a small percentage of email-attributed revenue — then the upgrade makes sense.

The platform is less important than the strategy. A well-built Omnisend setup outperforms a neglected Klaviyo account every time.
