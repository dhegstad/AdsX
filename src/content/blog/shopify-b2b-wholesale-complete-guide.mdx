Selling wholesale and B2B on [Shopify](https://shopify.pxf.io/c/6318547/3797171/13624) is no longer a workaround requiring clunky apps and separate storefronts. Shopify has invested heavily in native B2B capabilities, and in 2026, merchants can run sophisticated wholesale operations alongside their direct-to-consumer business from a single admin panel. The opportunity is substantial: B2B e-commerce transactions are projected to exceed $20 trillion globally, and businesses that buy online expect the same streamlined experience they get as consumers.

Whether you are a DTC brand looking to add wholesale as a revenue channel, an established wholesaler moving operations online, or a manufacturer wanting to sell directly to retailers, this guide covers everything you need to build, launch, and scale your B2B operations on Shopify.

## Why B2B and Wholesale on Shopify Makes Sense in 2026

The traditional wholesale model involved phone calls, faxed order forms, handshake deals, and manual invoicing. That model is dying. Modern B2B buyers are millennials and Gen Z professionals who grew up shopping online. They expect self-service portals, real-time inventory visibility, transparent pricing, and frictionless reordering.

Shopify recognized this shift and built B2B capabilities directly into the platform. The advantages of running wholesale on Shopify rather than a standalone B2B platform include:

**Unified inventory management.** One inventory pool serves both your wholesale and retail channels. When a wholesale customer orders 500 units, your retail inventory updates instantly. No more overselling or maintaining separate stock counts across platforms.

**Single admin panel.** Manage wholesale orders, retail orders, returns, and fulfillment from one dashboard. Your team does not need to learn a separate system or switch between platforms throughout the day.

**Shared infrastructure.** Your theme, product catalog, media assets, and integrations work across both channels. Updates to product descriptions, images, or specifications propagate to both B2B and DTC simultaneously.

**Lower total cost.** Running two separate platforms for B2B and DTC typically costs $500-$2,000/month more than a unified Shopify solution when you factor in platform fees, app subscriptions, and staff time managing dual systems.

**Built-in scalability.** Shopify's infrastructure handles traffic spikes, large order volumes, and international expansion without requiring you to upgrade servers or manage hosting.

The B2B e-commerce market is growing at roughly 18% annually, and businesses that establish online wholesale operations now position themselves to capture a disproportionate share of this growth.

## Understanding Shopify's Native B2B Features

Shopify has progressively expanded its B2B feature set. Here is what is available natively and what you should understand before building your wholesale channel.

### Company Accounts

Company accounts are the foundation of Shopify's B2B system. Unlike individual customer accounts used for DTC, company accounts represent business entities and support multiple buyers, locations, and configurations under a single company profile.

Each company account includes:

- **Company name and profile** with business details, tax ID, and contact information
- **Multiple locations** for businesses that ship to warehouses, stores, or distribution centers
- **Multiple buyers** under the same company, each with their own login credentials
- **Location-specific settings** including shipping addresses, tax exemptions, and payment terms
- **Order history** consolidated at the company level for easy reference

Setting up company accounts requires Shopify Plus or Shopify Advanced (with B2B features enabled). Navigate to Customers in your admin, then select Companies to create new accounts or import existing wholesale customers.

### Custom Price Lists

Price lists allow you to create wholesale pricing structures separate from your retail prices. You can create multiple price lists and assign them to specific companies or groups of companies.

**Fixed pricing** sets specific wholesale prices for each product variant. For example, a product retailing at $50 might have a wholesale price of $25 in your standard wholesale price list and $22 in your VIP wholesale price list.

**Percentage-based discounts** apply a flat percentage reduction across all products or specific collections. A 40% wholesale discount automatically calculates wholesale prices from your retail prices and adjusts when retail prices change.

**Volume-based pricing** (quantity price breaks) offers better pricing as order quantities increase. A product might be $25 each for 1-49 units, $22 each for 50-99 units, and $19 each for 100+ units. This encourages larger orders and rewards high-volume buyers.

You can combine these approaches within the same price list, using percentage discounts as a baseline and fixed pricing for specific high-margin or promotional products.

### B2B Catalogs

Catalogs control which products each wholesale customer can see and order. Not every wholesale customer needs access to your full product range. A clothing brand might offer different catalogs to boutique retailers versus department stores, or restrict access to new collections until official launch dates.

Creating catalogs involves:

1. Defining which products or collections are included
2. Assigning a price list to the catalog
3. Linking the catalog to specific company accounts
4. Setting catalog visibility rules and access dates

This is powerful for manufacturers selling to different retail verticals. A food manufacturer can show restaurant-format products to food service distributors while showing consumer-format products to grocery retailers, all from the same Shopify store.

### Payment Terms

B2B transactions rarely involve immediate payment. Wholesale buyers expect net payment terms that allow them to receive goods, sell them, and then pay the supplier.

Shopify supports configurable payment terms including:

- **Due on fulfillment** for cash-on-delivery arrangements
- **Net 7, Net 15, Net 30, Net 60, Net 90** for standard invoice terms
- **Custom terms** for negotiated arrangements with specific accounts

When a wholesale customer places an order with net terms, Shopify creates a draft order that converts to an invoice. The invoice tracks payment status and due dates. You can send payment reminders and manage collections directly from the admin.

For merchants extending net terms, credit risk is a real consideration. Start new accounts with shorter terms (Net 15 or Due on Fulfillment) and extend longer terms as the relationship matures and payment reliability is established.

### B2B Checkout Customization

The wholesale checkout experience differs from retail checkout. B2B buyers need:

- **Purchase order numbers** to reference on their internal systems
- **Company billing** rather than personal credit card payment
- **Shipping to multiple locations** within a single order
- **Order notes** for special handling instructions
- **Draft orders** that require internal approval before submission

Shopify's B2B checkout handles these requirements natively on Plus plans. The checkout automatically adapts based on whether the logged-in user is a B2B company buyer or a retail customer.

## Setting Up Your Shopify B2B Channel: Step by Step

### Step 1: Choose Your Shopify Plan

Your plan determines which B2B features are available natively.

| Feature | Basic ($39/mo) | Shopify ($105/mo) | Advanced ($399/mo) | Plus ($2,300/mo) |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Company Accounts | No | No | Limited | Full |
| Custom Price Lists | No | No | Limited | Full |
| B2B Catalogs | No | No | Limited | Full |
| Payment Terms | No | No | Limited | Full |
| Quantity Price Breaks | No | No | Yes | Yes |
| B2B Checkout | No | No | Partial | Full |
| Multiple Catalogs per Company | No | No | No | Yes |

For serious wholesale operations, Shopify Plus provides the most complete feature set. However, merchants on lower plans can achieve B2B functionality through third-party apps, which we cover in the next section.

### Step 2: Configure Company Accounts

Start by creating company accounts for your existing wholesale customers. For each company:

1. Navigate to Customers then Companies in your admin
2. Click "Add company"
3. Enter company name, tax ID, and primary contact
4. Add locations (shipping addresses) for the company
5. Set payment terms for the account
6. Assign a catalog and price list
7. Invite buyers to create their login credentials

For bulk setup, Shopify supports CSV import of company accounts. Prepare a spreadsheet with company details, contacts, and location information, then import through the Companies section.

### Step 3: Create Wholesale Price Lists

Build your pricing structure before inviting wholesale customers to your store.

**Determine your wholesale discount structure.** Industry standard wholesale discounts vary by category:

- **Apparel and fashion:** 50-55% off retail (keystone markup)
- **Beauty and cosmetics:** 40-50% off retail
- **Food and beverage:** 35-45% off retail
- **Electronics and accessories:** 30-40% off retail
- **Home goods and decor:** 45-55% off retail

Ensure your wholesale prices maintain healthy margins after accounting for cost of goods, shipping, and overhead. A common mistake is setting wholesale prices too aggressively and eroding profitability.

**Create tiered price lists.** Most successful wholesale operations use 2-4 pricing tiers:

- **Standard Wholesale (Tier 1):** 40% off retail, for new accounts and smaller retailers
- **Preferred Wholesale (Tier 2):** 45% off retail, for accounts ordering $5,000+/month
- **VIP Wholesale (Tier 3):** 50% off retail, for major accounts ordering $20,000+/month

**Set minimum order quantities.** Protect your margins and operational efficiency with minimum order requirements. Common approaches:

- Minimum order value ($250-$500 for first orders, $150-$300 for reorders)
- Minimum quantity per SKU (6, 12, or 24 units depending on product type)
- Minimum case pack quantities for products shipped in cases

### Step 4: Build B2B Catalogs

Create catalogs that match your wholesale customer segments. Common catalog structures include:

**Full catalog:** All wholesale-eligible products, assigned to established accounts with proven order history.

**New account catalog:** A curated selection of bestsellers and hero products, designed to onboard new wholesale customers without overwhelming them.

**Seasonal catalogs:** Time-limited access to new collections, pre-order items, or seasonal products.

**Category-specific catalogs:** Industry-tailored selections (e.g., a "Restaurant Supply" catalog showing bulk food products versus a "Retail" catalog showing consumer-packaged products).

### Step 5: Configure Tax Settings for B2B

Wholesale transactions often involve tax-exempt customers. Configure your tax settings to handle:

**Resale exemptions.** Retailers purchasing your products for resale are typically exempt from sales tax. Collect resale certificates and mark accounts as tax-exempt in Shopify.

**International VAT.** B2B transactions between VAT-registered businesses in different countries may qualify for reverse charge mechanisms. Configure VAT settings and collect VAT registration numbers.

**Tax documentation.** Maintain organized records of all tax exemption certificates. Use Shopify's built-in tax settings or integrate with Avalara or TaxJar for automated compliance.

### Step 6: Set Up Wholesale Shipping

Wholesale shipping differs significantly from retail shipping:

- **Palletized shipping** for large orders requires freight carrier integration
- **LTL (less than truckload) shipping** rates for medium orders
- **Volume-based shipping discounts** that reflect actual wholesale shipping costs
- **FOB pricing** where the buyer pays shipping from your warehouse

Configure shipping profiles in Shopify that apply specifically to B2B orders. You can set flat rates, weight-based rates, or calculated carrier rates for wholesale customers.

## B2B Checkout Customization

The wholesale checkout experience needs to feel professional and accommodate B2B-specific workflows.

### Purchase Order Support

B2B buyers typically need to reference a purchase order (PO) number with every order. This PO number connects your invoice to their internal procurement system.

On Shopify Plus, you can add a PO field to the checkout using checkout extensibility. On lower plans, add a PO field to the cart page using theme customization or a cart notes app.

Ensure PO numbers appear on:
- Order confirmation emails
- Packing slips
- Invoices
- Shipping notifications

### Draft Orders and Approval Workflows

Large wholesale orders often require internal approval before submission. Shopify's draft order system supports this workflow:

1. The buyer creates a draft order (or a sales rep creates one on their behalf)
2. The draft order is reviewed by a manager or purchasing department
3. Once approved, the draft converts to a confirmed order
4. Payment is processed according to the account's payment terms

For automated approval workflows, consider apps like Mechanic or Shopify Flow (available on Plus) to route orders above certain thresholds for manual review.

### Multi-Location Shipping

B2B customers frequently ship to multiple locations: different store locations, regional warehouses, or distribution centers. Shopify's company account structure supports multiple locations per company, each with its own shipping address.

For orders shipping to multiple locations simultaneously, you have two options:

1. **Separate orders per location:** The buyer places individual orders for each ship-to address. Simpler to manage but requires multiple checkout sessions.
2. **Split shipments:** The buyer places a single order, and you split fulfillment across multiple locations in the admin. This is operationally more complex but preferred by larger buyers.

## Bulk Pricing Tiers: Strategy and Implementation

Effective bulk pricing encourages larger orders while protecting your margins. Here is how to structure and implement pricing tiers that drive wholesale revenue.

### Designing Your Tier Structure

A well-designed tier structure balances three objectives:

1. **Incentivize volume:** Each tier should offer meaningful savings that motivate buyers to order more
2. **Protect margins:** Even your deepest discount tier must maintain profitability
3. **Create aspirational targets:** Buyers should feel they are close to the next tier, encouraging slightly larger orders

**Example tier structure for a beauty brand:**

| Tier | Monthly Volume | Discount | Per-Unit Example |
|------|---------------|----------|------------------|
| Bronze | $500-$2,499 | 40% off retail | $18.00 (from $30 retail) |
| Silver | $2,500-$9,999 | 45% off retail | $16.50 |
| Gold | $10,000-$24,999 | 48% off retail | $15.60 |
| Platinum | $25,000+ | 52% off retail | $14.40 |

The jump from Bronze to Silver (5 percentage points) is meaningful enough to motivate volume increases. The jump from Gold to Platinum (4 percentage points) is slightly smaller, reflecting that margins get tighter at higher discount levels.

### Implementing Quantity Price Breaks

Shopify's native quantity price breaks (available on Advanced and Plus) let you set per-product pricing tiers based on order quantity:

1. Navigate to a product in your admin
2. In the pricing section, click "Add quantity price break"
3. Set quantity thresholds and corresponding prices
4. Assign the price breaks to specific B2B price lists

**Example for a candle product:**

- 1-11 units: $12.00 each
- 12-47 units: $10.50 each (12.5% savings)
- 48-95 units: $9.00 each (25% savings)
- 96+ units: $7.80 each (35% savings)

These breaks align with common wholesale purchasing patterns (by the dozen, by the case of 48, by the pallet layer of 96).

### Communicating Pricing to Wholesale Customers

Transparency builds trust with wholesale buyers. Make your pricing structure visible and easy to understand:

- Display quantity break tables on product pages for logged-in wholesale customers
- Create a wholesale pricing guide (PDF) that buyers can share with their purchasing departments
- Show "You save X%" messaging as quantities increase in the cart
- Send proactive notifications when a buyer is close to the next pricing tier

## Managing B2B-Specific Catalogs

Catalog management for B2B requires different strategies than retail merchandising.

### Product Organization for Wholesale

Organize your wholesale catalog around how businesses buy, not how consumers browse:

**By category and sub-category.** Wholesale buyers know what they need. Organize products into clear categories with filter options for size, color, material, and other attributes.

**By case pack or minimum.** Group products by their minimum order configuration. A wholesale buyer ordering candles needs to know they come in cases of 12. Display this prominently.

**By margin or promotional status.** Create collections for high-margin products that you want to push to wholesale accounts, or seasonal promotions with limited-time wholesale pricing.

**By new arrivals and pre-orders.** Wholesale buyers need to see new products before they hit retail so they can plan their purchasing and merchandising.

### Restricting Catalog Access

Not every product should be available to every wholesale customer. Use catalogs to control access:

- **Geographic restrictions:** Products approved for sale in certain regions only
- **Channel restrictions:** Products available only to specific retail channels (online retailers versus brick-and-mortar)
- **Exclusivity periods:** New products available to select accounts before general wholesale release
- **Discontinued products:** Clearance pricing visible only to accounts willing to buy remaining inventory

### Pre-Orders and Seasonal Buying

Wholesale often involves pre-ordering products months before delivery. Structure your pre-order process:

1. Create a "Pre-Order" catalog with upcoming products
2. Set estimated delivery dates in product descriptions
3. Accept orders with payment due on shipment (or a deposit structure)
4. Update wholesale customers on production timelines
5. Convert pre-orders to fulfilled orders when inventory arrives

Shopify's draft order system or pre-order apps like Pre-Order Manager handle the mechanics of accepting orders for products not yet in stock.

## Handling Purchase Orders and Invoicing

### Purchase Order Workflow

A professional PO workflow for your Shopify wholesale channel includes:

**Receiving POs:** Accept purchase orders via your B2B portal, email, or sales rep entry. Enter POs as draft orders in Shopify, associating them with the correct company account.

**PO Confirmation:** Send order confirmation with your order number and the customer's PO number. Include expected ship dates, payment terms, and any notes about product availability.

**Fulfillment and Shipping:** Process and ship the order, updating the order status in Shopify. Send shipping notifications with tracking information referenced to the PO number.

**Invoicing:** Generate invoices that match the PO and include all required B2B details: PO number, payment terms, itemized pricing, shipping charges, tax (or tax-exempt notation), and payment instructions.

**Payment Collection:** Track invoice due dates and send payment reminders. For overdue accounts, have a clear escalation process.

### Invoice Generation

Shopify's native invoicing for B2B orders includes essential details, but many wholesale operations need more customization. Consider these approaches:

**Shopify's built-in invoicing** works for straightforward wholesale operations. Invoices are generated from orders and can be customized with your branding.

**Accounting integration** with QuickBooks, Xero, or NetSuite provides more professional invoice formats, automated payment tracking, and financial reporting that aligns with your accounting workflow.

**Dedicated invoicing apps** like Sufio or Order Printer Pro create professionally formatted PDF invoices with custom fields, multi-currency support, and batch processing.

## Running B2B and DTC on the Same Store

One of Shopify's biggest advantages for wholesale is the ability to run B2B alongside DTC on the same store. Here is how to make this work effectively.

### Separate Experiences, Single Backend

Your retail customers and wholesale customers should see different versions of your store:

**Retail visitors** see standard retail pricing, consumer-focused product descriptions, lifestyle imagery, and standard checkout with credit card payment.

**Logged-in B2B customers** see wholesale pricing, case pack information, simplified product descriptions focused on specifications, and B2B checkout with PO fields and payment terms.

Shopify Plus handles this separation natively through the B2B channel and company accounts. On lower plans, apps like Wholesale Club use customer tags to toggle between retail and wholesale views.

### Inventory Allocation

When running both channels from shared inventory, consider inventory allocation strategies:

**Shared pool:** Both channels draw from the same inventory. Simple to manage but risks wholesale orders consuming inventory needed for high-margin retail sales.

**Reserved inventory:** Allocate a percentage of inventory to each channel. For example, reserve 60% for wholesale and 40% for retail. This prevents one channel from cannibalizing the other.

**Priority-based allocation:** Fulfill retail orders first (higher margin) and wholesale orders from remaining inventory. This approach maximizes profitability but may frustrate wholesale customers.

**Location-based separation:** If you have multiple warehouse locations, dedicate specific locations to wholesale fulfillment and others to retail.

### Pricing Protection

Maintain pricing integrity across channels:

- **Wholesale customers should not see retail prices** (and vice versa). Use Shopify's catalog system or app-based visibility controls to enforce this.
- **MAP (Minimum Advertised Price) enforcement.** If you set MAP policies for retailers, document them clearly and monitor compliance.
- **Wholesale-only products.** Some products (bulk sizes, multi-packs) may be wholesale-only and should not appear on your retail storefront.

## Tax-Exempt Customer Management

Managing tax exemptions is critical for B2B compliance.

### Collecting and Verifying Exemption Certificates

Before granting tax-exempt status to any wholesale account:

1. **Request the certificate.** Ask for a valid resale certificate, tax exemption certificate, or VAT registration number during account setup.
2. **Verify validity.** Check that the certificate is current, covers your product categories, and is issued by the appropriate jurisdiction.
3. **Store securely.** Maintain digital copies of all exemption certificates with expiration dates.
4. **Set renewal reminders.** Most exemption certificates expire. Set calendar reminders to request updated certificates before expiration.

### Configuring Tax Exemptions in Shopify

For company accounts on Shopify Plus:

1. Navigate to the company account
2. Under tax settings, toggle "Tax exempt" for the applicable location
3. Enter the tax exemption certificate number
4. The system will automatically exclude tax from orders placed by this account

For individual customer accounts (non-Plus):

1. Navigate to the customer profile
2. Check "Tax exempt" in the tax settings
3. Enter relevant tax ID or certificate information in customer notes

### Multi-Jurisdiction Complexity

B2B tax exemptions vary by jurisdiction:

- **US resale exemptions** are state-by-state. A customer with a California resale certificate is still taxable on orders shipped to Texas unless they also have a Texas certificate.
- **EU VAT reverse charge** applies to B2B transactions between VAT-registered businesses in different EU member states.
- **Canadian provincial exemptions** vary by province and product category.

For complex multi-jurisdiction wholesale operations, invest in tax automation through Avalara or TaxJar. Manual tax management becomes unmanageable beyond 20-30 wholesale accounts.

## Scaling Your Wholesale Channel

### Building a Wholesale Sales Team

As your wholesale channel grows, dedicated sales resources become necessary:

**Inside sales representatives** manage inbound wholesale inquiries, onboard new accounts, and handle reorder support. One inside sales rep can typically manage 50-100 active wholesale accounts.

**Outside sales representatives** visit potential wholesale customers, attend trade shows, and build relationships with major accounts. Effective for expanding into new retail channels or geographic markets.

**Sales agent ordering** allows your reps to place orders on behalf of wholesale customers directly in Shopify. This is essential for trade show orders and phone orders. Shopify Plus supports this natively; lower plans can use apps like Wholesale Helper's agent ordering feature.

### Wholesale Marketing and Acquisition

Finding and converting new wholesale customers requires different tactics than DTC marketing:

**Trade shows and industry events** remain the most effective channel for wholesale customer acquisition. Budget $5,000-$15,000 per show for booth costs, travel, and samples.

**Industry directories and marketplaces** like Faire, Tundra, and Bulletin connect brands with retailers looking for new products. These platforms charge commissions (typically 15-25%) but provide access to curated retailer audiences.

**LinkedIn outreach** targets specific buyers at retail organizations. Personalized outreach highlighting your brand story, margins, and support generates meetings with purchasing decision-makers.

**Wholesale landing pages** on your website convert organic traffic into wholesale leads. Include an application form, product overview, margin information, and testimonials from current retailers.

### Automating Wholesale Operations

As order volume grows, automation becomes essential:

**Shopify Flow** (Plus) automates common wholesale workflows:
- Auto-tag customers based on order volume tier
- Send payment reminders for overdue invoices
- Notify sales reps when accounts have not ordered in 30 days
- Auto-approve reorders from trusted accounts

**Inventory management** through apps like Stocky or TradeGecko (now QuickBooks Commerce) synchronizes inventory across channels and automates purchase orders to suppliers when wholesale demand depletes stock.

**Shipping automation** through ShipStation or ShipBob handles wholesale order fulfillment, including palletized shipping, commercial invoicing for international orders, and carrier rate optimization.

## Common B2B Challenges and Solutions

### Challenge: Minimum Order Enforcement

**Problem:** Wholesale customers place orders below your minimum, making small orders unprofitable.

**Solution:** Configure minimum order values in your B2B settings or wholesale app. Display minimum requirements prominently during the shopping experience. Consider a "small order surcharge" of $15-$25 for orders below the minimum rather than blocking them entirely, as this covers your handling costs without losing the sale.

### Challenge: Payment Collection on Net Terms

**Problem:** Wholesale customers pay late or default on invoices.

**Solution:** Implement a structured collections process. Send automated reminders at 7 days before due, on the due date, and at 7, 14, and 30 days past due. Escalate to phone calls at 30 days past due. Consider requiring credit applications and trade references for net terms above $5,000. Use invoice factoring services for cash flow management on large receivables.

### Challenge: Catalog Pricing Errors

**Problem:** Retail and wholesale prices get mixed up, or price list updates do not propagate correctly.

**Solution:** Audit your price lists monthly. Use percentage-based wholesale pricing where possible so that retail price changes automatically flow to wholesale prices. Test the wholesale customer experience regularly by logging in as a test wholesale account and verifying pricing accuracy.

### Challenge: Returns and Defectives

**Problem:** Wholesale returns are more complex than retail returns due to larger quantities and different expectations.

**Solution:** Establish a clear wholesale return policy separate from your retail policy. Common wholesale terms include: returns within 30 days for defective merchandise only, credit memo rather than refund for returns, restocking fees of 15-25% for non-defective returns, and RMA (Return Merchandise Authorization) required before shipping returns.

## Measuring Wholesale Performance

Track these key metrics to evaluate and optimize your B2B channel:

**Revenue metrics:**
- Wholesale revenue as percentage of total revenue
- Average wholesale order value (target: 5-10x your retail AOV)
- Revenue per wholesale account
- Wholesale revenue growth rate month-over-month

**Account metrics:**
- Number of active wholesale accounts
- New account acquisition rate
- Account churn rate (target: under 10% annually)
