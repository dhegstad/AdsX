## How Does Shopify Flow Work and What Can It Automate?

Shopify Flow is your first automation tool because it is free, native, and requires no external accounts.

**How Flow works:** Every Flow workflow has three components:

- **Trigger:** The event that starts the workflow (order created, product inventory changed, customer created, etc.)
- **Condition:** The criteria that must be met for the workflow to continue (order total > $100, inventory < 20, customer tag = "VIP")
- **Action:** What happens when the trigger fires and conditions are met (add tag, send email, create task, change inventory)

**Setting up your first Flow workflow:**

**Step 1:** Navigate to Apps > Shopify Flow in your Shopify admin. Click "Create workflow."

**Step 2:** Click "Select a trigger" and browse the available triggers. For your first workflow, select "Order created."

**Step 3:** Click the "+" below the trigger to add a condition. Select "Order total" and set it to "greater than $200."

**Step 4:** Click the "+" below the condition to add an action. Select "Add customer tags" and enter "high-value-customer."

**Step 5:** Name your workflow (e.g., "Tag high-value customers") and turn it on.

This workflow now automatically tags every customer who places an order over $200, enabling you to create targeted marketing segments without manual tagging.

## What Are the Top 20 Automation Recipes for Shopify?

These proven automation recipes cover the most common Shopify operations. Each one replaces a manual task with an automated workflow.

**Order Management Automations:**

1. **Auto-tag orders by value tier.** Tag orders as "high-value," "medium-value," or "standard" based on order total thresholds. Platform: Shopify Flow.
2. **Route orders to the correct fulfillment team.** Send domestic orders to warehouse A and international orders to a 3PL partner via notification. Platform: Shopify Flow + Slack.
3. **Flag orders with notes for manual review.** When a customer adds an order note, send a notification to your operations team so the note is not overlooked. Platform: Shopify Flow.
4. **Auto-cancel high-risk orders.** When Shopify's fraud analysis flags an order as high risk, auto-hold fulfillment and notify your review team. Platform: Shopify Flow.

**Customer Management Automations:**

5. **Segment customers by purchase behavior.** Auto-tag customers as "first-time," "repeat," or "VIP" based on order count and total spend. Platform: Shopify Flow.
6. **Sync new customers to your CRM.** When a customer is created in Shopify, create a matching contact in HubSpot, Salesforce, or your CRM. Platform: Zapier.
7. **Add customers to email lists based on purchase.** When a customer buys from a specific collection, add them to the corresponding Klaviyo segment. Platform: Zapier or Klaviyo integration.
8. **Send a birthday discount automatically.** If you collect customer birthdays, trigger a birthday email with a unique discount code 7 days before their birthday. Platform: Zapier + Klaviyo.

**Inventory and Product Automations:**

9. **Low stock alerts.** Notify your team via Slack or email when any product drops below its reorder threshold. Platform: Shopify Flow.
10. **Auto-hide out-of-stock products.** When a product's inventory reaches zero, remove it from your online store and collections to prevent a poor customer experience. Platform: Shopify Flow.
11. **Re-publish when restocked.** When inventory is added back to a previously out-of-stock product, re-publish it and send a back-in-stock notification to waiting customers. Platform: Shopify Flow + Back in Stock app.
12. **Sync inventory to a spreadsheet.** Update a Google Sheet with current inventory levels daily for reporting and planning. Platform: Zapier or Make.

**Marketing Automations:**

13. **Post-purchase review request.** Send an email requesting a product review 14 days after an order is marked as delivered. Platform: Zapier + your email tool.
14. **Auto-post new products to social media.** When a new product is published, create a social media post on Instagram, Facebook, and Pinterest. Platform: Zapier + Buffer/Later.
15. **Abandoned cart escalation.** If Shopify's built-in abandoned cart email does not convert, trigger a more aggressive follow-up sequence via your email platform. Platform: Zapier + Klaviyo.
16. **Tag products for promotional campaigns.** Automatically tag products that meet sale criteria (seasonal, overstocked, low margin) so they are easy to include in promotional collections. Platform: Shopify Flow.

**Operations and Finance Automations:**

17. **Create an invoice in QuickBooks for every order.** When an order is paid, create a corresponding invoice in your accounting software. Platform: Zapier or Make.
18. **Notify your team of refunds.** When a refund is issued, send a notification to your finance channel with the order details and refund amount. Platform: Shopify Flow + Slack.
19. **Track fulfillment SLA.** If an order is not fulfilled within 48 hours, send an escalation notification to the operations manager. Platform: Shopify Flow or Zapier with a delay step.
20. **Weekly performance report.** Compile key metrics (orders, revenue, top products, inventory alerts) into a formatted Slack message or email sent every Monday morning. Platform: Make with scheduled trigger.

## How Do You Connect Shopify to Your Entire Tech Stack With Zapier?

Zapier bridges Shopify and the 6,000+ tools that Shopify Flow cannot reach directly.

**Step 1: Create a Zapier account and connect Shopify.** Sign up at zapier.com, then add Shopify as a connected app. Authorize Zapier to access your Shopify store data.

**Step 2: Understand Zapier's structure.** A "Zap" is a single automation with a trigger and one or more actions. A "Task" is one execution of a Zap. Free plans include 100 tasks per month — enough for small stores to test the platform.

**Step 3: Build your first Zap.** The most universally useful Shopify-to-external-tool Zap is syncing orders to your accounting software:

- Trigger: New Paid Order in Shopify
- Action: Create Invoice in QuickBooks Online (or Xero, FreshBooks, etc.)
- Map fields: Order total to invoice amount, customer name to client name, line items to invoice line items

**Step 4: Add multi-step logic.** Zapier supports conditional paths, filters, delays, and formatters. For example: If order total > $500, send a Slack notification AND create a QuickBooks invoice AND add the customer to a HubSpot "high-value" list.

**Step 5: Monitor and maintain.** Check your Zapier dashboard weekly. Review the task history for any failed Zaps, check that your task count is within your plan limits, and update Zaps when your business processes change.

## When Should You Use Make Instead of Zapier?

Make (formerly Integromat) excels at complex, multi-step workflows that Zapier handles awkwardly or expensively.

**Choose Make when:**

- Your workflow has branching logic (if/then/else paths with multiple outcomes)
- You need to process data in batches (aggregating multiple Shopify orders into a single report)
- You need to transform data between steps (reformatting dates, calculating values, merging fields)
- Your workflow has more than 5 steps (Make's per-operation pricing is more economical than Zapier's per-task pricing for complex workflows)
- You need to process webhooks from Shopify in real time with sub-second latency

**Choose Zapier when:**

- Your workflow is simple (1 trigger, 1-3 actions)
- You need a specific integration that Make does not support
- Your team prefers a simpler interface with less technical overhead
- You need the largest possible library of pre-built integrations

## How Do You Troubleshoot and Maintain Automations?

Automations require ongoing maintenance to stay accurate and effective.

**Set up monitoring.** Every automation platform provides error notifications. Enable email or Slack alerts for failed workflows. Check your automation dashboards weekly.

**Document every automation.** Create a simple spreadsheet listing: automation name, platform, what it does, when it was last updated, and who owns it. Without documentation, automations become a black box that nobody understands or maintains.

**Test after Shopify updates.** When Shopify releases updates or you change your theme, test your automations to verify they still work correctly. Platform updates and API changes can break existing workflows.

**Review quarterly.** Every quarter, review all active automations. Disable any that are no longer relevant. Update conditions and thresholds that may have changed with your business (new shipping policies, updated price tiers, changed team structure).

**Avoid over-automation.** Not every process should be automated. If a task requires judgment, happens rarely, or changes frequently, manual execution may be more reliable than an automation that needs constant adjustment.

## What Should You Do This Week?

Build your first automation system with these steps:

1. **List your 10 most repetitive tasks.** Write down every task you do more than 3 times per week that follows the same pattern. Estimate how many minutes each task takes. Sort by total weekly time spent.
2. **Set up Shopify Flow.** Open Shopify Flow and create two workflows: auto-tag high-value customers (orders over your chosen threshold) and low-stock alerts (inventory below your reorder point). Test both with sample data.
3. **Create a Zapier account and build one Zap.** Connect Shopify to your most-used external tool — your accounting software, CRM, or communication platform. Build the Zap, test it, and turn it on.
4. **Document your automations.** Start a simple spreadsheet tracking every automation you create: name, platform, trigger, action, date created. This becomes your automation registry and prevents duplicated or conflicting workflows.
5. **Schedule a weekly 15-minute automation review.** Check your automation dashboards for errors, review task counts against plan limits, and identify one new task from your repetitive tasks list to automate next week. Building your automation system incrementally prevents overwhelm and ensures each workflow is reliable before you add more.
