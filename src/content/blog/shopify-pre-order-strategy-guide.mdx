Pre-orders let you validate demand, generate revenue, and fund production before a single unit ships. For Shopify merchants, this means less inventory risk, better cash flow, and a built-in marketing event around every product launch.

The pre-order model is not new, but the tools available on Shopify in 2026 make it more accessible and more sophisticated than ever. You can run simple "reserve now, ship later" campaigns or full crowdfunding-style launches with funding goals, tiered rewards, and social proof mechanics — all within your Shopify store.

This guide covers the three types of pre-orders, how to set them up, and the marketing strategies that turn a pre-order campaign into a successful product launch.

## What Are the Different Types of Pre-Orders?

Not all pre-orders work the same way. The type you choose affects cash flow, customer expectations, and refund rates.

| Pre-Order Type | Payment Timing | Best For | Avg. Conversion Rate | Refund Rate | Cash Flow Impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pay Now | At order placement | Known ship dates, established brands | 3.5 – 5.0% | 8 – 15% | Immediate positive |
| Pay Later | At shipment | Uncertain timelines, new products | 2.0 – 3.5% | 3 – 5% | Delayed |
| Deposit + Balance | Split payment | High-ticket items ($200+) | 2.5 – 4.0% | 5 – 8% | Partial upfront |
| Crowdfunding-Style | At order, goal-dependent | New product launches, validation | 1.5 – 3.0% | 10 – 20% | Conditional |

### Pay Now Pre-Orders

The customer pays the full price at checkout. You ship when the product is available. This is the simplest model and generates immediate cash flow.

**Advantages:** Cash in hand to fund production. Stronger customer commitment — people who pay upfront are less likely to cancel than people who just reserved a spot.

**Risks:** If production delays push the ship date, you face refund requests and customer frustration. Legally, some jurisdictions require you to ship within a stated timeframe or offer a refund.

**Best practice:** Only use pay-now pre-orders when you have high confidence in your delivery timeline. Communicate a specific ship date (not "coming soon") and add a 1-2 week buffer.

### Pay Later Pre-Orders

The customer reserves the product but is not charged until it ships. Their payment method is authorized but not captured.

**Advantages:** Lower barrier for customers. Fewer refund requests since no money has changed hands. Better for products with uncertain production timelines.

**Risks:** Authorization holds expire (typically 7-30 days depending on the payment processor), which means you may need to re-authorize. Some customers will have insufficient funds when you attempt to capture.

**Best practice:** Use for products with delivery windows longer than 30 days. Send payment reminder emails 48 hours before capture.

### Crowdfunding-Style Pre-Orders

You set a funding goal and a deadline. If the goal is met, all orders are processed. If not, all payments are refunded. This is Kickstarter mechanics on your own Shopify store.

**Advantages:** Validates demand before committing to production. Creates urgency and social proof ("78% funded — 4 days left"). Eliminates the risk of producing inventory that does not sell.

**Risks:** If the campaign fails to meet its goal, you lose the marketing momentum and may lose potential customers permanently. Campaigns that stall at 40-60% funded create negative social proof.

**Best practice:** Set your funding goal at the minimum viable production run, not your aspirational target. A campaign that hits 200% funded is better marketing than one that barely crosses the finish line.

## How Do You Set Up Pre-Orders on Shopify?

Shopify does not have native pre-order functionality. You need a third-party app.

### Step 1: Choose Your Pre-Order App

**PreOrder Manager by Amai ($24.95/month)** — The most full-featured option. Supports all pre-order types, mixed carts (pre-order + in-stock items in one order), automatic activation when inventory hits zero, and partial payments.

**Pre-Order Now by Prestige ($19.95/month)** — Simpler interface, strong button and messaging customization. Good for merchants who want straightforward pay-now pre-orders without complexity.

**Crowdfunder ($29/month)** — Purpose-built for crowdfunding-style campaigns on Shopify. Includes funding goal tracking, progress bars, campaign timelines, and backer counts.

**Timesact ($9.90/month)** — Budget option with basic pre-order and coming-soon functionality. Suitable for stores running occasional pre-orders rather than a continuous program.

### Step 2: Configure the Product

Set the product to pre-order status in your chosen app. Configure the pre-order button text ("Pre-Order Now — Ships June 2026"), the expected delivery date, and any messaging that should appear on the product page.

For pay-now orders, no special payment configuration is needed — the standard Shopify checkout handles it. For pay-later orders, your app will handle the authorization-and-later-capture flow.

### Step 3: Set Communication Expectations

Create an automated email sequence for pre-order customers:

1. **Order confirmation** — Restate the expected ship date. Thank them for their trust.
2. **Production update (every 2-4 weeks)** — Share progress. Photos of prototypes, production line updates, anything that builds excitement.
3. **Ship date confirmation** — Sent 1-2 weeks before shipping. Confirm the exact date.
4. **Shipping notification** — Standard tracking information.

This communication sequence is not optional. Pre-order customers tolerate waiting. They do not tolerate silence.

### Step 4: Manage Inventory Transitions

When pre-order inventory arrives and converts to in-stock status, coordinate the transition carefully:

- Fulfill all pre-orders before making the product available for general purchase
- Update the product page from pre-order to standard "Add to Cart"
- Adjust any pre-order-specific messaging or pricing

## How Do You Market a Pre-Order Campaign?

Pre-orders are inherently marketing events. The product is not available yet, which creates scarcity and urgency — two of the most powerful conversion drivers in e-commerce.

**Build anticipation before the pre-order opens.** Start teasing the product 2-4 weeks before the pre-order launch. Behind-the-scenes content, product development stories, and sneak peeks build an audience that is ready to order on day one.

**Create a VIP early access list.** Collect email addresses from interested customers and give them first access to the pre-order, ideally with an exclusive discount or bonus. This creates your initial sales burst, which generates social proof for the public launch.

**Use countdown timers.** On the product page, in emails, and on social media. Countdown timers create urgency and give undecided buyers a deadline. For crowdfunding-style campaigns, pair the timer with a funding progress bar.

**Leverage social proof in real-time.** Display pre-order counts on the product page: "247 customers have already pre-ordered." This reduces purchase anxiety and creates momentum.

**Offer pre-order incentives.** Early bird pricing (10-20% off the eventual retail price), exclusive colorways or variants, bundled bonuses (free accessory with pre-order), or priority shipping. The incentive needs to be valuable enough to justify paying for a product that does not exist yet.

**Run limited-time pre-order windows.** Rather than leaving pre-orders open indefinitely, create specific windows (72-hour flash pre-order, one-week campaign). Open-ended pre-orders lack urgency. Time-limited campaigns drive action.

## What Are the Legal Considerations for Pre-Orders?

Pre-orders carry specific legal obligations that vary by jurisdiction.

**FTC Mail Order Rule (US):** If you advertise a specific ship date, you must ship within that timeframe. If you cannot, you must notify the customer and offer a full refund. Failing to do so violates federal trade regulations.

**Consumer Rights Act (UK/EU):** Customers have the right to cancel a pre-order and receive a full refund at any time before the product ships. You cannot charge cancellation fees on pre-orders in most EU jurisdictions.

**Payment authorization limits:** Credit card authorization holds expire after 7-30 days. For pay-later pre-orders extending beyond this window, you need a strategy for re-authorization.

**Clear disclosure:** Your product page must clearly state that the item is a pre-order, the expected delivery date, and the payment terms. Burying this information in fine print creates legal risk and customer service headaches.

## How Do You Handle Pre-Order Inventory Management?

The core challenge of pre-orders is matching commitments to actual inventory.

**Cap pre-orders to production capacity.** If your production run is 500 units, cap pre-orders at 500. Overselling creates fulfillment crises and customer anger.

**Track pre-order velocity.** Monitor how fast pre-orders are coming in. If you sell 200 units in 48 hours with a 500-unit cap, you may want to increase the production run or close pre-orders early to manage expectations.

**Build buffer stock.** Order 10-15% above your pre-order count to cover production defects, shipping damage, and additional demand at general release.

**Separate pre-order fulfillment from general inventory.** When production arrives, physically or digitally earmark pre-order units before making any inventory available for general sale.

## Your Pre-Order Launch Playbook

1. Select your pre-order type based on your product timeline and cash flow needs
2. Install and configure your pre-order app with clear delivery messaging
3. Build a VIP early access email list 2-4 weeks before launch
4. Create the pre-order product page with explicit delivery dates and incentives
5. Set up your automated communication sequence (confirmation, updates, ship date, tracking)
6. Launch to VIP list first, then open to the public 24-48 hours later
7. Monitor pre-order velocity and adjust production plans accordingly
8. Send biweekly updates to pre-order customers until shipment
9. Fulfill all pre-orders before converting to general availability
10. Analyze campaign data to optimize your next pre-order launch

Pre-orders on Shopify transform the traditional e-commerce model from "build it and hope they come" to "prove they will come, then build it." The risk shifts from unsold inventory to execution and communication — a trade-off that favors disciplined operators.
