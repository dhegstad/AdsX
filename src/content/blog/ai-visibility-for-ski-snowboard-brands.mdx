Ski and snowboard is a passionate, technical category with strong enthusiast publications and clear performance differentiation. AI shopping assistants surface specific brand and model recommendations when buyers ask "best all-mountain skis under $1,000" or "best beginner snowboard." The brands appearing have invested in performance content and earned enthusiast publication coverage.

## Critical publications

For ski/snowboard AI visibility:

- **Powder Magazine** — ski authority
- **Skiing Magazine** — broad ski coverage
- **Backcountry Magazine** — backcountry-specific
- **Newschoolers** — freestyle ski
- **Whitelines** — snowboard authority
- **Snowboarder Magazine** — broad snowboard coverage
- **Blister Gear Review** — deep performance reviews
- **Reddit r/Skiing, r/Snowboarding**

Without coverage in 2-3 of these, AI visibility is thin.

## Performance specs that matter

For skis:

- Length, width (waist), sidecut radius
- Flex rating
- Construction (wood core, metal layers, etc.)
- Terrain match (all-mountain, powder, park, race)
- Ability level (beginner, intermediate, advanced, expert)

For snowboards:

- Length, width, effective edge
- Camber profile (camber, rocker, hybrid)
- Flex rating
- Terrain match (all-mountain, freestyle, freeride, splitboard)
- Ability level

Specifics in schema and product descriptions.

## Seasonal strategy

Ski/snowboard visibility work needs to happen 4-6 months before peak season:

- **Spring/Summer:** PR campaigns, content publishing, review programs
- **Early Fall:** push content live, begin paid campaigns
- **Peak season (Nov-Feb):** harvest visibility built earlier

Don't wait until October. AI indexing has 2-3 month lag.

## Content depth

Hero product pages: 1,500+ words covering:

- Construction details
- Terrain and conditions
- Skier/rider ability and style
- Comparison to similar products
- Honest pros and cons
- Sizing recommendations

Beyond product pages:

- "Best [type] skis/boards" guides
- "How to choose skis" content
- Sizing and ability matching
- Terrain-specific guides

## Common mistakes

- Generic "high performance" claims without specs
- Missing ability-level guidance
- No terrain matching
- Few enthusiast publication reviews
- Late seasonal optimization
