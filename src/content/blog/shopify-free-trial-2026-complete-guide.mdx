## What You CAN'T Do During the Shopify Free Trial

While the trial is generous, there are a few limitations to be aware of.

### No Real Customer Transactions

The biggest limitation: **you cannot process real customer payments during the trial**. Your checkout is disabled for actual purchases. Customers who visit your store can browse and add items to their cart, but they cannot complete a purchase.

This means you cannot:

- Accept credit card payments
- Process PayPal transactions
- Fulfill actual orders
- Collect real revenue

To start accepting payments, you need to select a paid plan and set up Shopify Payments or another payment provider.

### No Password Removal (Without a Plan)

During the trial, your store is password-protected by default. Visitors see a "Coming Soon" page unless they have the password. You can share the password with friends and family for feedback, but your store is not publicly accessible to shoppers until you choose a plan.

### Third-Party Theme Limitations

While free themes are fully accessible during the trial, if you want to purchase and install a premium theme from the Shopify Theme Store (ranging from $180 to $350), you can preview them but may not be able to fully install until you are on a paid plan.

### No Custom Domain Activation

You can purchase or connect a custom domain during the trial, but it will not go live and resolve to your store until you activate a paid plan. Your store remains on the yourstore.myshopify.com subdomain during the trial.

### What This Means in Practice

The trial period is designed for **building and preparing**, not for selling. Think of it as your construction phase. You are setting up the store, adding products, configuring settings, and getting everything ready so that when you activate your paid plan, you can go live immediately.

This is actually a good thing. It means you can take the time to get everything right without pressure from live customers finding an unfinished store.

## Hidden Costs to Know About Before You Start

The Shopify free trial in 2026 is genuinely free, and the $1/month deal is genuinely $1/month. But once you are on a regular plan, there are costs beyond your monthly subscription that you should plan for.

### Transaction Fees

If you use **Shopify Payments** (Shopify's built-in payment processor, powered by Stripe), you pay only credit card processing fees — no additional Shopify transaction fees. This is the cheapest option for most merchants.

If you use a **third-party payment gateway** (like PayPal, Authorize.net, or a regional provider), Shopify charges an additional transaction fee on top of the gateway's own fees:

- Basic: 2.0% per transaction
- Shopify: 1.0% per transaction
- Advanced: 0.6% per transaction

**Bottom line**: Use Shopify Payments if it is available in your country. You avoid the extra transaction fee entirely.

### Apps

Most Shopify stores use 5-15 apps, and many of them are not free. Typical monthly app costs:

| App Category | Free Options | Paid Range |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Email Marketing | Shopify Email, Mailchimp | $15 - $300/month |
| Reviews | Judge.me free, Shopify Reviews | $15 - $99/month |
| SEO Tools | Basic Shopify SEO | $20 - $80/month |
| Upsell/Cross-sell | Many free options | $10 - $50/month |
| Subscription/Recurring | Limited free | $50 - $200/month |
| Loyalty/Rewards | Smile.io free tier | $20 - $200/month |
| Live Chat | Tidio free tier | $19 - $100/month |
| Shipping | Shopify built-in | $10 - $60/month |

A realistic app budget for a new store: **$50 to $150/month** on top of your Shopify plan. You can start with free apps and add paid ones as revenue grows.

### Themes

Shopify's free themes are excellent and sufficient for most stores. But if you want a premium theme from the Shopify Theme Store, expect to pay **$180 to $350 as a one-time cost**. Premium themes offer more design flexibility, built-in features that may reduce your need for apps, and often better performance.

Third-party theme marketplaces like Themeforest offer themes for $50-$80, but quality and Shopify compatibility vary.

### Custom Domain

Your Shopify store comes with a free myshopify.com subdomain, but you will want a custom domain for credibility. Options:

- **Buy through Shopify** — $14/year for .com domains, auto-configured
- **Buy from a registrar** (Namecheap, Cloudflare, GoDaddy) — $8-$15/year, manual DNS setup
- **Already own a domain** — Free to connect, just update DNS settings

### Email Costs at Scale

Shopify Email is free for your first 10,000 emails per month. Beyond that, it costs $1 per 1,000 additional emails. If you use a third-party email platform like Klaviyo, costs start at $20/month once you exceed 250 contacts.

## 10 Common Mistakes That Waste Your Shopify Free Trial

After watching thousands of merchants start their Shopify journeys, these are the most common trial-period mistakes — and how to avoid them.

### Mistake 1: Starting Without Preparation

Starting the trial on impulse without product images, descriptions, or a plan means you spend your free days doing prep work instead of building. Do the prep work before you start the clock.

### Mistake 2: Obsessing Over the Perfect Theme

You can change your theme anytime. Spending an entire day comparing themes is a waste of your trial. Pick a well-reviewed free theme, customize it with your brand colors and logo, and move on. You can always refine it later.

### Mistake 3: Installing Too Many Apps

New merchants often install 15-20 apps during the trial, trying to add every feature they have seen on competitor stores. This slows your store down, creates conflicts between apps, and overwhelms you with settings. Start with 3-5 essential apps maximum.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Mobile

If your store does not look good on a phone, it does not look good. Period. Over 70% of your traffic will come from mobile devices. Check every page, every product, and every step of the browsing experience on a phone before your trial ends.

### Mistake 5: Skipping Product SEO

Every product page has SEO fields: title tag, meta description, and URL handle. Filling these out during the trial means your products are search-engine-ready from day one. Skipping this step means starting from behind on organic search visibility.

### Mistake 6: Not Setting Up Collections

Dumping all your products into the store without organizing them into collections makes navigation confusing for customers. Create logical collections and build your main menu around them.

### Mistake 7: Using Placeholder Content

"Lorem ipsum" and "About us text goes here" pages signal to customers that your store is not ready. If a page is not finished, do not publish it. Only make pages live when they have real, polished content.

### Mistake 8: Forgetting Legal Pages

No privacy policy, no return policy, no terms of service — these are not optional. They are legally required in most jurisdictions and are trust signals for customers. Use Shopify's built-in policy generators to create them quickly.

### Mistake 9: Not Testing on Multiple Devices

Your store needs to work on iPhones, Androids, iPads, laptops, and desktop monitors. Test on at least 2-3 different devices and browsers. Ask friends with different devices to check as well.

### Mistake 10: Waiting Too Long After the Trial

Some merchants finish the trial with a great store and then wait weeks or months before selecting a plan. Momentum matters. Your store setup is fresh in your mind, your energy is high, and the $1/month deal makes the financial risk negligible. Choose a plan and launch.

## Frequently Asked Questions About the Shopify Free Trial in 2026

### How long is the Shopify free trial in 2026?

The Shopify free trial lasts **3 days** in 2026. During this time, you get full access to the Shopify platform without needing to enter a credit card or any payment information. After the 3-day free trial, you can continue for just $1/month for 3 months on any plan.

### Do you need a credit card for the Shopify free trial?

**No.** You can start the Shopify free trial with just an email address. No credit card, no payment method, no financial commitment whatsoever. You only need to enter payment details when you choose a paid plan after the trial ends.

### What happens after the Shopify free trial ends?

When your 3-day free trial ends, you have two options:

1. **Choose a plan** — Select any Shopify plan and pay just $1 for your first month (with 2 more months at $1 each after that)
2. **Do nothing** — Your store is paused but not deleted. You can come back anytime, select a plan, and pick up exactly where you left off

There is no automatic charge. Shopify does not bill you anything unless you actively choose a plan.

### Can you sell products during the Shopify free trial?

You can **set up** products, but you cannot **sell** them during the trial. The checkout is disabled for real customer transactions until you select a paid plan and configure a payment provider. Think of the trial as your build-and-prepare phase.

### How do you get the $1/month Shopify deal?

Start your free trial through an **[eligible promotional link](https://shopify.pxf.io/c/6318547/3797171/13624)**. When the 3-day trial ends and you select a plan, the $1/month pricing is automatically applied for your first 3 months. No promo code needed.

### Can you extend the Shopify free trial beyond 3 days?

Shopify does not officially offer trial extensions. However, the $1/month deal for 3 months effectively extends your low-cost exploration period to over 90 days for just $3 total. This is a far better deal than a longer free trial would be.

### Which Shopify plan should I choose after the free trial?

For most new merchants, **Shopify Basic ($39/month, but $1/month for your first 3 months)** is the right choice. It includes everything you need: unlimited products, abandoned cart recovery, discount codes, 24/7 support, and more. Only upgrade to a higher plan when you need features like professional reporting, more staff accounts, or lower transaction fees.

### Can I switch plans after choosing one?

Yes. You can upgrade or downgrade your Shopify plan at any time. Upgrades take effect immediately (prorated), and downgrades take effect at the start of your next billing cycle. There is no penalty for changing plans.

### Is the Shopify free trial available worldwide?

Yes. The Shopify free trial is available in virtually every country. However, Shopify Payments (the built-in payment processor) is only available in certain countries. If Shopify Payments is not available in your country, you can still use third-party payment gateways like PayPal, Stripe, or local providers.

### What happens to my data if I don't continue after the trial?

Your store data is preserved. Shopify keeps your account, products, settings, and customizations intact even after the trial expires. You can return months later, select a plan, and everything will be right where you left it. Shopify does eventually delete inactive accounts, but this typically takes well over a year.
