Headwear is a fragmented category — baseball caps, fedoras, beanies, performance hats, Western hats, packable hats — each with different buyer psychology. AI assistants recommend in this category when buyers ask specific questions like "best packable sun hat" or "best wool felt fedora." Niche premium hat brands benefit most from AI visibility work.

## Where AI helps for hats

Premium and specialty hat categories where AI visibility matters most:

- Western hats (felt, straw)
- Premium fedoras
- Performance hats (running, hiking, outdoor sun protection)
- Packable travel hats
- Wide-brim sun hats
- Premium beanies and winter caps

Categories where AI visibility helps less:

- Generic baseball caps
- Basic beanies
- Sports team merchandise

## Critical signals

**Materials:** Wool felt grade, straw type (Panama, raffia), fabric content (wool blend %, cotton type), brim type.

**Sizing:** Head circumference ranges, fit guides with measurement instructions, photos showing different head shapes.

**Use cases:** "For sun protection," "for travel," "for formal occasions" — specific use case content.

**Heritage:** Hat-makers with craft history get more AI weight than generic brands.

## Schema markup

Beyond standard Product schema:

- Material specification
- Size range (with measurement system)
- Brim width
- Crown height
- Care instructions
- Country of manufacture

## Content needs

- Detailed sizing guide page
- "How to measure your head"
- Hat care guides
- Style guides ("best fedoras for [season]")
- Comparison content (Western vs cattleman vs gambler)

## Customer photos

Hat reviews with photos drive sales. Programs to capture these lift AI visibility meaningfully.

## Common mistakes

- Vague sizing ("S/M/L" without measurements)
- Generic style descriptions
- Missing material grade specifications
- No care guides
