May 2026 was one of the busier months OpenAI has had in a while, and almost all of it points in the same direction: ChatGPT is becoming less of a chatbot you visit and more of an assistant that lives inside the tools and decisions you already make. A new default model, deeper memory, a spreadsheet integration, and a reworked shopping experience all shipped within days of each other. For anyone who sells online, the shopping changes are the headline, but the model and memory updates quietly reshape how products get recommended in the first place.

The throughline matters for e-commerce brands. When ChatGPT gets better at remembering what a shopper has asked before, pulling from their files and connected accounts, and answering with fewer fabricated claims, its product recommendations carry more weight with users. That makes AI visibility, the question of whether ChatGPT surfaces your store at all, more important than ever. Here is what actually shipped in May 2026 and what it means for merchants.

## GPT-5.5 Instant becomes the new default

On May 5, 2026, OpenAI began rolling out GPT-5.5 Instant as the default model for ChatGPT, replacing GPT-5.3 Instant. The headline claim is accuracy: in OpenAI's internal evaluations, GPT-5.5 Instant produced 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims than GPT-5.3 Instant on high-stakes prompts covering medicine, law, and finance. Benchmark scores also moved up, with an AIME 2025 math score of 81.2 (versus 65.4 previously) and MMMU-Pro multimodal reasoning of 76 (versus 69.2).

Beyond the numbers, OpenAI is positioning the model as faster to a useful answer: tighter responses, a more natural conversational tone, and better use of context the user has already shared. The rollout started on the web on May 5 with mobile following, and developers can call the model in the API as chat-latest. GPT-5.3 remains available to paid API users for a three-month transition window.

Why this matters for sellers: when shoppers ask ChatGPT for product or brand recommendations, a model that hallucinates less and reasons better is more likely to surface real, current products and fewer made-up ones. That rewards brands with accurate, well-structured information on the open web.

## Memory sources: ChatGPT remembers more, and shows its work

Alongside the model launch, OpenAI introduced memory sources across ChatGPT models. The assistant can now draw on past chats, saved memories, available files, and connected context such as Gmail to deliver more personalized suggestions, recommendations, and next steps. Plus and Pro users received the deeper personalization first, with broader access following.

The notable part is transparency. Memory sources give users visibility into what context was used to personalize a response, with controls to see, delete, or correct anything that is outdated or no longer relevant. Shared chats do not expose those sources to other people.

For e-commerce, persistent memory changes the recommendation game. A shopper who has told ChatGPT their size, budget, brand preferences, or past purchases gets recommendations shaped by that history. Brands that match a shopper's stated preferences, and that are easy for ChatGPT to verify, stand to benefit from this more continuous, context-aware behavior.

## A reworked shopping experience built on discovery, not checkout

The biggest e-commerce story is what ChatGPT shopping has become after OpenAI walked away from Instant Checkout. After that feature stalled, with very few of Shopify's millions of merchants ever going live and consumers seeing little advantage over Amazon or direct checkout, OpenAI refocused on product discovery rather than owning the transaction.

The current experience lets shoppers visually browse products instead of reading text lists, upload an image to find style-matched items, and compare options side by side with pricing and reviews. Instead of completing the sale inside ChatGPT, the assistant surfaces recommendations and routes shoppers to merchant storefronts and apps. We covered the strategic shift in depth in our breakdown of how ChatGPT dropped checkout and pivoted to product discovery.

Crucially for Shopify sellers, stores are integrated through Shopify Catalog, so eligible products can appear in ChatGPT's visual discovery without each merchant hand-building an integration. Larger US retailers including Target, Sephora, Best Buy, and Wayfair connected through OpenAI's expanded Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP), and Walmart went further with a dedicated in-ChatGPT app supporting account linking, loyalty, and payment. If you run on Shopify, getting your catalog clean and discoverable is the practical lever here; our guide to agentic storefronts that ChatGPT can discover walks through the mechanics. If you are not on Shopify yet, you can [start a free Shopify trial](https://shopify.pxf.io/c/6318547/3797171/13624) and inherit the Catalog integration.

## ChatGPT moves into Excel and Google Sheets

On May 5, 2026, OpenAI also made ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets generally available across all plans, powered by GPT-5.5. It had been in beta in Excel since March 5 and arrived in Google Sheets on April 22 before the global launch. It lives in a sidebar inside your spreadsheet and can build, update, and explain spreadsheets directly, including large, multi-tab files with formulas, references, and assumptions.

It is available globally to Free, Go, Plus, Pro, Business, Enterprise, Edu, and K-12 users. Business, Enterprise, Edu, and K-12 customers had a free preview through June 2, 2026, after which usage follows each plan's credits and terms.

For merchants and marketers, this is a quiet productivity win. Building inventory models, reconciling ad spend, or analyzing messy sales exports in plain language, without leaving the workbook, removes a real chunk of operational friction for lean e-commerce teams.

## New realtime voice models in the API

On May 7, 2026, OpenAI introduced new realtime voice models in the API that can reason, translate, and transcribe speech for more natural and intelligent voice experiences. While less directly tied to shopping than the discovery changes, better realtime voice is the foundation for voice-driven assistants and support agents, an interface where product recommendations and conversational commerce are likely to keep expanding.
