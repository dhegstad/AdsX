International sales represent the largest untapped growth opportunity for most Shopify stores. The math is simple: if your product sells in the United States, it likely sells in 10-30 other countries — each one effectively a new market you can enter without building a new product. Shopify's international tooling has reached the point where a single store can serve 50+ countries with localized pricing, languages, and checkout experiences.

This guide covers everything you need to expand internationally on Shopify — from the technical setup to the strategic decisions that determine success.

## How Does Shopify Markets Work?

Shopify Markets is the built-in internationalization framework that allows you to manage multiple countries from a single Shopify store. Here is what it handles:

**Core features:**

1. **Multi-currency pricing:** Automatically converts prices or lets you set manual prices per market
2. **Market-specific domains:** Use subfolders (/en-gb/), subdomains (uk.store.com), or country-specific domains (store.co.uk)
3. **Language translation:** Built-in AI translation or manual translation management
4. **Duties and import taxes:** Calculate and collect duties at checkout so customers are not surprised by fees on delivery
5. **Local payment methods:** Enable market-specific payment methods (iDEAL in Netherlands, Klarna in Europe, etc.)
6. **Market-specific pricing:** Set different prices, rounding rules, and catalog availability per market
7. **SEO optimization:** Automatic hreflang tags, localized sitemaps, and market-specific metadata

**Setting up Shopify Markets:**

1. Go to Settings > Markets in your Shopify admin
2. Add markets by country or region
3. Configure currency, language, and domain strategy for each market
4. Set up duties and tax collection
5. Enable local payment methods
6. Adjust pricing strategy (auto-conversion or manual pricing)
7. Publish and test the buyer experience for each market

## How Should You Handle Currency and Pricing?

Currency handling can make or break international conversion rates. Shoppers are 40% more likely to complete a purchase when they see prices in their local currency.

**Three pricing strategies:**

1. **Automatic conversion:** Shopify converts your base prices using live exchange rates. Simplest to manage but creates awkward prices (e.g., $49.99 becomes 47.23 EUR).

2. **Automatic conversion with rounding:** Shopify converts and rounds to the nearest .99 or .00. Better customer experience with minimal effort.

3. **Manual market-specific pricing:** You set prices for each market independently. Most control but requires ongoing maintenance. Best for markets where purchasing power differs significantly (e.g., pricing lower for Southeast Asia).

**Pricing recommendations by market:**

- For developed markets (UK, EU, Australia, Japan): price at or slightly above US prices — shipping and duties justify a small premium
- For developing markets (Southeast Asia, Latin America, India): consider lower pricing to match local purchasing power
- Always include or clearly disclose duties and taxes at checkout — surprise fees at delivery cause 25-40% of international returns

## How Do You Handle Duties, Taxes, and Legal Requirements?

Duties and taxes are the number one source of international customer complaints. Getting this right is essential.

**Duties and import taxes:**

Shopify Markets can calculate and collect duties and import taxes at checkout using a delivered-duty-paid (DDP) model. This means the customer pays all fees upfront and receives the package without surprise charges. This significantly reduces delivery refusals and returns.

**VAT/GST requirements by region:**

| Region | Tax Type | Registration Threshold | Rate |
|--------|----------|----------------------|------|
| European Union | VAT | Varies by country (often 0 for non-EU sellers) | 17-27% |
| United Kingdom | VAT | GBP 0 for non-UK sellers shipping to UK | 20% |
| Australia | GST | AUD 75,000 annual sales | 10% |
| Canada | GST/HST | CAD 30,000 annual sales | 5-15% |
| Japan | Consumption Tax | JPY 10M annual sales | 10% |
| Norway | VAT | NOK 50,000 annual sales | 25% |
| Switzerland | VAT | CHF 100,000 worldwide revenue | 8.1% |

**Key legal requirements:**

- **GDPR (EU/UK):** Cookie consent, privacy policy, data processing agreements. Non-compliance fines up to 4% of global revenue.
- **Consumer protection:** EU consumers have a mandatory 14-day return right. Many other markets have similar requirements.
- **Product compliance:** Certifications like CE marking (EU), UKCA marking (UK), or country-specific product safety standards may be required.

## How Should You Handle International Shipping?

Shipping is the biggest operational challenge of international expansion. Cost, speed, and reliability vary dramatically by destination.

**Shipping strategy options:**

1. **Direct shipping from domestic warehouse:** Simplest setup. Ship international orders from your existing warehouse or 3PL. Shipping times: 7-21 days. Cost: $15-50 per package.

2. **Regional fulfillment warehouses:** Store inventory in the UK, EU, or Australia for faster local delivery. Shipping times: 2-5 days. Setup cost: Moderate. Best for markets generating $10K+ per month.

3. **Shopify Fulfillment Network or global 3PL:** Use a 3PL with international warehouses (ShipBob, Flexport, or similar). Balances speed and cost.

**International shipping cost comparison:**

**Free shipping strategy for international orders:**

Set a higher free shipping threshold for international markets (e.g., $75 domestic, $125 international) to offset higher shipping costs while still incentivizing larger orders.

## How Do You Market to International Audiences?

International marketing requires more than translating your existing ads. Consumer behavior, platform preferences, and seasonal calendars differ by market.

**Marketing localization checklist:**

1. **Paid ads:** Create market-specific campaigns with localized copy, currency, and landing pages. Do not simply duplicate US campaigns.
2. **SEO:** Target local search engines (Google.co.uk, google.de) with hreflang tags and market-specific content.
3. **Social media:** Platform preferences vary — LINE in Japan, KakaoTalk in South Korea, WhatsApp in Europe and Latin America.
4. **Email:** Localize email flows with translated content and market-appropriate offers.
5. **Influencer marketing:** Partner with local influencers who understand the market context.

## What Is the Recommended International Expansion Roadmap?

**Phase 1 (Month 1-2): English-speaking markets**
- Enable Canada, United Kingdom, Australia on Shopify Markets
- Set up multi-currency with automatic conversion and rounding
- Configure duties collection at checkout
- Launch market-specific ad campaigns on Meta and Google

**Phase 2 (Month 3-4): Western Europe**
- Add Germany, France, Netherlands
- Invest in professional translation for product pages and checkout
- Enable local payment methods (Klarna, iDEAL, SOFORT)
- Register for VAT where required

**Phase 3 (Month 5-8): High-potential markets**
- Add Japan, South Korea, or other markets where you see organic demand
- Consider regional fulfillment for markets exceeding $10K/month
- Localize marketing fully including influencer partnerships

**Phase 4 (Month 9-12): Optimization and expansion**
- Analyze performance by market and double down on winners
- Evaluate whether regional warehousing improves margins and customer experience
- Expand to additional markets based on data

International expansion multiplies your addressable market without requiring a new product, a new brand, or a new store. The infrastructure Shopify provides through Markets makes the technical barriers lower than ever. The stores that expand internationally in 2026 will have a structural advantage over those that remain domestic-only — more customers, more data, and more resilience against any single market downturn.
