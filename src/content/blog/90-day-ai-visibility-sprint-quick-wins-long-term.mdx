The 90-Day AI Visibility Sprint is a structured, week-by-week plan that takes any brand from zero AI presence to consistent citations across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini. It works because it sequences the right activities in the right order: technical foundations first, citation-worthy content second, and credibility amplification third.

Content updated within 90 days gets 78% more AI citations. Brands on 4 or more platforms are 2.8x more likely to be cited. And with 900 million weekly ChatGPT users, the audience waiting on the other side of this sprint is enormous.

This is not a theoretical framework. It is a week-by-week execution plan with specific milestones, KPIs, and deliverables for each phase.

## What Should You Do Before Day 1?

Before starting the sprint, you need a baseline. Without knowing your current AI visibility, you cannot measure progress.

### Baseline Audit (Pre-Sprint)

Test 20 queries across three AI platforms and document the results:

| Query Type | Example | Test On |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Branded query | "What is [your brand]?" | ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini |
| Category query | "Best [your category] tools" | ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini |
| Comparison query | "[Your brand] vs [competitor]" | ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini |
| Problem query | "How to solve [problem you address]" | ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini |
| Recommendation query | "Recommend a [your product type] for [use case]" | ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini |

For each query, record:
- Whether your brand is mentioned (yes/no)
- Position of mention (first, second, third, or listed)
- Accuracy of description (correct/partially correct/incorrect)
- Sentiment (positive/neutral/negative)
- Sources cited (if the platform shows sources)

This baseline becomes your measurement framework for the entire sprint.

## What Happens in Days 1-30: The Foundation Phase?

The first 30 days focus exclusively on making your brand machine-readable and establishing the technical infrastructure AI models need to identify and categorize you.

### Week 1: Technical Audit and Structured Data

**Deliverables:**
- Complete technical SEO audit focused on AI crawler accessibility
- Implement Organization schema on homepage
- Implement Product or SoftwareApplication schema on all product pages
- Verify robots.txt allows GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and PerplexityBot
- Fix any page speed issues (target under 2 seconds)

**KPI:** All structured data validated via Google Rich Results Test and Schema.org validator.

### Week 2: Entity Consistency and Platform Presence

**Deliverables:**
- Audit brand information across 10+ platforms (website, LinkedIn, Crunchbase, G2, Capterra, Google Business Profile, social profiles, industry directories)
- Correct all inconsistencies in brand name, description, founding date, and key details
- Create or claim profiles on any missing platforms
- Ensure your brand appears on at least 4 platforms with consistent information

**KPI:** Brand name, description, and key facts identical across all platforms. Target: 4+ platforms with complete, consistent profiles. Brands on 4+ platforms are 2.8x more likely to be cited.

### Week 3: Top 10 Page Optimization

**Deliverables:**
- Identify your 10 highest-traffic pages
- Add definitive opening statements to each page
- Implement FAQ schema on each page (3-5 FAQs per page)
- Add Article schema to all blog posts
- Ensure clean heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3, no skipped levels)

**KPI:** All 10 pages have definitive opening statements, FAQ schema, and proper heading hierarchy.

### Week 4: Tracking Infrastructure

**Deliverables:**
- Set up weekly AI citation monitoring (test 20+ queries across 3 platforms)
- Create a tracking spreadsheet with date, query, platform, citation status, and accuracy
- Identify your top 5 competitor queries (where competitors are cited but you are not)
- Document first citation improvements from Weeks 1-3 changes

**KPI:** Tracking system operational. Baseline comparison shows any early improvements.

### Days 1-30 Summary

| Week | Focus | Key Deliverable | Success Metric |
|------|-------|----------------|----------------|
| 1 | Technical infrastructure | Structured data live | All schemas validated |
| 2 | Entity consistency | Platform presence unified | 4+ platforms consistent |
| 3 | Page optimization | Top 10 pages updated | FAQ schema + definitive openings |
| 4 | Tracking setup | Monitoring system active | 20+ queries tracked weekly |

## What Happens in Days 31-60: The Content Phase?

The second phase is about publishing content that AI models actively want to cite. This is the most labor-intensive phase but also where the most visible results emerge.

### Week 5: Citation-Worthy Content Production Begins

**Deliverables:**
- Publish 2 long-form guides (2,000+ words each) targeting category queries
- Each guide includes: definitive opening, comparison table, original data or analysis, FAQ schema
- Implement internal linking from existing pages to new content
- Begin building a content calendar for Weeks 6-8

**Content requirements per piece:**
- Definitive opening statement (quotable, specific, data-backed)
- At least 1 comparison table with specific data points
- 3-5 FAQ pairs with schema markup
- Question-based H2 headers
- Specific numbers and statistics throughout

### Week 6: Reddit Presence Launch

**Deliverables:**
- Identify 3-5 subreddits where your target audience asks questions about your category
- Create authentic Reddit accounts (or use existing ones) for team members
- Contribute 5+ helpful, detailed answers to relevant questions
- Do not promote your brand directly — provide genuine expertise
- Document threads where your brand or category is discussed

**KPI:** 5+ quality Reddit contributions with positive engagement (upvotes, follow-up questions).

### Week 7: Content Acceleration

**Deliverables:**
- Publish 3 additional content pieces (mix of guides, comparisons, and data-driven posts)
- Target long-tail queries (7+ words) — these have 61.9% higher chance of triggering AI Overviews
- Each piece follows the citation-worthy content template from Week 5
- Begin outreach to industry contacts for expert quotes and data contributions

**KPI:** 5 total new content pieces published. At least 2 target 7+ word long-tail queries.

### Week 8: Digital PR Outreach Begins

**Deliverables:**
- Create a media list of 20+ industry publications, analysts, and bloggers
- Draft 3 pitch angles based on your original data or unique expertise
- Send first round of outreach (10+ personalized pitches)
- Sign up for journalist query services (HARO, Qwoted, Connectively)
- Respond to at least 5 journalist queries with expert commentary

**KPI:** 10+ outreach pitches sent. 5+ journalist queries answered. 2+ published mentions targeted.

### Days 31-60 Summary

| Week | Focus | Key Deliverable | Success Metric |
|------|-------|----------------|----------------|
| 5 | Content production | 2 long-form guides published | Citation-worthy format confirmed |
| 6 | Reddit presence | 5+ community contributions | Positive engagement metrics |
| 7 | Content acceleration | 3 more pieces published | 5 total new pieces live |
| 8 | Digital PR launch | 10+ outreach pitches sent | First external mentions targeted |

## What Happens in Days 61-90: The Amplification Phase?

The final phase is about earning the external validation that accounts for 91% of AI citations. Your technical foundation is solid. Your content is citation-worthy. Now you need the third-party signals that push AI models to cite you over competitors.

### Week 9: Third-Party Mention Acceleration

**Deliverables:**
- Follow up on all Week 8 outreach (second touch)
- Send 10+ new outreach pitches to fresh contacts
- Publish a contributed article or guest post in an industry publication
- Earn at least 2 new third-party mentions (any format: quote, mention, feature, link)

**KPI:** 2+ new third-party mentions published. Total referring domain growth measurable.

### Week 10: Expert Roundup Publication

**Deliverables:**
- Publish an expert roundup featuring 10+ industry voices on a trending topic
- Each expert provides a unique quote or insight with name and credentials
- Promote the roundup to all featured experts (they share with their audiences)
- Implement Article and Person schema for each expert
- Pitch the roundup results to 5+ publications as newsworthy data

**KPI:** Expert roundup published with 10+ contributors. Shared by 5+ contributors.

### Week 11: Original Data Publication

**Deliverables:**
- Publish original research, survey results, or proprietary data analysis
- Create a dedicated landing page with clear methodology and key findings
- Draft a press release or media pitch around the key findings
- Send to 15+ journalists, analysts, and industry contacts
- Create social media assets highlighting key statistics

**KPI:** Original data piece published. 15+ media pitches sent. Data cited in at least 1 external source.

### Week 12: Measurement and Planning

**Deliverables:**
- Conduct full AI citation audit (same 20+ queries from pre-sprint baseline)
- Compare Day 90 results to Day 0 baseline
- Document all improvements: new citations, accuracy improvements, platform coverage
- Identify the top-performing content pieces and tactics
- Create a 90-day maintenance plan for ongoing citation growth

**KPI:** Measurable improvement in citation frequency and accuracy across all three AI platforms.

### Days 61-90 Summary

| Week | Focus | Key Deliverable | Success Metric |
|------|-------|----------------|----------------|
| 9 | Third-party mentions | 2+ new mentions earned | External validation growing |
| 10 | Expert roundup | 10+ expert roundup published | Shared by 5+ contributors |
| 11 | Original data | Proprietary research published | Cited by 1+ external source |
| 12 | Measurement | Full audit vs. baseline | Measurable citation improvement |

## What KPIs Should You Track Weekly?

Consistent measurement is the difference between a sprint and a guess. Track these KPIs every week:

| KPI | How to Measure | Target by Day 90 |
|-----|---------------|-----------------|
| Citation frequency | Test 20+ queries across 3 platforms | 30%+ mention rate |
| Citation accuracy | Review AI descriptions of your brand | 90%+ accuracy |
| Platform coverage | Track which AI platforms cite you | 3+ platforms |
| Query breadth | Count unique queries that trigger citations | 10+ unique queries |
| Third-party mentions | Monitor new external mentions | 5+ new mentions |
| Content pieces published | Count new citation-worthy content | 8-12 pieces |
| Reddit contributions | Track helpful community posts | 15+ contributions |
| Referring domains | Monitor via Ahrefs or similar | Measurable growth |

## What Are Realistic Timeline Expectations?

Not every brand will achieve the same results in 90 days. Your starting point, industry, and competition level all affect the timeline.

**Fastest results (30-45 days):** Brands with existing domain authority (DR 40+) and significant content that just needs optimization. Adding structured data and definitive statements to existing high-quality content can trigger citations within weeks.

**Moderate results (45-75 days):** Brands with moderate domain authority (DR 20-40) that need both content creation and technical optimization. The content phase (Days 31-60) is where these brands see their first citations.

**Slower results (75-90+ days):** New brands or brands in highly competitive categories. These brands need the full amplification phase to build sufficient third-party validation for AI citation. Domains with 32,000+ referring domains are 3.5x more likely to be cited by ChatGPT — newer brands need to compensate with exceptional content quality and targeted PR.

Regardless of starting point, the 78% citation boost from content updated within 90 days applies universally. The sprint's 90-day structure is specifically designed to keep all content within this freshness window.

## What Should You Do After Day 90?

The sprint ends, but AI visibility does not. After Day 90, transition to a sustainable maintenance cadence:

**Monthly requirements:**
- Publish 4-8 new citation-worthy content pieces
- Refresh 4-6 existing pieces (maintain the 90-day freshness cycle)
- Contribute 8-12 Reddit answers
- Send 10-15 digital PR pitches
- Track 20+ queries weekly across all AI platforms

**Quarterly requirements:**
- Publish 1 original data piece or expert roundup
- Conduct full citation audit with baseline comparison
- Update all structured data for accuracy
- Review and update entity consistency across all platforms

The compounding effect of AI visibility means the maintenance phase requires less effort per citation than the sprint. Your existing content, third-party mentions, and platform presence create a flywheel that generates citations with decreasing marginal effort.

SaaS brands that complete this sprint and maintain the cadence report 6x stronger conversion from AI search compared to Google organic. The 90-day sprint is the starting point — the compounding returns are what make it transformative.
