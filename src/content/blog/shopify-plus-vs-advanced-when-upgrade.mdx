The jump from [Shopify](https://shopify.pxf.io/c/6318547/3797171/13624) Advanced to Shopify Plus is not just a plan upgrade. It is a fundamental shift in how your e-commerce operation works. Advanced costs $399 per month. Plus starts at $2,300 per month. That price difference of over $22,000 per year demands a clear understanding of what Plus delivers and whether those capabilities generate enough return to justify the investment.

This guide provides a thorough comparison of both plans, breaks down the specific features that differentiate Plus from Advanced, and gives you a concrete ROI framework to determine when upgrading makes financial sense for your business. If you are approaching the revenue levels where Plus becomes a consideration, this analysis will help you time the upgrade correctly rather than moving too early or too late.

## Pricing Breakdown: What You Pay on Each Plan

Understanding the full cost picture requires looking beyond the monthly subscription fee.

### Shopify Advanced Pricing

- **Monthly subscription**: $399/month (or $299/month on annual billing)
- **Credit card processing (online)**: 2.4% + $0.30 per transaction
- **Credit card processing (in-person)**: 2.4% + $0.10 per transaction
- **Third-party gateway fee**: 0.5% per transaction
- **Staff accounts**: 15 included
- **Inventory locations**: Up to 10
- **Reports**: Custom report builder included
- **Annual cost (subscription only)**: $3,588 (annual billing)

### Shopify Plus Pricing

- **Monthly subscription**: Starting at $2,300/month (negotiable)
- **Credit card processing (online)**: Starting at 2.15% + $0.30 per transaction
- **Credit card processing (in-person)**: 2.4% + $0.10 per transaction
- **Third-party gateway fee**: 0.2% per transaction
- **Staff accounts**: Unlimited
- **Inventory locations**: Up to 200
- **Reports**: Custom report builder plus exclusive Plus reports
- **Annual cost (subscription only)**: Starting at $27,600

### The Revenue-Based Pricing Model on Plus

Shopify Plus uses a variable pricing structure for merchants with very high GMV. Once your revenue exceeds a certain threshold (typically around $800,000 per month), the subscription shifts from a flat fee to a percentage of revenue, usually 0.25% of GMV. This means a store doing $2 million per month would pay $5,000 per month in subscription fees rather than the base $2,300.

This variable pricing is negotiable and forms a key part of the contract discussion when you first move to Plus.

## Feature Comparison: What Plus Adds Over Advanced

### Checkout Customization

This is the most impactful difference between the two plans and the primary reason most merchants upgrade.

**On Advanced**: You get Shopify's standard checkout with limited customization. You can add your logo, change colors, and adjust basic layout settings. But you cannot add custom fields, modify the checkout flow, insert upsell offers, apply conditional logic, or significantly alter the checkout experience.

**On Plus**: Checkout extensibility gives you deep control over the checkout experience. Specific capabilities include:

- **Custom checkout UI extensions**: Add custom fields (gift messages, delivery date selectors, special instructions), loyalty program information, and trust badges directly in the checkout flow
- **Post-purchase upsells**: Present upsell offers after the customer completes their purchase but before the thank-you page. These one-click upsells typically convert at 5-15% and can increase average order value by 10-20%
- **Checkout branding**: Full control over checkout appearance including custom fonts, layout modifications, and branded elements
- **Conditional checkout logic**: Show or hide checkout sections based on cart contents, customer tags, or other variables

The conversion rate impact of checkout customization is substantial. Merchants who optimize their Plus checkout typically see 5-15% improvement in checkout completion rates. On a store doing $2 million per year, a 10% improvement in checkout conversion translates to $200,000 in additional revenue.

### Shopify Functions (Replacing Scripts)

Shopify Functions are backend customizations that modify how discounts, shipping, and payments work at a fundamental level.

**On Advanced**: You use standard discount codes and Shopify's built-in shipping rules. Customization is limited to what the admin interface provides.

**On Plus**: Functions enable:

- **Custom discount logic**: Buy X get Y free, tiered discounts based on cart value or customer segment, automatic bundle pricing, and discount combinations that are not possible with standard codes
- **Custom shipping logic**: Dynamic shipping rates based on product attributes, customer location, order history, or any other variable. For example, free shipping for VIP customers only, or different rates for oversized versus standard items
- **Custom payment logic**: Control which payment methods are available based on order value, customer tag, product type, or geographic location. You can hide COD for international orders or require wire transfer above a certain order value

These customizations enable pricing and fulfillment strategies that are impossible on lower plans and can significantly impact customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

### Shopify Flow (Advanced Automation)

While Shopify Flow is available on the standard Shopify plan and above, the Plus version offers more powerful capabilities and higher execution limits.

**Flow capabilities on Plus include:**

- **Automated order management**: Tag high-value orders for priority fulfillment, automatically hold suspicious orders for review, route orders to different warehouses based on customer location
- **Customer segmentation**: Automatically tag customers based on purchase behavior, lifetime value, or engagement metrics
- **Inventory management**: Trigger reorder notifications, automatically hide out-of-stock products, publish products when inventory arrives
- **Marketing automation**: Trigger personalized campaigns based on specific customer actions or order characteristics
- **Fraud prevention**: Create custom fraud rules that automatically cancel or hold orders matching specific risk patterns

The automation capabilities on Plus can replace 1-2 full-time employees for stores with complex operational workflows, saving $50,000-100,000 per year in labor costs.

### Multi-Store Management

**On Advanced**: You can run one store per plan. If you need separate stores for different regions, brands, or B2B/B2C segments, each requires a separate Shopify subscription.

**On Plus**: You get up to 9 additional expansion stores included in your Plus subscription. These expansion stores have some limitations compared to your primary store but are fully functional for selling. This is critical for:

- **International expansion**: Separate stores for different markets with local currencies, languages, and product assortments
- **B2B wholesale**: A dedicated wholesale store with custom pricing for business customers
- **Brand portfolio**: Multiple brands under one administrative umbrella
- **Flash sale or outlet stores**: Temporary or permanent clearance destinations

At $399 per month per Advanced store, running 3-4 separate stores would cost $1,200-1,600 per month just in subscriptions. On Plus, these expansion stores are included, which can make Plus cost-neutral or even cheaper than running multiple Advanced plans.

### Dedicated Support and Account Management

**On Advanced**: You get 24/7 support through Shopify's standard channels (chat, email, phone). Response times vary, and you do not have a dedicated point of contact.

**On Plus**: You receive:

- **Merchant Success Manager**: A dedicated Shopify contact who understands your business and provides proactive strategic guidance
- **Launch Manager**: For new Plus merchants, a dedicated resource who guides you through the migration and launch process
- **Priority support**: Faster response times and access to senior technical support staff
- **Plus Partner ecosystem**: Access to vetted Plus-certified agencies and developers

For businesses where downtime or technical issues have significant revenue impact, the dedicated support alone can justify a portion of the Plus cost. A 2-hour faster resolution on a checkout bug during a major sale could save tens of thousands of dollars.

### API and Technical Capabilities

**On Advanced**: API call limits are generous but can be constraining for stores with complex custom integrations or high-volume headless commerce implementations.

**On Plus**: You get significantly higher API rate limits, which is essential for:

- Headless commerce architectures using Shopify as a backend
- Complex ERP, PIM, or OMS integrations that require frequent data synchronization
- High-traffic stores where multiple systems need to query the Shopify API simultaneously
- Custom applications that process large volumes of orders, products, or customers

## When Does Upgrading to Plus Make Financial Sense?

The decision to upgrade should be based on a concrete ROI calculation, not a general feeling that your store has "outgrown" Advanced.

### The Transaction Fee Savings Calculation

The credit card processing rate difference between Advanced (2.4%) and Plus (2.15%) is 0.25 percentage points. Calculate your annual savings:

**Annual online revenue x 0.0025 = Annual transaction fee savings**

Examples:
- $1 million annual revenue: $2,500 in savings
- $2 million: $5,000
- $5 million: $12,500
- $10 million: $25,000

The subscription cost difference is approximately $22,800 per year (comparing $2,300/month Plus to $299/month Advanced annual). Based on transaction fees alone, Plus breaks even at approximately $9.1 million in annual revenue.

However, transaction fees are only one component of the ROI calculation.

### The Checkout Customization ROI

If checkout customization improves your conversion rate by just 5-10%, the revenue impact can be substantial:

**Current monthly revenue / Current checkout conversion rate x Improved conversion rate = New monthly revenue**

Example: A store doing $200,000 per month with a 65% checkout completion rate that improves to 72% through Plus checkout customization:

$200,000 / 0.65 x 0.72 = $221,538

That is $21,538 per month or $258,456 per year in additional revenue from checkout optimization alone. This single benefit far exceeds the cost difference between the plans.

### The Automation Savings

Quantify the labor hours that Shopify Flow automation would save:

- Automated order tagging and routing: 10-15 hours per week
- Automated customer segmentation: 3-5 hours per week
- Automated inventory management: 5-8 hours per week
- Automated fraud review: 5-10 hours per week

At a fully loaded labor cost of $25-40 per hour, automating 25-35 hours per week saves $32,500-72,800 per year.

### The Multi-Store Value

If you currently need or plan to need multiple stores, the expansion stores included with Plus provide direct cost savings:

- 2 expansion stores replace $7,176/year in Advanced subscriptions (2 x $299/month x 12)
- 4 expansion stores replace $14,352/year
- 9 expansion stores replace $32,292/year

### Total ROI Framework

Add up all the potential benefits:

| Benefit | Annual Value |
|---------|-------------|
| Transaction fee savings | $2,500-25,000 |
| Checkout conversion improvement | $50,000-250,000+ |
| Automation labor savings | $32,500-72,800 |
| Multi-store value | $0-32,292 |
| **Total potential benefit** | **$85,000-380,000+** |
| **Annual cost of upgrade** | **$22,800** |
| **ROI** | **3.7x-16.7x** |

This calculation shows why the upgrade to Plus typically makes sense for merchants doing $1-2 million or more in annual revenue. The checkout customization and automation benefits alone can dwarf the subscription cost difference.

## Signs You Are Ready to Upgrade

Beyond the financial calculation, several operational signals indicate that Plus would benefit your business.

### Your Checkout Is Losing Sales

If your analytics show a high cart-to-checkout drop-off rate (above 35%) and you have exhausted the customization options available on Advanced, Plus checkout extensibility is likely the solution. Common checkout friction points that Plus can address include missing trust signals at checkout, no post-purchase upsell capability, inability to add gift messaging or delivery date selection, and checkout branding that feels disconnected from your store experience.

### Your Team Spends Too Much Time on Manual Tasks

If your operations team manually tags orders, segments customers, manages inventory visibility, or handles other repetitive tasks that could be automated, Shopify Flow on Plus can eliminate these manual workflows. Track the hours your team spends on tasks that follow predictable rules (if this, then that). If the total exceeds 20 hours per week, the automation ROI is likely positive.

### You Need Multiple Storefronts

If you are operating or planning separate stores for B2B wholesale, international markets, or multiple brands, Plus expansion stores provide significant value. Running these stores under a single Plus subscription is more cost-effective and operationally simpler than managing multiple separate Advanced subscriptions.

### You Are Hitting API Limits

If your custom integrations experience rate limiting, your headless commerce architecture is constrained by API call limits, or your ERP synchronization falls behind during high-traffic periods, Plus's higher API limits solve these technical bottlenecks.

### Your Support Needs Exceed Standard Channels

If you regularly encounter issues that require escalation beyond standard support, wait too long for resolutions on critical issues, or need proactive strategic guidance from someone who knows your business, the dedicated Merchant Success Manager on Plus addresses these needs.

## The Upgrade Process: What to Expect

### Initial Contact and Scoping

Contact Shopify's Plus sales team or work with a Shopify Plus partner agency. The initial conversation covers your current revenue, growth trajectory, specific needs and pain points, timeline expectations, and budget considerations. Shopify will prepare a custom proposal based on your situation.

### Contract Negotiation

Plus contracts are typically 1-3 years. Key negotiation points include:

- **Monthly fee**: The $2,300 starting price is negotiable, especially for longer commitments
- **Variable rate threshold**: The GMV level at which variable pricing kicks in
- **Variable rate percentage**: The percentage charged above the threshold
- **Expansion stores**: How many are included in your contract
- **Migration support**: Level of technical assistance during migration
- **Payment terms**: Monthly versus quarterly billing

### Migration Planning

Migrating from Advanced to Plus is relatively straightforward because you are staying within the Shopify ecosystem. The main migration tasks include:

- Implementing checkout customizations
- Setting up Shopify Flow automations
- Configuring expansion stores (if needed)
- Training your team on Plus-specific features
- Connecting any new integrations that leverage Plus capabilities
- Testing all changes thoroughly before going live

Most Advanced-to-Plus migrations take 4-8 weeks, depending on the complexity of checkout customizations and automation workflows.

### Post-Migration Optimization

After migrating, focus on realizing the ROI that justified the upgrade:

**Month 1**: Implement basic checkout customizations and monitor conversion rate changes. Set up core Shopify Flow automations for order management and customer segmentation.

**Month 2**: Analyze checkout data and iterate on customizations. Add post-purchase upsell offers and measure their impact on AOV. Expand Flow automations to cover more operational workflows.

**Month 3**: Review the full ROI picture. Compare pre-migration and post-migration metrics for checkout conversion rate, average order value, operational labor hours, and customer satisfaction scores.

## When NOT to Upgrade to Plus

Plus is not right for every growing store. Avoid upgrading if:

### Your Revenue Does Not Support It

If your annual revenue is under $500,000, the subscription cost of Plus will consume too large a percentage of your revenue. Focus on growth strategies that increase revenue to the $1-2 million range before considering the upgrade.

### You Have Not Optimized Advanced

If you have not fully utilized the features available on Advanced (custom reports, calculated shipping, international pricing), upgrading to Plus adds capabilities you may not be ready to use. Maximize your current plan before adding more complexity.

### Your Team Cannot Implement Plus Features

Plus features require implementation. Checkout customization needs development resources, Flow automations need someone to build and maintain them, and expansion stores need staff to manage them. If you do not have the internal team or budget for a Plus partner agency, the features will go unused and the investment will not generate returns.

### Your Growth Is Not Sustainable

If your current revenue spike is seasonal or driven by unsustainable factors, locking into a multi-year Plus contract at $2,300+ per month is risky. Ensure your revenue trend is sustainable before committing.

## Alternatives to Consider Before Upgrading

### Shopify Advanced with Apps

Many Plus features can be approximated with Advanced plan apps:

- **Checkout upsells**: Apps like ReConvert or AfterSell provide post-purchase upsell functionality on lower plans (though with some limitations compared to Plus checkout extensibility)
- **Automation**: Zapier, Make.com, or other automation platforms can handle many workflow automations that Flow covers on Plus
- **B2B wholesale**: Apps like Wholesale Club or B2B Handshake provide wholesale functionality without a separate store

The combined cost of these apps is typically $100-300 per month, far less than the Plus upgrade.

### Custom Development on Advanced

Some Plus benefits can be achieved through custom development on Advanced:

- Advanced discount logic through custom apps
- Custom shipping integrations
- ERP and system integrations with optimized API usage

Custom development has upfront costs but no ongoing subscription premium.

### Shopify Plus Trial Period

Some Shopify Plus representatives offer trial periods or proof-of-concept arrangements for qualifying merchants. Ask about this option during the sales process. A trial lets you verify the ROI before committing to a full contract.

## Making the Decision: A Step-by-Step Framework

1. **Calculate your total potential savings** from lower transaction fees based on your actual revenue
2. **Estimate your checkout conversion improvement** based on industry benchmarks and your current conversion rate
3. **Quantify your automation savings** by tracking the hours your team spends on automatable tasks
4. **Add multi-store value** if you need or plan multiple storefronts
5. **Sum the total annual benefit** and compare it to the $22,800+ annual cost difference
6. **If the ROI exceeds 2x**, the upgrade is likely justified
7. **If the ROI is between 1-2x**, consider starting with Advanced plan apps and revisiting in 6-12 months
8. **If the ROI is below 1x**, stay on Advanced and focus on growing revenue
