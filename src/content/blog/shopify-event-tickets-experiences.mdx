## Why Event Businesses Choose Shopify Over Traditional Ticketing Platforms

Traditional ticketing platforms like Eventbrite, Ticketmaster, and Universe have dominated the market for years, but they come with significant costs and limitations that forward-thinking event organizers are working to escape.

### The True Cost of Traditional Ticketing Platforms

Consider the typical fee structure of major ticketing platforms:

| Platform | Service Fee | Payment Processing | Per-Ticket Minimum |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Eventbrite | 3.7% + $1.79 | Included | $1.79 |
| Ticketmaster | 10-25% | Included | Varies |
| Universe | 2.5% + $0.99 | 2.9% + $0.30 | $1.29 |
| Brown Paper Tickets | 5% + $0.99 | Included | $0.99 |

For a $50 ticket on Eventbrite, you're paying approximately $3.64 in fees per ticket. Sell 500 tickets and you've lost $1,820 to platform fees alone.

### The Shopify Advantage for Events

With Shopify, your cost structure changes dramatically:

- **Shopify Basic**: $29/month flat fee
- **Payment processing**: 2.9% + $0.30 (Shopify Payments)
- **Ticketing app**: $0-50/month or $0.50-2.00 per ticket

For that same $50 ticket, you're paying approximately $1.75 in processing (and potentially a small app fee). The savings compound quickly as your event volume grows.

Beyond cost savings, Shopify offers:

**Complete branding control**: Your tickets come from your domain, your emails, your brand experience—not a third-party platform that promotes their other events.

**Customer data ownership**: Every ticket buyer enters your ecosystem. You own the email addresses, purchase history, and relationship—essential for marketing future events.

**Unified commerce**: Sell tickets alongside merchandise, food and beverage add-ons, parking passes, and VIP upgrades in a single checkout experience.

**Flexibility**: Build exactly the ticketing experience you need, from simple workshop registrations to complex multi-day festival passes.

## Setting Up Your Shopify Store for Event Ticketing

Creating an effective event ticketing operation on Shopify requires the right foundation. Here's how to build it.

### Choosing Your Shopify Plan

For most event businesses, **Shopify Basic** ($29/month) provides everything you need to start:

- Unlimited products (tickets, add-ons, merchandise)
- Staff accounts for your team
- SSL certificate for secure transactions
- 24/7 support
- Standard payment processing (2.9% + $0.30)

As your event business grows, **Shopify** ($79/month) and **Advanced Shopify** ($299/month) offer lower transaction fees and advanced reporting that become cost-effective at higher volumes.

The right apps transform Shopify from an e-commerce platform into a full-featured ticketing system.

### Configuring Your Ticket Products

Creating effective ticket products requires attention to detail:

**Product Titles**: Use clear, descriptive titles that include the event name and ticket type:
- "Summer Music Festival 2026 - General Admission"
- "Cooking Masterclass with Chef Maria - Saturday Session"
- "VIP Concert Package - Front Row + Meet & Greet"

**Product Descriptions**: Include essential details:
- Event date, time, and duration
- Venue name and address
- What's included (access areas, amenities, food/drink)
- Age restrictions or requirements
- Refund and exchange policies
- Important instructions (parking, what to bring)

**Product Variants**: Use variants for different ticket types:
- General Admission vs VIP
- Adult vs Child vs Senior
- Single Day vs Weekend Pass
- Early Bird vs Regular vs Late pricing

**Inventory Management**: Set accurate inventory counts to enforce capacity limits. Enable "Track quantity" and set your maximum attendee count.

## Managing Capacity and Availability

Effective capacity management prevents overselling and ensures positive attendee experiences.

### Inventory-Based Capacity Control

Shopify's built-in inventory system provides basic capacity management:

1. Create your ticket product with variants for each ticket type
2. Set the inventory quantity to your capacity limit
3. Enable "Track quantity" in inventory settings
4. Optionally enable "Continue selling when out of stock" for waitlist functionality

### Time-Slot Based Capacity

For events with multiple sessions or time slots, ticketing apps provide advanced capacity management:

- Set capacity per time slot independently
- Display real-time availability to customers
- Automatically close sold-out sessions
- Allow waitlist signups for popular times
- Send notifications when spots open up

### Dynamic Capacity Strategies

Sophisticated event operators use dynamic capacity management:

**Tiered release**: Release tickets in waves to create urgency and manage demand:
- Early Bird (limited quantity at lower price)
- General Sale (main allocation)
- Final Release (last-minute tickets at premium)

**Capacity holds**: Reserve capacity for:
- Sponsors and partners
- Media and press
- Contingency for customer service issues
- Day-of box office sales

**Real-time adjustments**: Some ticketing apps allow capacity changes based on:
- Weather conditions for outdoor events
- Staff availability for experience-based activities
- Venue configurations for flexible spaces

## Check-In Systems and Day-of Operations

Smooth check-in operations set the tone for attendee experience and protect against fraud.

### QR Code and Barcode Ticketing

Modern Shopify ticketing apps generate unique codes for each ticket:

**QR codes** are the current standard:
- Scan quickly with any smartphone camera
- Contain encrypted ticket information
- Work in various lighting conditions
- Can include backup text codes

**Ticket delivery methods**:
- Email with PDF attachment
- Email with mobile-friendly web link
- Apple Wallet / Google Wallet integration
- SMS for critical delivery assurance

### Mobile Check-In Apps

Most ticketing apps include companion check-in apps for staff:

**Core functionality**:
- Scan QR codes using device camera
- Display attendee name and ticket details
- Mark tickets as checked in
- Prevent duplicate entry attempts
- Work offline with sync when connected

**Advanced features**:
- Search attendees by name or confirmation number
- Manual check-in for edge cases
- Add notes to attendee records
- Track check-in statistics in real-time
- Multiple device synchronization

### Check-In Hardware Options

For high-volume events, dedicated hardware improves efficiency:

**Smartphones and tablets**: Most cost-effective for small to medium events. Use protective cases and ensure sufficient battery packs.

**Dedicated barcode scanners**: Faster scanning for high-volume entry points. Bluetooth scanners connect to tablets running your check-in app.

**Kiosk systems**: Self-service stations for attendee check-in, badge printing, or will-call pickup.

**Wristband printers**: For multi-day events or venues where re-entry is common.

### Day-of Operations Best Practices

Prepare your team and systems for smooth event execution:

**Pre-event checklist**:
- Test check-in app on all devices
- Ensure all devices are charged and have backup power
- Download attendee list for offline access
- Brief staff on check-in procedures and edge cases
- Prepare printed backup lists as ultimate failsafe

**Entry point setup**:
- Clear signage directing attendees
- Separate lines for will-call vs mobile tickets
- Dedicated line for accessibility needs
- Staff positions for scanning, bag check, and customer service

**Real-time monitoring**:
- Dashboard showing check-in counts
- Alerts for unusual patterns (same ticket scanned multiple times)
- Communication channel between entry points and operations

## Handling Refunds, Exchanges, and Cancellations

Clear policies and efficient processes protect both your business and customer relationships.

### Setting Refund Policies

Define policies clearly on product pages and at checkout:

**Common policy structures**:
- Full refund up to 30 days before event
- 50% refund 7-30 days before event
- No refunds within 7 days (but exchanges allowed)
- No refunds for VIP or special experiences

**Policy considerations**:
- Credit toward future events vs cash refunds
- Ticket transfer or resale options
- Weather-related cancellation provisions
- Illness and emergency provisions

### Processing Refunds in Shopify

Shopify makes refund processing straightforward:

1. Navigate to the order in Shopify Admin
2. Click "Refund" and enter the amount
3. Choose to restock inventory (restore ticket availability)
4. Add internal notes about the refund reason
5. Notify customer via email

For partial refunds (keeping processing fees or cancellation fee), adjust the refund amount accordingly.

### Event Cancellation Procedures

If you must cancel an event:

1. **Stop ticket sales immediately** - Update products to unavailable
2. **Notify all ticket holders** - Email with clear explanation and refund timeline
3. **Process refunds systematically** - Export order list, process refunds in batches
4. **Communicate rescheduling if applicable** - Offer ticket holders first access to new dates
5. **Document everything** - Maintain records for accounting and potential disputes

### Insurance Considerations

Protect your business with appropriate coverage:

- Event cancellation insurance
- Liability insurance for attendees
- Weather insurance for outdoor events
- Non-appearance insurance for featured performers
