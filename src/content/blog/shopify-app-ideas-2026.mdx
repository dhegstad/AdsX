The Shopify app ideas worth building in 2026 cluster around three shifts: agentic commerce arriving at checkout, the Functions migration that retired Scripts on June 30, and merchants drowning in catalog and feed data work that AI surfaces now read directly. Here are 15 concrete ideas, each with the merchant pain, competition level, and monetization path.

![Team sketching Shopify app concepts on a whiteboard](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1553877522-43269d4ea984?w=1200&q=80)

## How to Read This List

An idea list is only useful if it is honest about difficulty, so each entry below names the pain, the reason the timing is now, the competitive picture, and how the app makes money. The scoring table at the end compares all 15 on pain severity, competition, and build effort. The scores are our judgment calls from working with Shopify merchants and app founders, not measurements; treat them as a starting grid for your own validation, not a verdict.

One theme runs through the list. Building got cheap, so the ideas that hold up are the ones with a structural moat: proprietary data accumulation, a migration tailwind, or a surface incumbents are slow to adopt.

## The 15 Ideas

### 1. Agentic checkout support layer

**Pain:** AI shopping agents are beginning to place orders on shoppers' behalf, and when an agent-driven order fails (payment mismatch, address quirks, out-of-stock race), the merchant sees a lost sale with no human to email. **Why now:** Shopify is investing directly in [agentic commerce infrastructure](/blog/agentic-commerce-shopify-future), and early merchants have no tooling to observe or troubleshoot agent traffic. **Competition:** Near zero today, which cuts both ways; validate volume before betting on it. **Monetization:** Monthly subscription tiered by order volume, with an enterprise tier for Plus brands piloting agent channels. Our guide to [agentic checkout optimization](/blog/agentic-checkout-optimization-shopify) covers the merchant-side problems such an app would absorb.

### 2. Catalog enrichment for AI shopping surfaces

**Pain:** AI assistants recommend products by reading structured data, and most merchant catalogs are a mess of missing attributes, inconsistent variants, and empty metafields. **Why now:** Shopify's product model got richer and AI surfaces now consume it directly; the [Product Catalog API opens a whole class of builds](/blog/things-to-build-shopify-product-catalog-api). **Competition:** Low to moderate. Plenty of bulk editors exist; few apps do opinionated enrichment with AI-assisted attribute extraction and human review queues. **Monetization:** Usage-based per SKU enriched, with a subscription floor. The data-quality audit is a natural free tier that converts.

### 3. Shopping feed doctor with LLM attribute extraction

**Pain:** Google, Meta, and TikTok feeds get disapproved or underperform because titles, categories, and attributes are wrong, and merchants find out via cryptic platform errors. **Why now:** Feed quality now also determines visibility in AI-driven shopping results, raising the cost of a sloppy feed. **Competition:** High at the commodity feed-sync level (established players own the plumbing), low at the diagnosis-and-fix level. **Monetization:** Subscription tiered by SKU count. The wedge is the report that says exactly which 40 products are bleeding spend and why, not another sync pipeline.

### 4. No-code Shopify Functions builder

**Pain:** Scripts died on June 30, 2026, and the replacement requires writing code that compiles to WebAssembly. Merchants who had a working tiered-discount Script now need a developer for changes they used to make themselves. **Why now:** The [Scripts-to-Functions migration](/blog/shopify-scripts-functions-migration-june-30-2026) created a dated, urgent, budget-holding customer base. **Competition:** Moderate and rising fast; several discount-function apps launched around the deadline. Shipping, payment, and validation functions are less contested than discounts. **Monetization:** Subscription by function count or order volume. Migration urgency supports higher price points than greenfield tools.

### 5. Checkout UI extension conversion suite

**Pain:** Plus merchants want upsells, trust badges, and custom fields in checkout, but every added block risks slowing the one page where speed is revenue. **Why now:** Checkout extensibility is the only sanctioned customization path since checkout.liquid retired, and quality bars now include checkout performance. **Competition:** High. The wedge is a built-in performance budget: the app refuses to ship a configuration that degrades checkout speed, and shows the measurement. **Monetization:** Percentage of incremental upsell revenue or flat tiers; the performance-guardrail positioning justifies premium pricing against cheaper competitors.

### 6. PDP content QA (not generation)

**Pain:** Merchants used AI to write thousands of product descriptions, and now the catalog contradicts itself: sizing claims differ between variants, translated pages drift from the source, banned claims slip into supplements copy. **Why now:** Generation is commoditized; accountability isn't. Regulators and marketplaces both punish inaccurate claims. **Competition:** Low. Nearly every AI content app writes; almost none audit. **Monetization:** Subscription by catalog size, with compliance-focused tiers for regulated verticals (supplements, cosmetics, CBD). Sells well to the merchant who already got burned once.

### 7. Zero-party data micro-surveys beyond attribution

**Pain:** Merchants know post-purchase attribution surveys, but the same one-question mechanic answers sizing confidence, gifting intent, restock timing, and churn risk, and nobody asks. **Why now:** Checkout and customer-account extensions give surveys native placements that old popup tools never had. **Competition:** Moderate in attribution specifically, low for the adjacent questions. **Monetization:** Free entry tier to win placement, paid tiers for branching logic, integrations, and analysis. The honest caveat: attribution incumbents can expand sideways into this, so speed and niche focus matter.

### 8. Returns abuse and policy engine

**Pain:** Return fraud (wardrobing, empty-box claims, serial refunders) eats margin, and blanket policy tightening punishes good customers to stop bad ones. **Why now:** Margin pressure makes returns the biggest untouched leak at most brands, and order-history signals are rich enough to score risk per request. **Competition:** Moderate. Returns logistics apps are established; risk-scoring layers are not. **Monetization:** Subscription plus per-return pricing at volume. Measurable savings make the renewal conversation easy, which is rare and valuable.

### 9. Inventory forecasting for sub-$5M merchants

**Pain:** Small merchants forecast in spreadsheets, then either stock out of winners or sit on dead inventory, and enterprise forecasting tools start at prices they cannot justify. **Why now:** The forecasting math hasn't changed, but AI-assisted development finally makes it viable to serve this segment at a price it can pay. **Competition:** Moderate; most incumbents chase upmarket. **Monetization:** Flat monthly tiers by SKU count, deliberately under the enterprise floor. Churn risk is real when a merchant's volume is too low for forecasts to beat gut feel, so gate the free trial by order volume.

### 10. Metaobject-powered programmatic landing pages

**Pain:** Merchants want SEO landing pages at scale (use-case pages, comparison pages, ingredient pages) and currently hack them together in page builders one at a time. **Why now:** Metaobjects made structured content native to Shopify, but the authoring experience for non-developers barely exists. **Competition:** High among generic page builders, low for structured programmatic generation with SEO guardrails. **Monetization:** Subscription by published page count. The risk to respect: pages without real content get sites demoted, so the app must enforce quality, not just scale.

### 11. Native B2B workflow add-ons

**Pain:** Shopify's native B2B handles catalogs and price lists, but merchants still bolt on approval chains, net-terms collections, and sales-rep ordering by hand. **Why now:** B2B is one of Shopify's fastest-moving product areas, and each platform release creates adjacent gaps faster than incumbents fill them. **Competition:** Low to moderate; the legacy wholesale apps predate native B2B and are mid-rewrite. **Monetization:** Higher price points than DTC apps ($99+) are normal because the buyer is running real invoice volume. Longer sales cycles are the tradeoff.

### 12. Customer account extension hub

**Pain:** The new customer accounts are a high-intent, logged-in surface, and most merchants show a bare order list where loyalty status, subscriptions, warranties, and reorders should live. **Why now:** Customer account UI extensions are recent enough that the category has no default winner. **Competition:** Low, with the caveat that loyalty incumbents will arrive; the window is measured in quarters, not years. **Monetization:** Subscription, with tiers keyed to which modules (loyalty, warranty, reorder) are active.

### 13. Billing and metrics analytics for app developers

**Pain:** App founders need MRR, churn, and cohort analytics on top of Shopify's billing events, and the tool many of them used is winding down. **Why now:** [Mantle's shutdown](/blog/mantle-shutting-down-shopify-app-partners) orphaned a paying customer base with an explicit migration deadline, which is the cleanest demand signal an idea can have. **Competition:** Low post-shutdown, but scrutinize why the incumbent wound down; the market may be smaller than it is loud. **Monetization:** Percentage-of-MRR or flat tiers. Selling to developers means informed, demanding customers and short sales cycles.

### 14. Accessibility and compliance monitor

**Pain:** Accessibility rules now carry real enforcement weight in the EU, and most themes plus app-injected widgets fail basic checks the merchant never sees. **Why now:** The European Accessibility Act's obligations started applying to e-commerce in mid-2025, and enforcement attention is growing; merchants are hearing about it from lawyers, not developers. **Competition:** Low within Shopify-native tooling; generic web scanners exist but don't speak theme-and-app language. **Monetization:** Subscription with a free scan as the acquisition wedge. Compliance stakes support strong retention.

### 15. AI visibility monitor for merchants

**Pain:** Shoppers increasingly ask AI assistants what to buy, and merchants have no idea how those assistants describe their store, whether their products appear, or what wrong information circulates. **Why now:** AI-mediated discovery is compounding, and [app and store visibility inside AI answers](/blog/shopify-apps-ai-visibility) is becoming its own channel to manage. **Competition:** Low to moderate; general-purpose AI-SEO monitors exist, Shopify-native ones barely do. **Monetization:** Subscription tiered by tracked queries and competitors. Reporting is the entry product; remediation guidance is the upsell.

## How to Pick One

Resist scoring your way to an answer. The table narrows fifteen to three or four; the final cut comes from your unfair advantages. A founder who ran returns at a brand should weight idea 8 far above its table score. A developer fluent in WebAssembly starts idea 4 with half the build risk priced out.

Then pressure-test the finalists the same way you'd validate anything: read the incumbents' one-star reviews, find the pain stated unprompted in merchant communities, and put a priced waitlist page in front of a couple hundred targeted visitors. Install the top incumbents and feel their onboarding yourself; a Partner development store covers most of it, and a trial store on [Shopify](https://shopify.pxf.io/c/6318547/3797171/13624) gives you the full merchant context with a live theme and checkout when the dev store falls short.

## Your Next Step

Pick the two ideas from the table that intersect with something you already know, and give each one evening of App Store research: top ten incumbents, review counts, rating trends, pricing floors, last-update dates. One of the two will look meaningfully weaker by midnight. Validate the survivor properly before you write a line of code.
