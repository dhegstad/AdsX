International sales represent the largest untapped growth opportunity for most Shopify stores. Over 70 percent of global e-commerce occurs outside the United States, yet only 35 percent of Shopify merchants actively sell to international customers. The merchants who do sell internationally report that cross-border revenue accounts for 20-40 percent of their total sales. The barrier has always been complexity: managing multiple currencies, calculating duties and taxes, translating content, setting regional prices, and navigating international shipping. Shopify Markets removes most of that complexity by providing a centralized system for managing international selling from within your Shopify admin.

This guide covers every aspect of setting up and optimizing [Shopify](https://shopify.pxf.io/c/6318547/3797171/13624) Markets for international selling, from initial configuration to advanced strategies for maximizing revenue in global markets.

## What Shopify Markets Is and How It Works

Shopify Markets is a cross-border management tool built into your Shopify admin that centralizes all the settings, configurations, and optimizations needed to sell internationally. Rather than managing each international selling requirement through separate apps, plugins, or manual processes, Markets provides a unified interface for currencies, languages, domains, duties, taxes, and pricing adjustments.

### The Problem Markets Solves

Before Shopify Markets, selling internationally on Shopify required a patchwork of solutions:

- Currency conversion apps for multi-currency display
- Translation apps for multilingual content
- Separate tax configuration for each country
- Manual duty calculations or third-party duty estimation tools
- International shipping apps for carrier integration
- Separate store instances for major markets (common for Shopify Plus merchants)

This fragmented approach was expensive, difficult to maintain, and created inconsistent customer experiences. Markets consolidates these functions into a single, managed system.

### How Markets Works

Shopify Markets organizes international selling around the concept of "markets," where each market represents a country or group of countries with shared settings. For each market, you configure:

- **Currency**: Which currency customers see prices in
- **Language**: Which language the store content displays in
- **Domain**: Whether the market uses a subfolder (store.com/fr), subdomain (fr.store.com), or country-specific domain (store.fr)
- **Pricing**: Whether prices are auto-converted or manually set for each market
- **Duties and taxes**: Whether duties are estimated or collected at checkout
- **Product availability**: Which products are available in each market

Your primary market (usually your home country) serves as the default. International markets inherit your primary settings and allow you to override specific configurations for each region.

## Setting Up Shopify Markets

### Step 1: Access and Enable Markets

Navigate to Settings in your Shopify admin, then click Markets. You will see your primary market (your home country) already configured. Click "Add Market" to create your first international market.

### Step 2: Create Your Priority Markets

Start with markets where you have the highest demand or strategic interest. Use your Shopify Analytics and Google Analytics data to identify which countries are already generating traffic and orders.

Common priority markets for US-based merchants:

1. **Canada**: Closest market with minimal cultural differences, English-speaking, and straightforward shipping
2. **United Kingdom**: Large English-speaking market with high e-commerce adoption
3. **European Union**: Treat as a single market initially, with Germany, France, and Netherlands as the highest-volume countries
4. **Australia**: English-speaking, high purchasing power, strong e-commerce culture

For each market, you can either create individual country markets or group countries. Grouping countries (like all EU countries in one market) simplifies management, while individual markets allow more granular control.

### Step 3: Configure Multi-Currency Pricing

For each market, enable the local currency. Shopify Markets supports automatic currency conversion using real-time exchange rates plus your configured conversion fee (default 1.5 percent by Shopify).

**Automatic conversion**: Prices are calculated from your primary currency using the current exchange rate. This requires no manual price management but can result in odd prices (like $47.83) that feel unnatural in the local market.

**Rounding rules**: Configure rounding rules to create natural-looking prices. Set prices to round to the nearest .99 or .95 to match local pricing conventions. For example, if the converted price of a $49.99 product is 46.83 euros, rounding to 46.99 euros creates a more natural price point.

**Manual price overrides**: For key products or markets, manually set prices in local currency rather than relying on conversion. This gives you full control over pricing strategy in each market and ensures prices feel natural and competitive. Manual pricing requires more maintenance but produces better results for high-priority markets.

### Step 4: Set Up International Domains

International domains improve both SEO and customer trust. Shopify Markets supports three domain strategies:

**Subfolders (recommended for most merchants)**: Your store displays at store.com/fr for France, store.com/de for Germany, and so on. This approach consolidates domain authority for SEO and requires no additional domain purchases. Shopify automatically creates and manages subfolders for each market.

**Subdomains**: Your store displays at fr.store.com, de.store.com, etc. This provides clear market separation while keeping a single root domain. Slightly less optimal for SEO than subfolders but cleaner URLs.

**Country-specific domains**: Your store uses store.fr for France, store.de for Germany, etc. This requires purchasing additional domains and provides the strongest local presence for SEO and customer trust. Best for markets where you are investing heavily in local marketing and brand building.

For most merchants starting out with international selling, subfolders provide the best balance of simplicity, SEO benefit, and customer experience.

### Step 5: Configure Language and Translation

Add languages for each market through the Shopify admin under Settings, then Languages. Shopify Markets supports automatic translation through the Translate and Adapt app (free) or third-party translation apps.

**Translation approach by content type**:

- **Product titles and descriptions**: Machine translation with human review for top 20-30 products. Machine-only translation for the remainder of the catalog
- **Navigation and UI elements**: Human translation for accuracy and natural language. These are high-visibility elements that customers interact with on every page
- **Homepage and landing pages**: Professional human translation for key marketing pages. These pages drive first impressions and conversion
- **Checkout flow**: Professional human translation is essential. Any confusion during checkout leads directly to abandoned carts
- **Legal pages (returns, privacy, terms)**: Professional human translation to ensure legal accuracy
- **Blog content**: Machine translation if translating blog posts; alternatively, create market-specific content

### Step 6: Configure Duties and Taxes

International orders may be subject to import duties, customs fees, and local taxes (VAT, GST). How you handle these charges significantly affects customer experience and conversion rates.

**Option 1: DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)**: You collect duties and taxes at checkout and handle customs clearance on behalf of the customer. The customer knows their total cost upfront with no surprise charges at delivery. This is the recommended approach for professional international selling and is available through Shopify Markets Pro.

**Option 2: DAP (Delivered at Place)**: Duties and taxes are charged by the carrier at delivery. The customer may be surprised by additional charges, which leads to refused deliveries and negative experiences. This is the default approach without Markets Pro.

**Setting up duties estimation**: Assign HS (Harmonized System) codes to your products. HS codes are international product classification codes that customs authorities use to determine duty rates. Shopify provides guidance on selecting the correct codes, and Markets uses these codes to calculate estimated duties at checkout.

**Country-specific tax configuration**: Enable automatic tax collection for countries where you have tax obligations. Shopify Markets automatically applies the correct VAT or GST rates for supported countries.

## Regional Pricing Strategies

Setting prices for international markets involves more than currency conversion. Strategic pricing accounts for local market conditions, competitive landscapes, and consumer expectations.

### Price Adjustment by Market

Shopify Markets allows you to set a percentage price adjustment for each market. This adjustment applies on top of currency conversion, allowing you to raise or lower prices for specific markets.

**When to raise prices**:
- Markets with higher shipping costs (to offset fulfillment expenses)
- Markets with higher duties or taxes (to maintain margins after import costs)
- Markets with lower competitive pressure in your product category
- Markets with higher purchasing power and willingness to pay premium prices

**When to lower prices**:
- Markets with strong local competition at lower price points
- Markets with lower purchasing power where your standard pricing is prohibitive
- Markets where you are trying to establish brand awareness and market share
- Markets where currency conversion creates awkwardly high prices

Typical price adjustments range from -15 percent to +25 percent relative to your primary market pricing.

### Pricing Psychology Across Cultures

Different markets have different pricing expectations:

- **United States**: .99 endings are standard (49.99)
- **European Union**: Both .99 and .00 endings are common, varying by country
- **Japan**: Round numbers ending in 00 are preferred (4,980 yen rather than 4,999 yen)
- **United Kingdom**: .99 endings similar to the US
- **China**: Numbers containing 8 are considered auspicious

Configure rounding rules in Shopify Markets to match local conventions. This small detail affects perceived price fairness and conversion rates.

### Competitive Pricing Analysis

Research competitor pricing in each target market before setting your prices. Use local versions of Google Shopping, Amazon, and category-specific marketplaces to understand the competitive price range. Price your products competitively within the local context rather than simply converting your domestic prices.

A product priced at $79 in the US might need to be 59 euros in France (lower than the direct conversion) to be competitive against local brands, or it might support 89 euros in markets where imported goods carry a premium cachet.

## International Shipping and Fulfillment

Shipping is the operational backbone of international selling. Your shipping strategy directly affects delivery times, costs, customer satisfaction, and return rates.

### Shipping Options for International Orders

**Direct shipping from your primary location**: The simplest approach. Ship international orders from the same warehouse or fulfillment center that handles domestic orders. Delivery times are longer (7-21 days) and shipping costs are higher, but there is no additional infrastructure to manage.

**International fulfillment centers**: Store inventory in warehouses located in your major international markets. This reduces delivery times to 2-5 days and shipping costs for customers, but requires managing inventory across multiple locations. Shopify Fulfillment Network and third-party 3PLs like ShipBob, Deliverr, or local providers offer international warehousing.

**Dropshipping for international markets**: Partner with suppliers who can fulfill orders from locations closer to your international customers. This eliminates the need for international inventory but reduces your control over product quality and shipping experience.

### Carrier Selection by Market

Different carriers offer the best value for different international routes:

- **USPS Priority Mail International**: Cost-effective for lightweight packages under 4 lbs to most countries. Delivery in 6-10 business days
- **DHL Express**: Premium carrier with excellent global coverage. 2-5 day delivery. Higher costs but includes customs brokerage and tracking
- **FedEx International**: Reliable for North America, Europe, and Asia. 3-7 day delivery depending on service level
- **UPS International**: Strong for B2B shipments and heavy packages. Good coverage in North America and Europe
- **Royal Mail**: Cost-effective for shipping to and within the UK
- **Australia Post**: Best rates for Australian market delivery

Configure carrier-specific shipping rates for each market in Shopify's shipping settings. Offer multiple shipping options (economy and express) to give customers control over the cost-speed tradeoff.

### Shipping Rate Strategies

**Free shipping thresholds**: Set market-specific free shipping thresholds. International customers may need a higher threshold to account for the higher shipping costs. A domestic free shipping threshold of $75 might translate to $125-150 for international orders.

**Flat-rate shipping**: Offer a single flat rate for each market or region. This simplifies the customer's decision and makes shipping costs predictable. Calculate the flat rate to approximately break even on average across the range of order sizes you ship to that market.

**Real-time carrier rates**: Display actual carrier rates at checkout based on order weight, dimensions, and destination. This is the most accurate but can lead to sticker shock for customers in remote or expensive-to-ship destinations.

**Free international shipping**: If your margins support it, free international shipping is the strongest conversion driver for cross-border sales. Build the average shipping cost into your product prices to maintain margins.

### Returns and Exchanges for International Orders

International returns are the biggest operational challenge of cross-border selling. Have a clear strategy before expanding internationally:

**Option 1: Local return addresses**: Partner with a returns processing company in each major market that receives returns on your behalf and either reshapes or consolidates them for bulk return shipment. Services like Happy Returns, Loop Returns, and local logistics partners facilitate this.

**Option 2: Customer-paid return shipping**: Clearly communicate that international return shipping is the customer's responsibility. This is the simplest approach operationally but may reduce conversion for first-time international customers.

**Option 3: Refund without return**: For products with low per-unit cost (under $20-30), consider refunding international customers without requiring the physical return. The cost of international return shipping often exceeds the product value, making refund-without-return more cost-effective.

**Option 4: Store credit instead of refunds**: Offer store credit for international returns to retain the revenue while satisfying the customer. Combine this with customer-paid return shipping for cases where a physical return is necessary.

## Optimizing for International SEO

### International SEO Fundamentals

Shopify Markets' domain structure provides the foundation for international SEO, but additional optimization is needed to rank well in local search results.

**Hreflang tags**: Shopify Markets automatically implements hreflang tags, which tell search engines which language and country version of a page to show in search results. These tags prevent duplicate content issues and ensure French customers see your French-language pages, German customers see German pages, and so on.

**Local keyword research**: Keywords do not translate directly between languages. Research keywords in each market's native language using local search tools. The way German customers search for "running shoes" (Laufschuhe) may involve different terminology than a direct translation would suggest.

**Local content**: Create market-specific blog content, landing pages, and category descriptions that reference local trends, seasons, holidays, and cultural context. This local relevance improves both search rankings and customer engagement.

**Local backlinks**: Build backlinks from websites in your target markets. Guest posts on local blogs, partnerships with local influencers, and PR outreach to local media improve your domain's authority in each market's search results.

### Google Merchant Center for International Markets

Configure separate Google Merchant Center feeds for each market, with localized product titles, descriptions, and prices. This ensures your products appear correctly in Google Shopping results in each country with the right currency, language, and pricing.

## Shopify Markets Pro: When to Upgrade

Shopify Markets Pro adds managed international services that simplify the most complex aspects of cross-border selling.

### What Markets Pro Includes

- **Managed duties and import taxes**: Accurate duty calculation, collection at checkout, and remittance to customs authorities
- **Customs documentation**: Automated generation of commercial invoices and customs declarations
- **Guaranteed landed cost**: Customers see the complete cost including duties and taxes at checkout with no surprise charges
- **Extended carrier options**: Access to international carrier networks optimized for each market
- **Regulatory compliance**: Automated compliance with import regulations in supported markets

### When Markets Pro Is Worth the Investment

Upgrade to Markets Pro when:

- International orders exceed 50-100 per month
- Customer complaints about surprise duty charges are common
- Your products are subject to significant import duties (above 10 percent)
- You are expanding into markets with complex customs requirements (EU, UK, Australia)
- You want to offer a premium, transparent international shopping experience

### Markets Pro Costs

Markets Pro charges a per-transaction fee that includes duty collection, tax remittance, and customs processing. The fee varies by market and product type but typically ranges from 2-6 percent of the order value. While this adds to your transaction costs, it replaces the manual effort, customer service costs, and lost sales that unpredictable duty charges create.

## Measuring International Performance

### Key Metrics by Market

Track these metrics for each international market:

- **Revenue by market**: Total sales and growth rate for each market
- **Conversion rate by market**: How well you convert visitors from each market compared to your primary market
- **Average order value by market**: How spending patterns differ across markets
- **Customer acquisition cost by market**: How much you spend to acquire each customer in each market
- **Return rate by market**: Whether certain markets have higher return rates due to shipping times, sizing differences, or product expectations
- **Customer lifetime value by market**: Whether international customers become repeat buyers at rates comparable to domestic customers

### Evaluating Market Profitability

Revenue alone does not determine market profitability. Calculate true profitability by accounting for:

- Currency conversion fees (1.5 percent on Shopify Payments)
- Shipping cost differentials
- Duty and tax collection costs (if using Markets Pro)
- Higher return processing costs
- Translation and localization costs
- Market-specific marketing spend

A market generating $10,000/month in revenue but costing $3,000 in additional expenses may be less profitable than a smaller market generating $5,000/month with only $500 in additional costs.

### When to Expand to New Markets

Expand to additional markets when:

- Your existing international markets are profitable and growing
- You have operational capacity to handle increased complexity
- Analytics data shows significant organic traffic from untapped markets
- You have validated product-market fit through small-scale testing
