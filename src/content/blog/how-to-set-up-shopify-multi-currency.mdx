International selling is one of the fastest paths to revenue growth for Shopify merchants. Cross-border e-commerce is projected to reach $7.9 trillion by 2030, and Shopify stores that sell in multiple currencies see an average 13% increase in international conversion rates compared to single-currency stores.

This guide covers everything you need to set up multi-currency selling on [Shopify](https://shopify.pxf.io/c/6318547/3797171/13624), from Shopify Markets configuration to currency-specific pricing strategies.

## What Is Shopify Markets and How Does It Enable Multi-Currency?

Shopify Markets is Shopify's built-in international selling solution. It centralizes the management of multi-currency pricing, language translations, local payment methods, duties, and taxes—all from a single Shopify store.

Before Shopify Markets, international selling required separate stores per country or complex third-party app configurations. Now, a single store can serve customers in 150+ countries with localized experiences.

### What Shopify Markets Includes

| Feature | Included With | Details |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| Multi-currency pricing | All Shopify plans | Automatic or manual pricing in 130+ currencies |
| Currency conversion | All Shopify plans | Auto-updates with exchange rates |
| Rounding rules | All Shopify plans | Clean price points across currencies |
| Market-specific pricing | All Shopify plans | Manual price adjustments per market |
| Local payment methods | Shopify Payments required | iDEAL, Bancontact, Klarna, etc. |
| Duty and tax calculation | All Shopify plans | Automatic collection at checkout |
| Market-specific domains | All Shopify plans | Subfolders, subdomains, or ccTLDs |
| Language translations | All Shopify plans | Translate storefront content |
| Product availability control | All Shopify plans | Control which products sell in which markets |

## How Do You Set Up Shopify Markets?

### Step 1: Access Markets Settings

In your Shopify admin, go to **Settings > Markets**. You will see your primary market (typically your home country) already configured.

### Step 2: Create New Markets

Click **Add market** and define your international markets. You can group countries into markets strategically:

**Recommended market structure:**

| Market Name | Countries | Currency | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| North America | US, Canada | USD, CAD | Shared logistics, similar buying behavior |
| Europe (EU) | EU countries | EUR | Single currency, unified VAT rules |
| United Kingdom | UK | GBP | Post-Brexit requires separate tax handling |
| Australia/NZ | Australia, New Zealand | AUD, NZD | Shared shipping zone |
| Rest of World | All others | Auto-convert | Catch-all for smaller markets |

Group countries that share similar shipping costs, tax structures, and consumer behaviors. This keeps management simple while allowing localized pricing.

### Step 3: Enable Currencies for Each Market

Within each market's settings:

1. Enable the local currency (EUR for Europe, GBP for UK, etc.)
2. Choose between automatic conversion or manual pricing
3. Set rounding rules (round to nearest .99, .95, or custom)
4. Configure any market-specific price adjustments (increase/decrease by percentage)

### Step 4: Configure Currency Conversion Settings

Go to **Settings > Markets > [Market] > Products and pricing**.

**Automatic conversion** — Shopify converts your base currency prices using current exchange rates plus your configured rounding rules. Easiest to manage but gives you less pricing control.

**Manual pricing** — You set specific prices for each product in each currency. More work but allows you to optimize pricing for local market conditions.

**Percentage adjustment** — Apply a blanket markup or markdown to all prices in a market. For example, increase European prices by 10% to account for VAT inclusion and higher logistics costs. This is a pragmatic middle ground between fully automatic and fully manual pricing.

### Step 5: Set Up Duties and Taxes

Navigate to **Settings > Markets > [Market] > Duties and taxes**.

**For EU markets:**
- Enable VAT collection at checkout
- Choose whether to display prices inclusive or exclusive of VAT (EU law requires inclusive display for B2C)
- Register for EU OSS if your total EU sales exceed 10,000 EUR annually

**For other international markets:**
- Decide between DDU (Delivered Duty Unpaid) and DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)
- If DDP, assign HS (Harmonized System) codes to all products for accurate duty estimation
- Add country-of-origin information to products

**DDP vs. DDU comparison:**

| Factor | DDU (Duty Unpaid) | DDP (Duty Paid) |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Customer experience | Worse — surprise charges on delivery | Better — all costs clear at checkout |
| Refused deliveries | Higher (5-15%) | Near zero |
| Checkout conversion | Lower — uncertainty about total cost | Higher — full transparency |
| Your operational burden | Lower — carrier handles duty collection | Higher — you estimate and collect duties |
| Best for | Low-value orders, testing new markets | Established markets, premium brands |

### Step 6: Configure Payment Methods

If you use Shopify Payments, local payment methods activate automatically per market. To verify:

1. Go to **Settings > Payments**
2. Under Shopify Payments, click **Manage**
3. Review which local payment methods are enabled per market
4. Enable any additional methods relevant to your key markets

### Step 7: Set Up International Domains

Shopify supports three domain strategies for international markets:

| Strategy | Example | SEO Impact | Management |
|----------|---------|------------|------------|
| Subfolders | yourstore.com/en-gb/ | Good (leverages main domain authority) | Easiest |
| Subdomains | uk.yourstore.com | Moderate | Medium |
| Country-code TLDs | yourstore.co.uk | Best (strong local signals) | Most complex |

For most Shopify merchants, subfolders provide the best balance of SEO value and management simplicity. The subfolder approach keeps all your domain authority consolidated while sending clear geographic signals to search engines.

## How Do You Set Optimal Prices for Each Market?

Automatic currency conversion is a starting point, but optimized international pricing considers more than exchange rates.

### Pricing Strategy by Market

**Factor in local purchasing power.** A price that converts well in the US may be too high for customers in Southeast Asia or too low to maximize revenue in Switzerland. Research competitor pricing in each market.

**Account for local costs.** International shipping, duties, payment processing fees, and VAT all vary by market. Your pricing needs to absorb these costs while maintaining target margins.

**Use psychological pricing per currency.** Each currency has its own "magic numbers." EUR prices often end in .90 or .99. GBP prices commonly end in .99 or .95. JPY prices should end in round numbers since there are no decimal places.

### Price Adjustment Recommendations

| Market | Typical Adjustment | Reason |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| EU (VAT-inclusive) | +10-20% over US price | VAT inclusion, shipping costs |
| UK | +5-15% over US price | VAT, shipping |
| Canada | Match or +5% in CAD | Similar purchasing power, lower shipping than overseas |
| Australia | +10-15% over US price | Shipping distance, GST |
| Japan | Varies widely | Strong purchasing power, research local pricing |

## How Do You Optimize the International Customer Experience?

Enabling multi-currency is just the technical foundation. The customer experience around it determines whether international visitors actually convert.

### Currency Selector Placement

Place a visible currency/country selector in your header or footer. Shopify Markets automatically detects visitor location via IP and suggests the appropriate market, but customers should always be able to manually switch.

### Language Translation

For your primary international markets, translate your store into local languages. Shopify's Translate & Adapt app (free) handles basic translations. For professional quality, use a combination of AI translation and human review for key pages (homepage, product pages, checkout).

### Localized Shipping Information

Display shipping costs and estimated delivery times per market. "Free shipping" means nothing if the customer does not know when their order will arrive. International customers particularly value delivery time transparency.

### International Returns Policy

Clearly communicate your international returns process. If you do not accept international returns (or charge a restocking fee), state this clearly before checkout. If you have local return addresses in key markets, highlight this as a benefit.

## How Do You Measure International Market Performance?

Track these metrics per market monthly:

| Metric | Why It Matters | Target |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| Conversion rate by market | Identifies markets with UX issues | Within 75% of domestic rate |
| Average order value by market | Validates pricing strategy | Aligned with purchasing power |
| Return rate by market | Flags sizing, quality, or expectation issues | Under 10% |
| Shipping cost as % of AOV | Margin impact | Under 15% |
| Customer acquisition cost by market | Marketing efficiency | Positive ROI within 60 days |

### Common International Selling Issues

1. **High return rates in specific markets** — Often indicates sizing confusion (offer detailed size charts with local sizing systems) or product expectations misaligned with descriptions
2. **Low conversion despite high traffic** — Usually a payment method issue. Check if you offer the preferred local payment option.
3. **Cart abandonment at checkout** — Frequently caused by unexpected duty/tax charges. Switch to DDP and show all-inclusive pricing.
4. **Slow international shipping complaints** — Set clear delivery expectations on product pages and offer expedited options for key markets.
