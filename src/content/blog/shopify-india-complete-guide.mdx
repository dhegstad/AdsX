## GST Compliance for Shopify Sellers in India

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is non-negotiable if you're selling in India. Here's what you need to know:

### Do You Need GST Registration?

**You need GST registration if**:
- Annual turnover exceeds ₹20 lakhs (products) or ₹40 lakhs (services)
- You're a service provider supplying services through e-commerce
- You're importing goods
- You're in specific states regardless of turnover

**You don't need GST if**:
- Your annual turnover is below ₹20 lakhs
- You're purely exporting goods

### Current GST Rates on E-commerce

Most product categories fall into these GST brackets:
- **0%**: Food items, books, medicines
- **5%**: Clothing, footwear, most textiles
- **12%**: Most electronics, gadgets, home appliances
- **18%**: Cosmetics, furniture, apparel, most services
- **28%**: Luxury goods, high-end electronics

Different categories have different rates—verify your product category on the GST Council website.

### Shopify + GST Integration

You'll need to:

1. **Register for GST**: File Form REG-01 at www.gst.gov.in (takes 1-2 weeks)
2. **Configure GST in Shopify**: Use apps like "Aye Taxes" or "Taska" to automate GST calculation
3. **Set up GSTR returns**:
   - **GSTR-1**: Outward supply (sales) - monthly or quarterly
   - **GSTR-3B**: Self-declared summary - before GSTR-1
   - Most sellers file monthly; check your registration category

4. **Maintain records**: Keep invoices for 6 years per GST requirements

### GST for Exports

Significant advantage: Exports from India are taxed at 0% GST. If you sell internationally and your annual exports exceed ₹20 lakhs:
- You can claim GST refund on inputs
- No GST charged to international customers
- Document exports with commercial invoices and shipping proof

**Pro tip**: Use accounting software like Zoho Books or Finway that integrates with Shopify to automate GST calculations and GSTR filing.

### Setting Up Shipping in Shopify

1. **Choose provider(s)**: Select based on coverage, pricing, and reliability
2. **Get API credentials**: Each provider gives you account details
3. **Configure in Shopify**: Settings > Shipping and delivery > Set up shipping zones
4. **Create shipping rates**:
   - Standard (4-7 days)
   - Express (2-3 days)
   - Overnight (where available)
5. **Install apps**: Use provider apps for real-time tracking integration
6. **Print labels**: Use Shopify's label printing for seamless workflow

**Best Practice**: Negotiate rates with at least 2 providers based on your expected monthly volume (discounts typically apply at ₹2,00,000+ monthly shipments).

## Cross-Border Selling from India

One of Shopify's greatest strengths is enabling Indian sellers to go global. Here's how:

### Countries You Can Sell To

Shopify Markets feature allows you to sell to 200+ countries. Popular markets for Indian sellers:
- USA and Canada (largest markets)
- UK and EU
- Southeast Asia (Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand)
- UAE and GCC (geographic proximity)
- Australia and New Zealand

### Currency Conversion

Customers in target countries see prices in their local currency. Shopify handles currency conversion automatically—you set exchange rate markup (typically 2-3%).

### International Shipping

Options for shipping globally:
1. **Courier services**: DHL, FedEx, UPS (expensive but reliable)
2. **Dedicated international logistics**: ShipRocket, Shiprocket Pro (cost-effective, India-optimized)
3. **Print on demand**: For apparel/products, use services like Printful
4. **Dropshipping**: Partner with international fulfillment centers

**Most cost-effective**: ShipRocket for parcels under 500g to popular countries (typically ₹150-400 to USA).

### Customs and Regulations

**Important considerations**:
- Each country has customs restrictions—verify your products
- Declare correct values to avoid customs issues
- Use commercial invoices for international shipments
- GST doesn't apply (0% for exports)
- Some items require certificates (cosmetics, food, electronics)

### International Payment Processing

Shopify handles multi-currency processing automatically. Customers pay in their currency, you receive in INR. Payment gateways like Razorpay also support international card payments.

### Scaling International Business

Once you've proven domestic success, international expansion can 5-10x your revenue:
- Start with English-speaking countries (USA, UK, Australia)
- Expand to geographically close markets (Southeast Asia)
- Gradually add more markets
- Use analytics to understand which markets drive highest ROI

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Ignoring GST from the Start

Don't leave GST compliance to later. It compounds into a nightmare. Implement from day one.

### 2. Poor Product Photography

Most Indian customers can't touch products. High-quality images are essential. Invest in photography.

### 3. Limited Payment Options

Offer Razorpay, not just credit cards. UPI adoption is massive in India.

### 4. Slow Shipping Promises

Never promise 2-day delivery pan-India unless using premium couriers. Set realistic expectations, over-deliver.

### 5. Neglecting Customer Service

Whatsapp is a must. Phone support preferred. Email alone is insufficient.

### 6. Wrong Product Pricing

Factor in GST, shipping, gateway fees. Many new sellers underprice. Use pricing calculators.

### 7. No Mobile Optimization

Mobile-first is non-negotiable in India. Test on actual phones and slow connections.
