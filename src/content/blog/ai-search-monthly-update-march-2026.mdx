Welcome to the March 2026 edition of our monthly AI search briefing. Every month, we track changes across every major AI search platform and distill what matters for brands managing their AI visibility and advertising strategies.

This month brought significant developments across the board: ChatGPT rolled out major shopping upgrades, Perplexity expanded its advertising platform with new targeting capabilities, Gemini deepened its integration with Google's commerce ecosystem, Claude gained meaningful market share in professional research, and Grok made moves into real-time recommendation territory.

Here is everything that changed and what it means for your brand.

## ChatGPT Updates

March was a big month for ChatGPT, particularly on the commerce and advertising side. OpenAI continues to push aggressively toward making ChatGPT a primary shopping and product discovery interface.

### Shopping Experience 2.0 Launch

On March 3, OpenAI launched what it calls "Shopping Experience 2.0," a significant overhaul of how ChatGPT handles product queries and purchase-related conversations.

**Key changes:**

- **Visual product carousels**: When users ask for product recommendations, ChatGPT now displays interactive carousels with product images, pricing, ratings, and retailer links directly in the conversation. This replaces the previous text-only recommendation format.
- **Price comparison integration**: ChatGPT now pulls real-time pricing from multiple retailers for the same product, displaying a price comparison table within the conversation. Users can see where to get the best deal without leaving ChatGPT.
- **Purchase memory**: ChatGPT now remembers past purchases and product preferences (for Plus and Pro subscribers) and factors them into future recommendations. A user who previously bought organic skincare products will see that preference reflected in future beauty recommendations.
- **Review synthesis improvements**: The model now synthesizes reviews from a broader set of sources and presents pros/cons summaries in a more structured format, including specific review counts and average ratings.

### Ad Platform Enhancements

OpenAI also shipped several updates to its advertising platform:

- **Conversion API v2**: New server-side tracking capabilities that improve attribution accuracy by 20-30% compared to pixel-only tracking. This addresses a major advertiser complaint about measurement gaps.
- **Dynamic creative optimization**: ChatGPT Ads now automatically tests multiple ad copy variations and optimizes toward the highest-performing version based on engagement and conversion data.
- **Audience expansion controls**: Advertisers can now set guardrails on how aggressively ChatGPT expands targeting beyond specified conversation topics. This prevents budget waste on tangentially related queries.
- **Shopping Ads API**: E-commerce brands can now programmatically manage shopping ad campaigns, including automated bid adjustments based on inventory levels and margin data.

### Model Update: GPT-5.5 Turbo

OpenAI quietly rolled out GPT-5.5 Turbo as the default model for free-tier ChatGPT users on March 8. Key implications:

- **Improved factual accuracy**: Early testing shows a 15% reduction in hallucinated brand claims, which means more reliable brand mentions
- **Longer context retention**: Conversations now maintain context more effectively over extended sessions, which benefits brands that appear in multi-turn research conversations
- **Better source attribution**: The model is more consistently citing sources for factual claims, increasing the importance of having authoritative, citable content

### What This Means for Brands

The Shopping Experience 2.0 launch is the most consequential ChatGPT update for e-commerce brands this year. If you sell products online, here is what to do now:

1. **Ensure your product feed is connected and current.** ChatGPT's visual carousels pull from product feeds. If your data is stale or incomplete, you will not appear in these high-visibility placements. Update pricing, images, and availability daily.

2. **Optimize for review synthesis.** ChatGPT is now pulling reviews from more sources. Brands with reviews distributed across multiple platforms (not just their own site) will see better representation in the new review summaries.

3. **Test Shopping Ads if you have not already.** The new ad format is significantly more engaging than the previous text-only sponsored placements. Early adopters are seeing 40-60% higher click-through rates.

4. **Implement Conversion API v2.** If you are running ChatGPT Ads, the new conversion tracking is a material improvement. Migrate from pixel-only to server-side tracking within the next 30 days to improve your measurement accuracy and campaign optimization.

5. **Consider the "purchase memory" implications.** ChatGPT now remembers preferences and past purchases. Brands that successfully convert a first-time AI-referred customer have a higher probability of being recommended to that user again. First impressions matter more than ever.

## Perplexity Updates

Perplexity continued its rapid evolution from pure research tool to full-fledged advertising platform, with several notable developments in March.

### Industry Vertical Ad Targeting

On March 5, Perplexity launched industry vertical targeting for its advertising platform. Advertisers can now target users based on professional context rather than just query topics.

**Available verticals:**

- Technology and software
- Financial services and fintech
- Healthcare and life sciences
- Legal and professional services
- Manufacturing and industrial
- Education and EdTech
- Real estate and construction
- Retail and e-commerce

This is powered by Perplexity's analysis of query patterns, session context, and (for logged-in users) profile data. Early testing shows that vertical targeting improves B2B campaign performance by 25-35% compared to topic-only targeting.

### Perplexity Enterprise Search Expansion

Perplexity expanded its enterprise search product to 15 new enterprise customers in March, bringing its total enterprise deployment count to over 200 organizations. While this is a product update rather than an advertising update, it matters for brand visibility because:

- Enterprise deployments increase total query volume, particularly for B2B categories
- Enterprise users tend to have higher purchase authority
- Queries within enterprise contexts often relate directly to procurement and vendor evaluation

### New "Deep Research" Ad Format

Perplexity introduced a new ad format called "Deep Research Sponsorships" that targets users who activate Perplexity's multi-step research mode. When a user initiates a deep research query (which involves Perplexity conducting multiple searches, synthesizing findings, and producing a comprehensive report), sponsors can place their brand within the final research report.

**Format details:**

- Appears as a "Recommended Solution" or "Featured Provider" within the research report
- Includes up to 150 words of brand copy, a link, and a call to action
- Priced on a per-report basis ($8-$25 per appearance depending on category)
- Currently available for technology, finance, and healthcare verticals

### Source Citation Algorithm Update

Perplexity updated its source citation algorithm on March 11, changing how it selects and prioritizes sources in its answers. Based on our testing:

- **Recency is now weighted more heavily**: Fresher content is more likely to be cited, particularly for queries about products, services, and market trends
- **Review aggregation sources** now appear more frequently in product-related citations
- **Primary sources** (brand websites, official documentation) are now cited more prominently than secondary sources (blog posts about the brand)
- **Structured data** continues to be a strong positive signal for citation likelihood

### What This Means for Brands

Perplexity's March updates reinforce its position as the premium platform for reaching professional, research-oriented audiences. Here is what to act on:

1. **If you are a B2B brand, test industry vertical targeting immediately.** The ability to target by professional context rather than just query topic is a significant advancement. Set up a test campaign with $2,000-$3,000 targeting your specific vertical and compare performance to topic-only campaigns.

2. **Refresh your content calendar.** The source citation algorithm now favors recency more heavily. Brands that publish and update content regularly will see improved organic citation rates. Aim for weekly content updates at minimum.

3. **Evaluate Deep Research Sponsorships.** If your product involves a complex purchase decision (enterprise software, financial products, professional services), the Deep Research format reaches users at the exact moment they are conducting in-depth evaluation. The per-report pricing makes it easy to test with limited budget.

4. **Prioritize primary source content.** Perplexity is now giving more citation weight to official brand sources over third-party mentions. Ensure your product pages, documentation, and owned content are comprehensive, well-structured, and up to date.

## Gemini Updates

Google's Gemini continued its integration with the broader Google ecosystem in March, with updates that blur the line between traditional search and AI-powered discovery.

### AI Mode Expansion in Google Search

Google expanded Gemini's "AI Mode" to all U.S. users on March 7, making the AI-powered search experience available to everyone rather than just opted-in beta users. AI Mode now appears as a default tab alongside the traditional search results, images, and shopping tabs.

**Key stats from the rollout:**

- 38% of U.S. Google users activated AI Mode at least once in the first week
- Shopping-related queries in AI Mode grew 145% week-over-week
- Average AI Mode session is 2.3 queries, with users asking follow-up questions

### Google Shopping Integration

Gemini's AI Mode now fully integrates with Google Shopping data, including:

- **Product Knowledge Panels**: When Gemini mentions a product, it can display a rich panel with images, pricing from multiple retailers, reviews, and specifications
- **Merchant Center data**: Brands with optimized Google Merchant Center feeds see their products featured more prominently in Gemini's AI responses
- **Local inventory**: For location-based queries, Gemini can show local availability and store information alongside AI recommendations
- **Price tracking**: Users can set price alerts for products recommended by Gemini, creating an ongoing engagement loop

### Ad Integration in AI Mode

Google began testing ad placements within Gemini AI Mode responses for a subset of users. While still in limited beta, the formats being tested include:

- Sponsored product mentions within AI-generated answers
- Shopping ad cards at the end of product recommendation responses
- "Explore more" sponsored links for category queries

This is particularly significant because it represents Google applying its advertising infrastructure (the world's most sophisticated) to AI-generated responses. Early performance data suggests these ads achieve 2-3x the CTR of traditional search ads for the same queries.

### Gemini Advanced for Research

Gemini Advanced (Google's premium tier) received a significant upgrade to its research capabilities, including the ability to analyze uploaded documents, compare products across multiple dimensions, and generate structured recommendation reports. This positions Gemini Advanced as a competitor to Perplexity's deep research functionality.

### What This Means for Brands

Google's moves with Gemini are arguably the most strategically important developments in AI search this month, because they tie AI recommendations directly to Google's existing advertising and commerce infrastructure.

1. **Optimize your Google Merchant Center feed.** This is now the most important single data source for AI visibility on Gemini. Ensure your product titles, descriptions, images, pricing, and availability are accurate and comprehensive. Brands with poor Merchant Center data will be invisible in Gemini's shopping recommendations.

2. **Prepare for Gemini AI Mode ads.** While still in beta, full rollout is expected in Q2 2026. Brands already running Google Shopping campaigns will have a seamless transition. If you are not running Google Shopping, start now to build performance history before AI Mode ads launch broadly.

3. **Monitor your AI Mode visibility.** With 38% of U.S. Google users already trying AI Mode, this is not a future consideration; it is a current channel. Run test queries in AI Mode to see if and how your brand appears, and adjust your content and technical SEO accordingly.

4. **Local businesses: optimize for local AI recommendations.** Gemini's local inventory integration means that "near me" and location-based AI queries will increasingly feature local product availability. Ensure your local inventory data is connected and current.

## Claude Updates

Anthropic's Claude had a quieter month in terms of feature launches, but made meaningful gains in market share and user engagement that matter for brand visibility.

### Market Share Growth

Claude's monthly active user base grew 22% in February (the most recent data available), reaching an estimated 45 million monthly active users. This growth was driven primarily by:

- Expansion of Claude's free tier capabilities
- Growing adoption in professional and enterprise contexts
- Strong word-of-mouth among developers, researchers, and knowledge workers

While still significantly smaller than ChatGPT, Claude's user base is now large enough that it represents a material channel for brand visibility, particularly in professional and technical categories.

### Improved Product Recommendation Capabilities

Anthropic updated Claude's approach to product and service recommendations in early March. Based on our testing:

- Claude is now **more willing to make specific recommendations** rather than providing generic advice. Previous versions frequently hedged with "it depends on your needs" without naming specific brands. The updated model names specific products and services more often.
- **Comparative analysis is stronger**: When asked to compare products, Claude now provides more structured, detailed comparisons with clearer differentiation between options
- **Source awareness has improved**: Claude now better reflects information from its training data about brand quality, market position, and customer sentiment

### Claude Enterprise Adoption

Anthropic announced that Claude is now deployed in over 500 enterprise environments, with particular strength in:

- Financial services (compliance research, market analysis)
- Legal (contract review, legal research)
- Healthcare (clinical research, drug interaction analysis)
- Technology (code review, documentation, technical research)

Enterprise deployments generate high-value queries from professional users with purchasing authority. Brands in these verticals should pay particular attention to their Claude visibility.

### What This Means for Brands

Claude's growing market share means it can no longer be treated as a secondary platform for AI visibility. Here is what to do:

1. **Include Claude in your AI visibility monitoring.** If you are only tracking ChatGPT and Perplexity, you are missing a growing segment of your potential audience. Add Claude to your monthly query testing across all relevant brand and category queries.

2. **Optimize for Claude's recommendation style.** Claude tends to favor content that is comprehensive, well-structured, and factually precise. Long-form, authoritative content with clear category positioning performs well in Claude's recommendations. Avoid hype and superlatives; Claude's model tends to deprioritize content that reads as promotional.

3. **Focus on professional and technical content.** Claude's user base over-indexes in professional categories. If you serve B2B or professional audiences, Claude visibility may deliver disproportionate value compared to its overall market share.

## Grok Updates

X's AI assistant Grok made several notable moves in March that expand its relevance as a brand visibility platform.

### Real-Time Recommendation Engine

Grok launched a "What's Hot Right Now" feature that combines real-time social sentiment analysis with product recommendations. When users ask about trending products, viral brands, or "what everyone is talking about," Grok now pulls from:

- Real-time X post volume and sentiment for brands and products
- Trending purchase mentions and reviews shared on X
- Influencer and creator product endorsements posted within the last 72 hours

This makes Grok the only AI platform with a real-time social signal layer for recommendations. Brands with strong X presence and active social conversation see significantly higher Grok visibility than those without.

### Grok Shopping Integration (Beta)

xAI launched a shopping integration beta for Grok that allows product recommendations to include pricing, images, and purchase links. The beta is limited to select retail categories (electronics, fashion, beauty) but is expected to expand throughout Q2 2026.

### Expanded Query Categories

Grok's query volume grew 45% in March, driven by X's integration of Grok into more surfaces within the X app. Grok now handles an estimated 80 million queries per month, making it a meaningful (if still smaller) player in AI search.

### What This Means for Brands

Grok's real-time social signal integration creates a unique dynamic that other AI platforms do not replicate.

1. **Maintain an active X presence.** Grok is the only AI platform where social media activity directly influences recommendation visibility. Brands with consistent posting, active engagement, and positive social sentiment will see stronger Grok recommendations.

2. **Monitor brand mentions on X.** Negative sentiment on X can now directly impact your Grok visibility. Implement social listening to identify and address negative conversations quickly.

3. **Consider Grok for trend-driven categories.** If your brand operates in fashion, beauty, consumer electronics, or other trend-sensitive categories, Grok's real-time recommendation engine can surface your products during viral moments. This is a unique advantage that no other AI platform currently offers.

4. **Do not over-invest yet.** Grok's 80 million monthly queries represent meaningful but still modest scale compared to ChatGPT and Gemini. Allocate monitoring and optimization effort proportional to Grok's share of your category's AI query volume.

## Cross-Platform Trends to Watch

Beyond individual platform updates, several broader trends emerged in March that will shape AI search strategy in the months ahead.

### Trend 1: The Shopping Wars Are Heating Up

Every major AI platform is investing heavily in commerce capabilities. ChatGPT's Shopping Experience 2.0, Gemini's Google Shopping integration, Perplexity's commerce partnerships, and Grok's shopping beta all point in the same direction: AI platforms want to own the product discovery and purchase journey.

**Brand implication:** Product data quality is becoming the most important factor in AI visibility for e-commerce brands. Invest in comprehensive, accurate, multi-platform product feeds.

### Trend 2: Professional and Enterprise AI Is Growing Faster Than Consumer

While consumer AI gets the headlines, enterprise and professional AI deployment grew 3x faster than consumer usage in Q1 2026. Perplexity Enterprise, Claude Enterprise, and Gemini for Workspace are all seeing rapid adoption.

**Brand implication:** B2B brands have a time-limited window to establish AI visibility with professional audiences before these channels become as competitive as consumer AI. The cost-per-visibility is lower now than it will be in 6 months.

### Trend 3: Real-Time Signals Are Gaining Weight

Grok's real-time social integration and Perplexity's increased recency weighting in citations reflect a broader trend: AI platforms are moving beyond static training data toward real-time information. Brands that publish, update, and generate fresh signals regularly will outperform those relying on evergreen content alone.

**Brand implication:** Shift from a "publish and forget" content strategy to a "publish and update" approach. Set a monthly cadence for refreshing key content with current data, new reviews, and updated product information.

### Trend 4: Attribution Is Getting Better, But Still Imperfect

ChatGPT's Conversion API v2 and improved tracking across platforms are making AI advertising measurement more reliable. However, cross-platform attribution remains a challenge, and organic AI visibility is still difficult to measure with precision.

**Brand implication:** Invest in multi-touch attribution infrastructure now. The brands that solve AI measurement first will be able to optimize spend more effectively than competitors flying blind.
