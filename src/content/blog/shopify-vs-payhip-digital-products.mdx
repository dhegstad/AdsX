Payhip is one of the lower-friction platforms for selling digital products. Free starter tier, intuitive setup, and a transaction-fee model mean creators can list their first product within an hour. The trade-off is reduced capability versus Shopify — fewer customization options, weaker SEO, less suitable for brand-building.

Here's how to decide between them.

## Payhip strengths

**Zero monthly fee on free tier.** Pay only when you sell. Friendly economics for testing whether your product idea works.

**Simple setup.** Account, product upload, instant store. Twenty minutes from sign-up to first listing.

**Digital product handling.** Native delivery for ebooks, courses, software downloads, memberships.

**EU VAT handling.** Built-in EU VAT calculation and remittance — meaningful for digital sellers serving European customers.

**License key generation.** For software products, automatic license key delivery.

**Built-in affiliate program.** Recruit affiliates without third-party tools.

## Payhip weaknesses

**Limited customization.** Templates exist but flexibility is constrained.

**Weaker SEO.** Single product pages don't rank well. You're dependent on direct traffic and email.

**Brand presence limited.** You're on Payhip's domain unless you connect a custom domain (paid plan only).

**Scaling at higher revenue.** Works at $1-10K/month. At $30K+/month, the limitations get noticeable.

**Email and marketing tools thin.** Basic email capture; no advanced flows or segmentation.

## Pricing structure

**Payhip:**
- Free tier: 0% monthly + 5% transaction fee
- Plus: $29/month + 2% transaction fee
- Pro: $99/month + 0% transaction fee

**Shopify Basic:**
- $29/month + 2.4% transaction fee + payment processing

For a digital product seller doing $5K/month:

- Payhip free: $0/month + $250 fees = $250 total
- Payhip Plus: $29 + $100 fees = $129 total
- Shopify Basic: $29 + $120 + ~$30 apps = $179 total

Payhip Plus wins on cost at this volume. Shopify catches up at $10K+/month when the additional capabilities matter more.

## What Shopify offers that Payhip doesn't

- Real SEO and content marketing
- Full theme customization
- Massive app ecosystem
- Better customer data and segmentation
- Real B2B and wholesale features
- Long-term scalability

## What Payhip offers that Shopify makes harder

- True free tier
- Built-in EU VAT remittance
- License key delivery (Shopify needs apps)
- Native affiliate program (Shopify needs apps like UpPromote or Refersion)

## Decision framework

**Payhip works when:**

- You sell only digital products
- Revenue is under $10K/month
- You have an existing audience driving traffic
- You don't need SEO from your store
- You want zero monthly fee while testing

**Shopify works when:**

- You sell digital + physical products
- Revenue exceeds $10K/month
- You need SEO traffic
- You want full brand customization
- You're building a long-term business

**Run both when:**

- You sell digital templates on Payhip (low-friction, solo creator energy)
- You sell premium products on Shopify (brand experience)

## A real creator example

A productivity expert running both:

- Payhip for $19-49 templates (40+ products, $4K/month revenue)
- Shopify for premium $399 program (brand-led)

The Payhip listings are low-effort — upload, set price, share link. The Shopify store is the brand. Different tools for different purposes.

## Common mistakes

**Building primary business on Payhip when you should be on Shopify.** If you're scaling, you're going to migrate eventually. Earlier is better.

**Using Shopify when you should use Payhip.** Single-template seller doing $500/month doesn't need Shopify's complexity.

**Not migrating top products to Shopify when scaling.** Hero products deserve the SEO and brand environment.

**Treating Payhip as long-term home for serious business.** It's a starter platform for most use cases.

## What to do this week

If you're a digital product creator deciding between, start with Payhip if you have under 5 products and no existing audience. The free tier eliminates risk.

Migrate to Shopify when you've validated demand, want brand expansion, and revenue justifies the operational depth.
