## Product Page Optimization for Health and Wellness

Your product pages must accomplish multiple goals: convert visitors, build trust, and maintain compliance. Here's how to optimize them.

### Essential Product Page Elements

**Above the fold:**

- Trust badges (certifications, testing)

**Product description structure:**
1. Primary benefit statement

4. Who should take it
5. How to use/dosage
6. Quality and testing information

**Supplement facts panel:**
- Full ingredient disclosure
- Amounts per serving
- Percent daily values
- Other ingredients list
- Allergen statements

**Trust elements:**
- Third-party testing certificates
- Manufacturing certifications
- Customer reviews
- Clinical research citations (if applicable)

### Compliance and Disclaimers

Every supplement product page must include appropriate disclaimers:

```
*These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration.
This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.
```

Position this disclaimer prominently on product pages, typically below the product description or near the add-to-cart button.

**Additional compliance considerations:**
- Avoid disease claims ("cures," "treats," "prevents")
- Use structure-function claims only ("supports immune health")
- Include allergen warnings prominently
- Display proper supplement facts panels
- Note any drug interaction warnings

### Building Trust Through Transparency

Health and wellness consumers are increasingly skeptical. Build trust through:

**Ingredient transparency:**
- Full disclosure of all ingredients
- No proprietary blends (show exact amounts)
- Explain why each ingredient is included
- Note ingredient forms and bioavailability

**Testing and quality:**
- Third-party testing documentation
- Certificate of Analysis (COA) availability
- Heavy metal testing results
- Potency verification

**Manufacturing information:**
- GMP certification display
- Made in USA/origin information
- Facility certifications
- Quality control process overview

## Certifications and Compliance Documentation

For health and wellness brands, certifications aren't optionalâ€”they're essential for both compliance and conversion.

### Required Certifications

**FDA Facility Registration:**
If you manufacture or distribute supplements in the US, your facility must be registered with the FDA. This applies to both domestic and international facilities.

**GMP Certification:**
Good Manufacturing Practice certification ensures your products are produced consistently and meet quality standards. This is legally required for supplement manufacturing.

**Third-Party Testing:**
While not legally required, third-party testing has become a market expectation:
- NSF International
- ConsumerLab
- USP (United States Pharmacopeia)
- Informed Sport (for athletic products)
- BSCG (Banned Substances Control Group)

### Value-Add Certifications

These certifications can increase conversion rates and justify premium pricing:

| Certification | Impact | Best For |
|--------------|--------|----------|
| USDA Organic | High trust, premium positioning | Whole food supplements |
| Non-GMO Project | Growing consumer demand | Broad appeal |
| Certified Vegan | Essential for vegan products | Plant-based formulas |
| Gluten-Free Certified | Important for sensitive consumers | Wide audience |
| NSF Certified for Sport | Required by many athletes | Sports nutrition |
| Certified B Corp | Brand values alignment | Mission-driven brands |

### Displaying Certifications on Shopify

Create dedicated certification trust sections:

**Homepage:** Certification logo bar above the fold or in hero section
**Product pages:** Certification badges near add-to-cart
**Footer:** Comprehensive certification listing
**Dedicated page:** Full certification documentation and COAs

## Payment Processing for Health Products

Most health and wellness products process through standard Shopify Payments without issue. However, some categories require attention.

### Standard Health Products (No Issues)

- Vitamins and minerals
- Protein powders and sports nutrition
- General wellness supplements
- Fitness equipment and accessories
- Healthy foods and snacks

### Higher-Risk Categories (May Need Special Processing)

**CBD and hemp products:**
Shopify Payments doesn't support CBD. You'll need a specialized processor like:
- Square
- PayKickstart
- Authorize.net with CBD-friendly merchant account

**Weight loss supplements:**
Some aggressive weight loss claims can trigger payment issues. Keep claims compliant and evidence-based.

**Sexual wellness supplements:**
May require specialized processing depending on marketing claims.

**Prescription-adjacent products:**
Products that mimic prescription medications may face scrutiny.

### Optimizing Payment for Health and Wellness

**Offer multiple payment options:**
- Credit/debit cards (standard)
- Shop Pay (increases conversion)
- PayPal (trust factor for new customers)
- Apple Pay/Google Pay (mobile conversion)
- Buy now, pay later (Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay)

**Subscription-specific considerations:**
- Ensure your processor supports recurring billing
- Set up proper dunning management for failed payments
- Implement card updater services

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Compliance Mistakes

**Making disease claims:**
Never claim your product "treats," "cures," "prevents," or "diagnoses" any disease. This violates FDA regulations and can result in enforcement action.

**Missing required disclaimers:**
The FDA disclaimer must appear on all supplement products. Missing it creates legal exposure.

**Inadequate testing documentation:**
Keep thorough records of all testing. You may need to produce these for customers, retailers, or regulators.

### Operational Mistakes

**Ignoring shelf life:**
Supplements have expiration dates. Build inventory management around FIFO (first in, first out) and avoid overstocking.

**Underestimating subscription complexity:**
Subscription management requires dedicated attention. Failed payments, modifications, and churn need systematic handling.

**Scaling too fast without infrastructure:**
Rapid growth with inadequate fulfillment, support, or inventory management damages brand reputation.

### Marketing Mistakes

**Overpromising results:**
Health consumers are increasingly skeptical. Overpromising leads to returns, chargebacks, and negative reviews.

**Ignoring customer education:**
Health products require explanation. Invest in content that helps customers understand and properly use your products.

**Neglecting retention:**
Acquisition costs in health are high. Focus equally on retention, subscriptions, and customer lifetime value.
