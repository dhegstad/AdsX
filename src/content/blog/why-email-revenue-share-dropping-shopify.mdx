Email revenue declining as a share of total revenue is one of the most overlooked DTC issues. Total revenue might still grow because paid ads compensate, but the email channel — usually the highest-ROI marketing investment — is degrading. The decline accelerates if not addressed.

This guide covers the diagnostic for email revenue share decline.

## First, calibrate

Healthy email revenue share for DTC:

- 20-35% of total revenue: healthy
- 35-45%: well-developed program
- Above 45%: possibly over-dependent
- 10-20%: under-developed but functional
- Below 10%: serious issue

Compare your current share to your historical average. The trend matters more than absolute number.

## Diagnostic 1: Deliverability

Most common cause of email decline.

Signs:

- Open rates declining month over month
- Click-through rates also declining
- Recent ISP-specific drops (Gmail open rate suddenly tanks, Yahoo holds)
- Spam complaint rate above 0.1%

Test:

- Send through GlockApps or Mail Tester
- Check inbox placement
- Review Klaviyo's deliverability metrics
- Check sender reputation in Google Postmaster Tools

Fix:

- Suppress unengaged subscribers (90+ days no opens, 180 days no clicks)
- Re-engage before suppression with sunset flow
- Authenticate domain (DKIM, SPF, DMARC)
- Improve sender reputation gradually with cleaner list

## Diagnostic 2: List decay

Lists naturally decay 20-30% per year. If you're not adding subscribers faster than you're losing engagement:

Signs:

- Active list size shrinking
- Engagement rates declining despite stable send volume
- New subscriber acquisition slowing

Fix:

- Audit signup capture mechanisms (popups, exit intent, account creation)
- Test signup offer (lead magnet, discount, content)
- Multi-channel capture (SMS in addition to email)

## Diagnostic 3: Flow performance decline

Automated flows (welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase) degrade over time without maintenance:

Signs:

- Specific flow conversion rates dropping
- Recent flow edits that may have hurt performance
- Outdated content in flows

Fix:

- Audit each flow's conversion rate vs. 6 months ago
- Refresh creative and copy in flows
- A/B test flow improvements
- Add new flows where missing (browse abandonment, win-back, etc.)

## Diagnostic 4: Send frequency issues

Too few sends = leaving revenue on the table. Too many = list fatigue and deliverability damage.

Signs:

- Recently increased frequency, engagement dropping
- Recently decreased frequency, revenue dropping
- Frequency stable, engagement still dropping (deliverability)

Fix:

- Aim for 2-4 broadcast sends per week + flows
- Segment to send relevant content rather than blasting list
- Use engagement-based segmentation (active vs. lapsed)

## Diagnostic 5: Content quality

Email content that doesn't deliver value gets ignored:

Signs:

- Lower clicks on every send regardless of subject line
- Higher unsubscribe rates
- Subscribers responding to flows but not broadcasts

Fix:

- Audit recent broadcast content
- Mix promotional with educational/branded
- Improve creative quality
- Match content to audience interests via segmentation

## Diagnostic 6: Subject line quality

Subject lines drive opens. Low opens cascade to low everything.

Signs:

- Open rate dropping significantly across all sends
- Higher than usual variance in open rates between sends

Fix:

- A/B test subject lines weekly
- Avoid spam-trigger words
- Keep subject lines under 50 characters
- Personalize where appropriate (first name, product name)

## Diagnostic 7: SMS overlap

If you've added SMS recently, it may be cannibalizing email:

Signs:

- Email engagement dropping coincident with SMS launch
- Customers opting out of email but staying on SMS

Fix:

- Coordinate cadence between channels (don't send both same day for same campaign)
- Different content tracks for each channel
- Let subscribers choose channel preferences

## Diagnostic 8: Welcome series performance

Welcome series often drives 20-30% of email revenue. If it's underperforming:

Signs:

- Welcome flow conversion rate below 10%
- High unsubscribe rate within first 7 days
- Outdated welcome content

Fix:

- Refresh welcome flow content
- Test welcome offer
- Test number of welcome emails (3-5 typically optimal)

## Diagnostic 9: Abandoned cart performance

Another major email revenue source. If declining:

Signs:

- Cart abandonment recovery rate dropping
- Specific cart flows underperforming
- Cart link click rates dropping

Fix:

- Refresh abandoned cart creative
- Test offer escalation in second/third email
- Verify cart links work correctly

## Diagnostic 10: Email tool issues

Less common but real:

- Recent migration issues
- Tracking setup problems
- API or integration breaking

Audit your email tool's reporting integrity. Check for tracking gaps.

## A diagnostic walkthrough

If your email revenue share is dropping:

1. Calculate current share vs. 6 months ago
2. Check deliverability (open rate trend, inbox placement)
3. Check list size trend
4. Audit flow performance individually
5. Audit broadcast performance
6. Check for tooling or integration issues

Most decline is one of: deliverability, flow degradation, or list decay.

## What to do this week

If your email revenue share is dropping:

- Run a deliverability test
- Pull flow performance metrics
- List cleanup if not done in 90+ days

If healthy, audit quarterly to maintain.
