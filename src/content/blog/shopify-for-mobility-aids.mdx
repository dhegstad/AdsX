## Managing Shipping Heavy and Large Items

This is where many new mobility aids retailers struggle. Shipping a 50-lb wheelchair cross-country costs $50-$150 per unit, dramatically impacting your unit economics.

### Shipping Cost Strategy

**Transparent Pricing Approach:**

Rather than hiding shipping costs, many successful mobility aids retailers:
1. Include shipping in the product price for popular local regions
2. Show real-time calculated shipping at checkout
3. Offer free shipping thresholds for multiple-item orders
4. Partner with specific carriers known for medical equipment handling

**Carrier Selection:**

- **FedEx Ground**: Best for most mobility aids (handles up to 150 lbs, good coverage)
- **UPS Ground**: Reliable alternative with similar capacity
- **Freight Carriers**: XPO, Old Dominion for bulk institutional orders
- **White Glove Delivery**: Premium option for high-value items with assembly/setup

**Shopify Shipping Integration:**

Configure Shopify's built-in shipping:

1. Go to Settings → Shipping and Delivery
2. Add shipping zones (US regions, international if applicable)
3. Set up carrier accounts with FedEx/UPS directly
4. Enable real-time carrier rates at checkout
5. Consider dimensional weight pricing for bulky items

### Dimensional Weight Pricing

Mobility aids are often bulky but not extremely heavy. Carriers charge based on whichever is greater: actual weight or dimensional weight.

**Dimensional Weight Formula:** (Length × Width × Height) ÷ 166 = Dimensional Weight

**Example:** A folding walker measures 32" × 20" × 8"
- Dimensional weight: (32 × 20 × 8) ÷ 166 = 30.9 lbs
- Actual weight: 7 lbs
- Carrier charges for 30.9 lbs (the higher number)

Account for this in your pricing strategy. Some retailers use packaging optimization to reduce dimensions:
- Use narrower shipping boxes when possible
- Fold/disassemble products when safe
- Minimize excess packing material

### Logistics Apps and Optimization

- **Shippo**: Compare rates across carriers in real-time

- **Printful/Printful**: Inventory management for heavy products

Many successful mobility aids retailers report that optimizing shipping logistics is their single highest impact business improvement after launching.

## Medicare, Insurance, and Compliance Considerations

This is a crucial section because mobility aids touch regulated healthcare space. Your approach matters.

### Direct-to-Consumer vs. Insurance-Billable

**Key Distinction:**

Your Shopify store will primarily handle direct consumer purchases (customers pay out-of-pocket or use personal insurance reimbursement). Medicare and insurance companies typically have their own supplier networks and don't reimburse consumers for purchases through general e-commerce sites.

**However**, you should understand this landscape:

### Medicare Durable Medical Equipment (DME) Suppliers

Medicare covers certain mobility aids when prescribed by a physician and provided by approved DME suppliers. These products include:
- Wheelchairs and scooters
- Walkers and canes
- Grab bars and bathroom safety equipment
- Raised toilet seats
- Crutches and similar items

**Important for Your Business:**

1. **You Don't Need to Be a Medicare Supplier to Sell**: Your Shopify store can sell to anyone, including patients who will seek Medicare reimbursement separately
2. **Compliance Matters**: Ensure products meet FDA requirements and safety standards
3. **Honest Descriptions**: Clearly state if items are prescription-only or suitable without prescription
4. **Privacy**: If handling any healthcare information, ensure HIPAA compliance considerations

### FDA Classifications

Most mobility aids fall into FDA categories:

- **Class I**: Low-risk items (canes, crutches, walkers, grab bars) - minimal regulation
- **Class II**: Moderate-risk items (wheelchairs, rollators) - require compliance and documentation
- **Class III**: High-risk items (certain specialized equipment) - require pre-market approval

For Class I and II products (your likely range), ensure:
- Manufacturer provides certification/compliance documentation
- You source from legitimate suppliers
- Safety standards are met (ISO 11228-1 for manual handling, etc.)

### Insurance and Prescription Requirements

Some considerations for your store:

1. **Optional Prescription Collection**: Offer customers a form to upload prescriptions during checkout (helps with their insurance claims)
2. **Insurance Guidance**: Provide a helpful page explaining how customers can seek reimbursement
3. **Documentation**: Provide itemized invoices suitable for insurance claims
4. **Price Transparency**: Display manufacturer's suggested retail prices so customers can understand insurance coverage

### Transparency and Ethics

The mobility aids market attracts unethical sellers. Differentiate yourself through integrity:

- Honest product descriptions without exaggerated health claims
- Clear return policies (customers need to try items for comfort)
- Legitimate source verification for branded products
- Expert customer service for technical questions
- Safe shipping practices for medical equipment

## Website Accessibility: Essential for Your Audience

Here's a critical truth: You're selling to customers with disabilities. Your website must be accessible. This is both ethically essential and strategically important.

### WCAG 2.1 Compliance

Aim for WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as a minimum standard:

**Vision Accessibility:**
- Sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
- No information conveyed by color alone
- Resizable text without loss of functionality
- Text alternatives for images (alt text)
- Readable fonts and sufficient line spacing

**Motor Accessibility:**
- Keyboard navigation for all features
- Sufficient target size for buttons (minimum 44×44 pixels)
- No time-dependent interactions
- Clear focus indicators
- No rapid flashing that could trigger seizures

**Cognitive Accessibility:**
- Clear, simple language
- Consistent navigation
- Predictable interactions
- Error messages and correction suggestions
- Logical heading hierarchy

**Hearing/Audio:**
- Captions for any videos
- Transcripts for audio content
- Don't rely on sound for information

### Practical Implementation on Shopify

**Built-in Shopify Features:**
1. Use semantic HTML (proper heading structure H1, H2, H3)
2. Add descriptive alt text to all product images
3. Test with keyboard navigation only
4. Ensure theme supports accessibility settings
5. Use sufficient color contrast in your design

**Accessibility Apps for Shopify:**
- **AccessiBe**: Automated accessibility overlay and manual remediation
- **Userway**: Accessibility scanning and improvement tools
- **Equally**: AI-powered accessibility features
- **Accessible Web**: Comprehensive WCAG compliance tools

**Specific Adjustments for Mobility Aids Stores:**

1. **Filter/Search Accessibility**: Ensure product filters work with keyboard and screen readers
2. **Detailed Alt Text**: "Silver aluminum rollator with seat and brakes" not just "rollator"
3. **Accessible Video**: All assembly videos should have captions
4. **Readable Product Tables**: Use proper table markup for specification comparisons
5. **Clear Pricing**: Don't hide prices behind interactions

### Why Accessibility Matters Strategically

Beyond ethics:
- **Larger Audience**: Approximately 26% of US adults have some type of disability (CDC)
- **Better SEO**: Accessible sites typically rank better in search results
- **Legal Protection**: Accessibility violations can result in ADA lawsuits
- **Customer Trust**: Accessible design signals quality and care to all users
- **Improved UX**: Accessibility improvements benefit everyone (larger text, keyboard nav, captions, etc.)
