Private label e-commerce has created some of the most profitable Shopify stores. But it's also one of the most misunderstood business models.

Most entrepreneurs conflate private label with dropshipping, thinking they're the same thing. They're not. And that confusion costs money.

This guide breaks down what private label actually is, how it compares to alternatives, and exactly how to launch and scale a private label brand on Shopify—from finding the right manufacturer to building AI visibility for your products.

## Private Label vs White Label vs Dropshipping: The Critical Differences

Before launching anything, you need to understand exactly what you're building.

### Private Label: Own Your Product and Brand

**What it is:** You partner with a manufacturer to create a product to your specifications, then sell it exclusively under your own brand.

**How it works:**
1. You research a product idea or identify market demand
2. Find a manufacturer willing to customize/create the product
3. Place a minimum order quantity (MOQ) - you pay upfront
4. Manufacturer produces the goods
5. You receive inventory, store it, and sell through your store
6. You own the entire brand relationship with customers

**Financial model:**
- You pay manufacturer: $2-$15 per unit (varies by product)
- You sell retail: $20-$200+ per unit
- Typical margins: 60-80% gross profit after COGS

**Advantages:**
- Complete brand control
- Highest profit margins of all models
- Unique products competitors can't easily copy
- Build lasting customer relationships
- Easier to get AI shopping recommendations (unique products)
- Can command premium pricing

**Disadvantages:**
- Requires significant upfront capital
- Inventory risk if products don't sell
- Longer time to market
- Need to manage manufacturing logistics
- Minimum order quantities can be large
- More complex operations

### White Label: Branded Reselling

**What it is:** A manufacturer makes a product that already exists, and you put your branding on it. The product itself isn't customized.

**How it works:**
1. Find a white label manufacturer with existing products
2. Add your branding (logo, packaging design)
3. Place order for your branded version
4. Sell under your brand

**Financial model:**
- Similar margins to private label
- Lower customization costs
- Faster to market

**Why choose white label:**
- You want to brand-resell without custom development
- Faster launch than full private label
- Lower design and development costs
- MOQs are often lower

**The problem with white label:**
Many other sellers are also white labeling the same product. The physical product is identical to competitors, only packaging differs. This makes it harder to differentiate for AI visibility.

### Dropshipping: Zero Inventory

**What it is:** You sell products directly from a supplier without holding inventory. The supplier ships orders directly to customers.

**How it works:**
1. Find dropshipping supplier with product catalog
2. Add their products to your store (often with high markups)
3. Customer orders from you
4. You forward order to supplier with customer's address
5. Supplier ships directly to customer

**Financial model:**
- Low margins: 20-40% typical
- No upfront inventory costs
- Immediate access to thousands of products

**Why dropshippers exist:**
- Test products with zero risk
- Scale with no inventory investment
- Access to global suppliers instantly

**Why dropshipping is increasingly difficult:**
- AI visibility is nearly impossible (identical products everywhere)
- Customer service problems (supplier delays, quality)
- Customers can buy directly from supplier
- Race to lowest price erodes margins

### Quick Comparison Table

| Aspect | Private Label | White Label | Dropshipping |
|--------|---------------|------------|--------------|
| **Upfront Cost** | $5,000-$50,000+ | $3,000-$15,000 | $500-$2,000 |
| **Customization** | Full (product shape, materials) | Minimal (packaging only) | None |
| **Margins** | 60-80% | 50-70% | 20-40% |
| **Time to Market** | 4-6 months | 2-4 months | 1-2 weeks |
| **Inventory Risk** | High | Medium | None |
| **AI Visibility Potential** | Excellent (unique products) | Good (if branded well) | Poor (identical products) |
| **Scalability** | Limited by MOQ/manufacturing | Limited by MOQ | Unlimited |
| **Brand Control** | Complete | Strong | Weak |

**The key insight:** Private label requires more investment but creates a defensible, scalable business. Dropshipping requires less investment but creates an increasingly difficult business. Private label is the long-term play.

## Finding the Right Private Label Supplier

Your supplier makes or breaks your business. Choosing wrong creates delays, quality issues, and wasted capital.

### Where to Find Manufacturers

**1. Alibaba (alibaba.com)**

The world's largest B2B marketplace connecting buyers with manufacturers.

**How to find suppliers:**
1. Search for your product (e.g., "wireless earbuds manufacturer")
2. Filter by:
   - Minimum order quantity (match your budget)
   - Years in business (prefer 5+ years)
   - Verification badge (shows legitimacy)
3. Check ratings and reviews carefully
4. Contact 5-10 suppliers

**Red flags on Alibaba:**
- New sellers with no verification
- Extremely low prices (often indicates quality issues)
- Unwillingness to provide samples
- Poor English communication
- No clear product specifications

**Alibaba tips:**
- Always request a sample before committing
- Use Alibaba's escrow payment system for protection
- Expect 2-4 week response times (time zone differences)
- Negotiate on MOQ and pricing

**2. Global Sources (globalsources.com)**

B2B platform with stricter vetting than Alibaba. Suppliers pay to list, so it filters out casual operators.

**Advantages:**
- Higher quality suppliers on average
- Better verification
- More professional communication

**Disadvantages:**
- Fewer suppliers in some niches
- Higher supplier costs (passed to buyers sometimes)

**3. Manufacturing Directories**

- **ThomasNet:** US/North America manufacturers
- **Made-in-China:** China-specific suppliers
- **Kompass:** European manufacturers
- **TradeKey:** Global supplier network

**4. Industry-Specific Trade Shows**

Most industries have annual trade shows. Attending (or virtually attending) connects you with manufacturers directly.

**5. Local Manufacturing**

For some products, domestic manufacturing is feasible and has advantages:
- Faster shipping and lead times
- Higher quality control
- Easier communication
- Can support "Made in USA/UK" positioning

**Finding local manufacturers:**
- Industry associations
- Chamber of commerce
- Local business networks
- Business development organizations

### How to Evaluate Manufacturers

**Initial Evaluation: Contact and Communication**

1. **Responsiveness** — Do they reply within 24 hours?
2. **English clarity** — Can you understand technical specifications?
3. **Flexibility** — Are they open to customization?
4. **MOQ realism** — Can you meet their minimum?

**Qualification Process**

1. **Request a sample** — Always see the product before committing. Budget $50-$500 for samples.

2. **Evaluate the sample:**
   - Quality meets your standards?
   - Branding works correctly?
   - Packaging feels professional?
   - All specs match?

3. **Get detailed pricing breakdown:**
   ```
   Quantity | Unit Cost | Tooling Fee | Packaging | Shipping
   100      | $12.50    | $500        | $0.50     | $350
   250      | $11.00    | $500        | $0.50     | $750
   500      | $9.75     | $500        | $0.50     | $1,200
   1000     | $8.50     | $500        | $0.50     | $2,000
   ```

4. **Understand lead times:**
   - How long from order to production?
   - How long for customization/tooling?
   - Shipping time to you?
   - Total timeline realistic for your market?

5. **Confirm MOQ and customization options:**
   - What's their absolute minimum?
   - Can you do smaller initial orders?
   - What customizations are possible?
   - Are there fees for custom colors, packaging?

6. **Verify quality standards:**
   - What QC processes do they use?
   - What's their defect rate?
   - What's the return/replacement policy?
   - Can you request third-party inspection?

### Red Flags That Mean: Don't Work With Them

- Won't provide references from other buyers
- Only accepts wire transfer (escrow is standard)
- Significantly undercuts all other quotes (quality risk)
- Can't explain their manufacturing process clearly
- Unwilling to provide detailed specifications
- Samples arrive damaged or don't match specs
- Communication becomes evasive when issues arise

### Negotiating Your First Order

**Your leverage points:**

1. **Promise of repeat orders** — "If this goes well, we'll order monthly"
2. **Growth timeline** — "We're starting with 500, planning 5,000+ next year"
3. **Multiple products** — "We want to develop 3-5 products over time"
4. **Network referrals** — "We're part of [network/mastermind] with other brands"

**What you can negotiate:**

1. **MOQ reduction:**
   - "Can you do 200 instead of 500 for our first order?"
   - Expect 20-30% premium for lower MOQ

2. **Pricing adjustments:**
   - "What's your best price for 300 units?"
   - Competitive quotes help negotiate down

3. **Payment terms:**
   - Standard: 50% deposit, 50% on completion
   - Aim for: 30% deposit, 70% on completion
   - Only after building trust

4. **Lead time:**
   - "Can you expedite to 8 weeks instead of 12?"
   - Usually costs 15-25% extra

5. **Customization scope:**
   - Logo placement
   - Color options
   - Packaging design
   - Instructions/manuals

**Never negotiate:**
- Quality standards
- Third-party inspection
- Sample approval
- Testing requirements

## Branding and Packaging Customization

Your packaging is often the customer's first physical interaction with your brand. It's not an afterthought—it's your product's first impression.

### Private Label Packaging Strategy

**Level 1: Basic Customization (Budget: $500-$1,500)**

- Your logo on standard box
- Basic product label with your branding
- Information card or insert with brand story
- Simple color scheme matching your brand

**Appropriate for:**
- Testing market fit initially
- Budget-conscious launches
- Generic product categories

**Level 2: Branded Customization (Budget: $1,500-$5,000)**

- Custom box design with color printing
- Professional product labels
- Branded tissue paper or insert
- Custom thank you card
- QR code linking to tracking or content

**Appropriate for:**
- Premium positioning
- Differentiation from competitors
- Building brand recognition
- Most successful private label brands use this level

**Level 3: Premium Unboxing (Budget: $5,000-$15,000+)**

- Fully custom box structure
- Multi-color printing on all sides
- Branded filler material (custom crinkle paper, cotton fill)
- Thoughtfully designed inserts
- Free gift or sample
- Personalized thank you letter
- Professional photography for unboxing

**Appropriate for:**
- Ultra-premium positioning
- Established brands with scale
- Building viral unboxing content
- High-price-point products

### The Design Process

**Step 1: Hire a designer**

Don't do this yourself unless you have design experience. Budget: $500-$2,000 for initial design.

**Options:**
- Fiverr/Upwork (cheaper, variable quality)
- Local design agencies (more expensive, better results)
- Specialized e-commerce design firms (recommended)

**Step 2: Create a brand guidelines document**

Your designer needs:
- Brand name and tagline
- Color palette (specific HEX codes)
- Logo files (multiple formats)
- Typography preferences
- Brand voice/personality description
- Target customer description

**Step 3: Design multiple iterations**

Request 2-3 design concepts before selecting final direction.

**What to evaluate:**
- Does it stand out? (Will it catch attention in a delivery truck?)
- Is branding clear? (Logo, name visible immediately?)
- Does color psychology work? (Colors match brand positioning?)
- Does it feel premium? (Or does it look cheap?)
- Is information hierarchy correct? (Most important details prominent?)
- Does it photograph well? (Will it look good on Instagram?)

**Step 4: Test with actual samples**

Before mass producing, order small sample quantities (usually 25-50 units) to evaluate:
- Color accuracy on actual materials
- Print quality and sharpness
- Durability (will printing fade?)
- Feel and texture
- Assembly/packing clarity

**Step 5: Finalize production files**

Provide manufacturer with:
- High-resolution design files (300 DPI minimum)
- Color specifications (Pantone codes, CMYK values)
- File format (AI, PDF, EPS)
- Print-ready guidelines
- All variations (if multiple box sizes, colors, etc.)

### Packaging Elements That Matter

**1. Exterior Box Design**

- **Logo placement:** Top left or center-top. Visible at first glance.
- **Product imagery:** High-quality photo showing product in best light
- **Key benefit headline:** One compelling reason to buy (visible from 3 feet away)
- **Social proof:** "10,000+ Happy Customers" or similar
- **QR code:** Links to unboxing video, brand story, or product tips

**2. Product Labels**

- Barcode and product details
- Your brand name prominent
- Key specifications
- Safety/warning information if needed
- Batch/lot number for traceability

**3. Interior Experience**

- Branded tissue paper (top-of-box reveal)
- Filler material that's branded and eco-friendly
- Product positioned for best visibility
- Protective materials that look intentional, not cheap
- Thank you card or personalized insert
- Care instructions or product tips

**4. Information Card/Booklet**

Include:
- Brand story (why you created this product)
- How to use/maintain
- Warranty information
- Link to customer community (if exists)
- Next product teaser
- Satisfaction guarantee statement

**5. Environmental Considerations**

Modern consumers care about sustainability:
- Use recyclable/biodegradable materials
- Avoid excessive plastic
- Source eco-friendly tissue paper and fills
- Consider plastic-free tape
- Print sustainability message on box

## Understanding and Managing Minimum Order Quantities

MOQ is where many private label dreams die. Understanding and negotiating MOQ can mean the difference between launching and staying stuck.

### What MOQ Actually Means

Minimum Order Quantity is the smallest batch a manufacturer will produce for you.

**Example:** MOQ of 500 units means:
- You must order at least 500 units
- You pay for 500 units upfront (typically)
- You receive 500 units
- If you only want 100, most manufacturers won't do it

### Why Manufacturers Enforce MOQ

1. **Setup costs:** Customizing production lines, creating tooling, preparing materials
2. **Efficiency:** Small batches are inefficient, increase per-unit costs significantly
3. **Risk management:** They're not making that much profit on setup, need volume to justify it
4. **Supplier economics:** Their raw material suppliers also have minimums

### Typical MOQs by Category

| Product Category | Typical MOQ | Reasoning |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------|
| Simple items (stickers, koozies) | 100-500 | Low setup, quick production |
| Basic goods (phone cases, t-shirts) | 300-1,000 | Standard setup time |
| Electronics (earbuds, chargers) | 500-2,000 | Complex assembly |
| Appliances | 1,000-5,000 | High custom tooling |
| Furniture | 500-2,000 | Significant assembly |
| Consumables (supplements, tea) | 300-1,000 | Batch-based production |

### Can You Negotiate MOQ Lower?

**Yes, with leverage:**

**Strategy 1: Offer growth timeline**
> "We're starting with 500 units to test. If successful, we'll reorder monthly. Year 1 projection: 5,000+ units."

**Strategy 2: Find aggregation partners**
Some companies aggregate orders from multiple brands to hit MOQ together. You reduce your MOQ, share risk.

**Strategy 3: Accept higher unit costs**
> "We'll accept $12/unit instead of $9/unit if you can do 200 units instead of 500."

The math: 200 units × $12 = $2,400 vs. 500 units × $9 = $4,500. You pay less total but more per unit.

**Strategy 4: Offer advance payment**
> "If we pre-pay 50% today, can you do a 300-unit MOQ?"

Manufacturers love upfront cash. It covers their material costs immediately.

**Strategy 5: Look for overstock or closeout inventory**
Some manufacturers have excess inventory from cancelled orders. You can sometimes buy at steep discounts with no MOQ.

### Managing Inventory Risk

**The real challenge:** Investing $5,000-$20,000 in inventory that might not sell.

**Inventory risk mitigation:**

**1. Pre-launch validation (before you order)**

- Create a landing page describing the product
- Run targeted ads ($500-$2,000 budget) to validate demand
- Ask for email signups/pre-orders
- Target: 100+ emails or 10+ pre-orders before ordering
- This de-risks the MOQ investment

**2. Conservative initial orders**

- Don't order 2,000 units on your first private label product
- Start with MOQ or slightly above (300-500 units typical)
- If it sells well, reorder larger quantities (economies of scale kick in)

**3. Inventory projections**

Create realistic sales forecasts:

```
Conservative estimate:
- Month 1: 10 sales (testing, low traffic)
- Month 2: 20 sales (growing brand awareness)
- Month 3: 40 sales (word of mouth, SEO)
- Month 4+: 60+ sales/month (established)

Total for 500-unit order:
Month 1: 10 units (490 remaining)
Month 2: 20 units (470 remaining)
Month 3: 40 units (430 remaining)
Month 4: 60 units (370 remaining)
Month 5: 75 units (295 remaining) - time to reorder if trending up
```

**4. Multiple SKU strategy**

Rather than ordering 500 units of one product:
- Order 250 of product A
- Order 150 of product B
- Order 100 of product C

This spreads risk across multiple products.

**5. Pricing to manage inventory**

You don't have to be "cheap" to move inventory:

- **Over-inventory:** Increase price 10-15%. Sell fewer units, profit more per sale
- **Under-inventory:** Lower price 10-15%. Create urgency, move more volume
- **Seasonal:** Adjust pricing based on demand cycles

The goal isn't volume—it's total profit from that inventory.

## Building Your Private Label Brand on Shopify

You have the product. Now you need to position it as a brand customers trust.

### Brand Positioning: The Foundation

Before building your store, define your brand in writing.

**1. Brand Statement (1-2 sentences)**

> "Ergo Tools creates sustainable, precision hand tools for professionals and serious hobbyists who refuse to compromise on quality."

versus

> "Best prices on tools online."

The first is a brand. The second is a commodity.

**2. Target Customer Profile**

Be specific:

> "Sarah, 34, runs a restoration business, works with wood and metal, values quality tools that last decades, willing to pay premium for durability, active on Instagram, reads woodworking blogs, follows product-focused influencers."

versus

> "People who buy tools."

**3. Brand Personality**

How does your brand "talk"?
- Professional and authoritative?
- Friendly and approachable?
- Inspiring and aspirational?
- Educational and thorough?
- Humorous and bold?

This determines everything from your copy to your visual style.

**4. Key Brand Attributes** (3-5)

Examples:
- Sustainable/Eco-friendly
- Precision-engineered
- Lifetime warranty
- Made for professionals
- Designed for beginners

**5. Your Origin Story**

Why did you create this? Real stories sell:

> "After 20 years as a restorer, I couldn't find paint brushes that lasted through a single project. Every brush seemed designed for disposability. So I partnered with a Japanese manufacturer to create brushes that actually last. Every Precision Brush comes with our lifetime guarantee because we believe tools shouldn't be disposable."

### Shopify Store Setup for Private Label

**1. Choose your store name**

Not your product name—your brand name.

**Best store names:**
- Memorable and easy to spell
- Suggests your category or benefit
- Could work for multiple products
- .com domain available (preferred)

**Examples:**
- ErgoPro.com (ergonomic products)
- PatrinaCrafts.com (handmade/vintage style)
- Precision.Store (high-quality goods)

**Avoid:**
- Overly cute names that don't age well
- Names hard to spell or remember
- Category keywords (too limiting)
- Numbers or special characters

**2. Install critical Shopify apps**

Essential for private label success:

| App | Purpose | Cost |
|-----|---------|------|
| Growave/Loox | Product reviews (social proof) | $20-99/month |
| Judge.me | Alternative review platform | Free-$99/month |
| Omnisend | Email marketing | Free-$99/month |
| Postscript | SMS marketing | Free-$99/month |
| Search/SEO apps | Product discoverability | Free-$50/month |

**3. Set up collections strategically**

Don't just list all products. Create thematic collections:

**Bad structure:**
- All Products
- New Arrivals

**Better structure:**
- New Products
- By Use Case (e.g., "For Home Office," "For Professionals")
- By Price Point
- Best Sellers
- Gift Guide
- Customer Favorites

Collections help AI understand your product range and positioning.

### Copywriting for Private Label Products

Your product descriptions are your sales force.

**The formula:**

```
Paragraph 1: Problem + Solution + Your Angle
[Specific problem your customer faces + how this product solves it + what makes your version special]

Paragraph 2: Why it's different
[What makes this product superior to alternatives]

Paragraph 3: Benefits (not features)
[How this improves the customer's life]

Paragraph 4: Specific use cases
[Situations where this product excels]

Paragraph 5: Technical specs
[Complete specifications and details]

Paragraph 6: What's included
[Packaging contents, warranty, guarantees]
```

**Example:**

**Bad (generic, feature-focused):**
> "Aluminum phone stand. Adjustable height. Fits all phones. Silver color."

**Better (benefit-focused, specific):**

> "Stop straining your neck during video calls. The ErgoPro Stand positions your phone or tablet at eye level, eliminating the head-down posture that leads to chronic neck pain. We engineered this for remote workers and content creators who spend hours on video.

> Unlike flimsy plastic stands that wobble and tip, our aircraft-grade aluminum construction provides rock-solid stability. The weighted base and silicone pads keep your device secure without scratching screens or surfaces.

> Benefits: Reduces neck strain, improves posture during calls, frees up desk space, keeps screens at optimal viewing angle, looks professional on video backgrounds.

> Use cases: Zoom calls and video conferences, livestreaming and content creation, FaceTime with family, watching recipes while cooking, bedside video watching, gaming and streaming.

> Specifications: 4.5" x 3.2" footprint, supports up to 7" screens, adjustable 15-75 degrees, aluminum construction, weight 8.2 oz, color options: brushed silver or space gray.

> Included: ErgoPro Stand, silicone grip pads (replacement sets available), 30-day satisfaction guarantee, lifetime structural warranty."

The second version takes up more space but converts significantly better because it addresses the customer's actual needs.

### Setting Competitive Pricing

Don't just add margin and call it a day.

**Research phase:**
1. Find competitor products (similar quality/positioning)
2. Document their retail prices
3. Check their review counts (proxy for sales volume)
4. Identify your positioning relative to them

**Pricing strategies:**

**Option 1: Premium positioning**
- Price 20-30% above lowest competitor
- Justify with quality, service, warranty
- Target professional/serious customer
- Lower volume, higher profit per sale

**Option 2: Competitive positioning**
- Price 5-10% above lowest competitor
- Match quality, differentiate on service
- Mid-volume, healthy margins
- Good for established categories

**Option 3: Value positioning**
- Price at or 5% below lowest competitor
- Emphasize efficiency, ease, accessibility
- Higher volume required
- Only if you have cost advantages

**Example pricing matrix:**

| Customer Type | Price Point | Positioning |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Premium buyer | $49.99 | "Best in category, lifetime warranty" |
| Value buyer | $39.99 | "Best value, great reviews" |
| Professional | $59.99 | "Used by industry pros" |

## Building AI Visibility for Your Private Label Store

This is where private label has a major advantage over dropshipping.

### Why Private Label Gets AI Visibility

AI shopping assistants recommend products based on:
1. **Uniqueness** — Is this product truly different?
2. **Authority** — Is the seller trustworthy and knowledgeable?
3. **Content quality** — Can AI understand the product and positioning?
4. **Social proof** — Do customers validate this?

Private label wins on uniqueness and authority because:
- Your product is unique (competitors can't buy the same product)
- You control the brand narrative
- You're the authority (you designed it)
- You can invest in social proof

**Compare to dropshipping:**
- Product is identical to competitors
- No brand narrative to tell
- No authority (just a reseller)
- Low motivation to gather reviews

### Private Label AI Visibility Strategy

**1. Emphasize what makes it unique**

In all marketing, product descriptions, and content:
- "Engineered by [Your brand]"
- "Designed specifically for [use case]"
- "The only [product] with [unique feature]"

**2. Build content authority**

Create detailed content explaining:
- How and why you created this product
- Your design process and decisions
- Why this approach is better
- Customer stories of transformation

**3. Generate social proof intentionally**

## Common Mistakes Private Label Entrepreneurs Make

Learn from others' failures.

### Mistake 1: Launching Without Market Validation

**Wrong:** Invest $10,000 in manufacturing based on a hunch

**Right:**
1. Create landing page describing product
2. Run $500-$1,000 in targeted ads
3. Get 50+ email signups or 5+ pre-orders
4. Only then commit to manufacturing

**Cost of validation:** $1,000
**Cost of wrong product:** $10,000

### Mistake 2: Choosing Wrong Manufacturer

**Wrong:** Pick cheapest supplier to minimize costs

**Right:**
1. Get samples from multiple suppliers
2. Evaluate quality and communication
3. Check references and other customers
4. Choose for reliability, not just price

A $0.50 savings per unit on a $10 product is 5% cost reduction. But a bad manufacturer will cost you 10x that in delays, quality issues, and lost customers.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Packaging

**Wrong:** Plain packaging with your logo thrown on

**Right:**
1. Invest $1,500-$3,000 in packaging design
2. Create unboxing experience
3. Use packaging to tell brand story

Packaging is your brand's first impression. It's worth the investment.

### Mistake 4: Generic Product Selection

**Wrong:** "I'll sell phone stands because they're cheap to source"

**Right:**
1. Identify your expertise or passion
2. Serve a specific customer type
3. Solve a specific problem
4. Build a cohesive brand

The best private label brands have a clear perspective. "Tools for remote workers" beats "generic gadgets."

### Mistake 5: Under-investing in Marketing

**Wrong:** "I'll launch the store and customers will find me"

**Right:**
1. Budget 20-30% of revenue for marketing
2. Mix paid ads, content, social, partnerships
3. Build email list from day one
4. Test and optimize continuously

You can have the best product, but if nobody knows about it, it won't sell.

### Mistake 6: Not Building Reviews Early

**Wrong:** Launch store, wait for organic reviews to appear

**Right:**
1. Email every customer requesting reviews
2. Offer small incentive (future discount, not payment for positive review)
3. Follow up with non-reviewers
4. Target 20+ reviews in first 3 months

Reviews are credibility. Build them intentionally.
